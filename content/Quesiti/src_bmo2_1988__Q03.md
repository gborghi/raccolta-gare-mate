*Find value of a^4+b^4+c^4-2abc given symmetric equations in x,y,z*

> The numbers $a$, $b$, $c$, $x$, $y$, $z$ satisfy the equations
> $$x^4 - y^4 - z^4 = 2ayz$$
> $$-x^4 + y^4 - z^4 = 2bxz$$
> $$-x^4 - y^4 + z^4 = 2cxy$$
> and also $xyz \neq 0$.
> 
> By using the first two equations express $z$ in terms of $a$, $b$, $x$, $y$. Prove that
> $$x^2(1 - b^2) = y^2(1 - a^2) = xy(ab - c)$$
> and hence find the value of $a^4 + b^4 + c^4 - 2abc$ (independently of $x$, $y$, $z$).
