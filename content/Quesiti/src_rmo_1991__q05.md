*Lines through point on side of triangle, parallelism chain*

> Take any point $P_1$ on side $BC$ of a triangle $ABC$ and draw the following chain of lines: $P_1P_2$ parallel to $AC$ ($P_2$ on $AB$); $P_2P_3$ parallel to $BC$ ($P_3$ on $AC$); $P_3P_4$ parallel to $AB$ ($P_4$ on $BC$); $P_4P_5$ parallel to $CA$ ($P_5$ on $AB$); $P_5P_6$ parallel to $BC$ ($P_6$ on $AC$). Here $P_2$, $P_3$ lie on $AB$; $P_3$, $P_5$ lie on $CA$; $P_1$, $P_4$ lie on $BC$. Show that $P_6P_1$ is parallel to $AB$.
