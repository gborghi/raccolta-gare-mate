*Airlines X and Y on n cities, travel m flights avoiding Y-after-X*

> Let $m$ and $n$ be positive integers satisfying $\dfrac{m(m+1)}{2} < n$. In a certain country there are $n$ cities and two airlines $X$ and $Y$. Each airline operates some direct (non-stop) flights between pairs of cities. The following is known:
> \begin{itemize}
> \item For every city $C$, it is impossible to return to $C$ by taking only direct flights of the same airline, starting and ending at $C$ (i.e., no airline has a cycle through $C$ using only its own flights).
> \item For every two distinct cities, one can travel from either city to the other using only direct flights of one airline (not necessarily the same airline for both directions).
> \end{itemize}
> Note: if there is a direct flight from city $C$ to city $D$, there is not necessarily a direct flight from $D$ to $C$.
> 
> Prove that there exists a city from which one can depart and take exactly $m$ direct flights in succession so that the following condition is satisfied:
> 
> \textit{Condition: an $X$-flight is never immediately followed by a $Y$-flight.}
