*How many pieces does a cube split into by 28 perpendicular bisector planes?*

> Let $C$ be a wooden cube. For each of the $28$ pairs of vertices of $C$, we cut the cube $C$ by the perpendicular bisector plane of the two vertices in the pair. Into how many pieces is the cube divided?
> 
> **Note:** Given two points $A$ and $B$ in space, the perpendicular bisector plane of $A$ and $B$ is the set of points in space equidistant from $A$ and $B$. In other words, it is the plane perpendicular to segment $AB$ passing through the midpoint of $AB$.
