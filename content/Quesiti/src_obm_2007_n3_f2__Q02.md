*Geometric probability that a random point on a segment lies within distance 5/3 of the origin*

> A point $B = (x; y)$ is chosen at random on the points of the segment $A$ that lies on the line $x + y = 2$ with $0 \le x \le 2$ (so $A$ runs from $(0,2)$ to $(2,0)$). Let $P$ be the origin. Determine the probability $p$ that the chosen point $B$ is at distance at most $\frac{5}{3}$ from the origin, that is, that $\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \le \frac{5}{3}$. The probability equals the length of the sub-segment $\overline{P_1 P_2}$ of $A$ satisfying the condition, divided by the length of $A$, which is $\sqrt{(0-2)^2 + (2-0)^2} = 2\sqrt{2}$.
