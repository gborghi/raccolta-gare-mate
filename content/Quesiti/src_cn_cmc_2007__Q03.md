*Probability that a - 2b + 10 > 0 for balls drawn with replacement*

> Nine balls of the same size and color, numbered $1, 2, \ldots, 9$, were put into a packet. Now $A$ draws a ball from the packet, noted that it is of number $a$, and puts it back. Then $B$ also draws a ball from the packet and noted that it is of number $b$. Then the probability for the inequality $a - 2b + 10 > 0$ to hold is $($\quad$)$.
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{52}{81}$ $\quad$ (B) $\dfrac{59}{81}$ $\quad$ (C) $\dfrac{60}{81}$ $\quad$ (D) $\dfrac{61}{81}$
