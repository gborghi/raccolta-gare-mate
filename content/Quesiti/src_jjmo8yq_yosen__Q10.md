*Possible multisets of 30 scores when each of 3 problems solved by 10*

> Thirty students took a test consisting of $3$ problems. Solving the problems correctly is worth $1$ point, $2$ points, and $4$ points respectively, and an incorrect answer is worth $0$ points. Given that, in the test results, each of the $3$ problems was solved correctly by exactly $10$ students, how many possibilities are there for the collection of the $30$ students' scores? (Collections that differ only in the order of the scores are regarded as the same.)
