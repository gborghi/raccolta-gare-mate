# Coupe Animath Printemps 2014 — Quesito 6

*Max circles at equal distance from four non-concyclic points*

> Given a point $P$ and a circle $\mathcal{C}$ in the plane, define the distance from $P$ to $\mathcal{C}$ as the minimum length $PM$ where $M$ is a point on circle $\mathcal{C}$. For example, if $P$ belongs to the circle then the distance from $P$ to $\mathcal{C}$ is zero, and if $P$ is the centre of the circle then the distance from $P$ to $\mathcal{C}$ equals the radius of $\mathcal{C}$.
> 
> Given four non-concyclic points $A, B, C, D$, what is the maximum number of circles that are at equal distance from all four points?
