*With F(k) the product of all divisors of k, prove or disprove that two distinct naturals have equal F.*

> For a natural number $k$, denote by $F(k)$ the product of all divisors of $k$. Prove or disprove that there exist two different numbers $m,n\in\mathbb{N}$ with $F(m)=F(n)$.
