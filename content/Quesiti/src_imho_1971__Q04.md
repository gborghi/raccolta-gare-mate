*Acute-angled triangles and minimal geodesic on tetrahedron ABCD*

> All the faces of tetrahedron $ABCD$ are acute-angled triangles. We consider all closed polygonal paths of the form $XYZТX$ defined as follows: $X$ is a point on edge $AB$ distinct from $A$ and $B$; similarly, $Y$, $Z$, $T$ are interior points of edges $BC$, $CD$, $DA$, respectively. Prove:
> 
> (a) If $\angle DAB + \angle BCD \ne \angle ABC + \angle CDA$, then among the polygonal paths, there is none of minimal length.
> 
> (b) If $\angle DAB + \angle BCD = \angle ABC + \angle CDA$, then there are infinitely many shortest polygonal paths, their common length being $2AC \sin(\alpha/2)$, where $\alpha = \angle BAC + \angle CAD + \angle DAB$.
