*Prove every positive integer not in a given infinite set belongs to the sum of two or more members*

> Prove that every positive integer which is not a member of the infinite set
> $$3,\,-2,\,2^2 \cdot 3,\,-2^3,\,\ldots,\,2^{2k} \cdot 3,\,-2^{2k+1},\,\ldots$$
> is equal to the sum of two or more members of the set.
