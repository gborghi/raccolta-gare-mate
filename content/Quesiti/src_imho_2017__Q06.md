*Primitive point (x,y); finite set S of primitives with polynomial-sum identity*

> An ordered pair $(x, y)$ of integers is called a primitive point if the greatest common divisor of $x$ and $y$ is $1$. Given a finite set $S$ of primitive points, prove that there exist a positive integer $n$ and integers $a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_n$ such that, for each $(x, y)$ in $S$, we have:
> $$a_0 x^n + a_1 x^{n-1} y + a_2 x^{n-2} y^2 + \cdots + a_{n-1} x y^{n-1} + a_n y^n = 1.$$
