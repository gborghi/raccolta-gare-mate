*Exercise 2, Q10: inequality on product of primes between sqrt(N) and N*

> Exercise 2 - Asymptotic behaviour of the sequence of super primes. Of course, the ordered sequence $(s_n)$ of super prime numbers tends to $+\infty$, but one wishes to show that it tends very fast to infinity, in the sense that the quotient $\left(\frac{s_{n+1}}{s_n}\right)$ tends itself to $+\infty$. More explicitly, we fix a non-zero natural integer $M$, and we will show that there exists a rank $n_0$ such that, for every $n \ge n_0$, one has $\frac{s_{n+1}}{s_n} \ge M$. To do this, we admit the following result, which it is therefore useless to demonstrate: For every non-zero natural integer $N$, one has $Q_N \le 4^N$, where $Q_N$ is the product of the prime numbers comprised (in the broad sense) between $1$ and $N$. (10) Show that for every integer $N \ge 4^{2(M+1)}$, one has $4^{(M+1)\left(\pi(N) - \pi(\sqrt{N})\right)} \le 4^N$. One may consider the product of the prime numbers comprised between $\sqrt{N}$ (not included) and $N$.
