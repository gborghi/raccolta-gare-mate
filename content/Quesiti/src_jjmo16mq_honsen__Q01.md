# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2018 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 1

*Game on regular k-gon with integers; find all k where B always wins*

> Let $k$ be an integer with $k \ge 3$. Using a regular $k$-gon, players $A$ and $B$ play the following game. The following is repeated:
> 
> $A$ writes one integer from $1$ to $100$ on a blank face of the regular $k$-gon (one not yet used); $B$ writes, on that same face, whether that integer is a perfect square or not.
> 
> Scoring is based on the faces of the regular $k$-gon: if there exist three vertices of the $k$-gon that form an isosceles triangle such that all three corresponding faces have the same value written by $B$, then $B$ wins; otherwise $A$ wins. Find all values of $k$ such that, regardless of $A$'s choices, $B$ can always guarantee a win.
