*Functions c(n,k) with c(n,0)=c(n,n)=1 and c(n+1,k)=2^k c(n,k)+c(n,k-1); prove c(n,k)=c(n,n-k).*

> The functions $c(n, k)$ are defined for integers $n \ge k \ge 0$ by $c(n, 0) = c(n, n) = 1$ for all $n \ge 0$ and
> $$c(n+1, k) = 2^k c(n, k) + c(n, k-1) \quad \text{for} \quad n \ge k \ge 1.$$
> Prove that $c(n, k) = c(n, n-k)$ for all $n$ and $k$.
