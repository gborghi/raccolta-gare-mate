*Tetrahedron ABCD; M,N midpoints of AB,CD. A point P on MN satisfies MP=CN and NP=AC; O is circumcenter. Show O!=P implies OP perpendicular to MN.*

> In a tetrahedron $ABCD$, $M$ and $N$ are the midpoints of the edges $AB$ and $CD$, respectively. Suppose that a point $P$ on segment $MN$ satisfies $MP=CN$ and $NP=AC$. Let $O$ be the circumcenter of the tetrahedron. Show that if $O\ne P$, then $OP\perp MN$.
