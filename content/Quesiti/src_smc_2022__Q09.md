*Calculate the distance light travels in one nanosecond at speed 3×10^8 m/s.*

> A light-nanosecond is the distance that a photon can travel at the speed of light in one billionth of a second. The speed of light is $3 \times 10^8$ ms$^{-1}$. How far is a light-nanosecond?
>
> - **(A)** 3 cm
> - **(B)** 30 cm
> - **(C)** 3 m
> - **(D)** 30 m
> - **(E)** 300 m
