*Prove recursively defined d(n,m) are always integers*

> Numbers $d(n, m)$ with $m$, $n$ integers, $0 \le m \le n$, are defined by
> $$d(n, 0) = d(n, n) = 1 \quad \text{for all } n \ge 0$$
> and
> $$m\,d(n, m) = m\,d(n-1, m) + (2n - m)\,d(n-1, m-1)$$
> for $0 < m < n$. Prove that all the $d(n, m)$ are integers.
