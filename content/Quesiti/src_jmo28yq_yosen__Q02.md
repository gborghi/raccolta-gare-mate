*Distribute cards 1-9 into 3 boxes forming arithmetic progressions*

> There are 9 cards, each labeled with one of the integers from $1$ to $9$, one card per number. They are distributed, 3 cards each, into 3 indistinguishable boxes. In how many ways can this be done so that, for every box, the three numbers on its cards form an arithmetic progression when arranged in increasing order? Here, three numbers $a, b, c$ form an arithmetic progression when $b - a = c - b$ holds.
