*Infinite sequence from finite start: finitely many positive or finitely many negative terms*

> Let $N$ be a positive integer and let $(s_k)_{k \ge 1}$ be a sequence of positive integers with all terms at most $N$. Annabel begins by choosing integers $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_N$. She then extends this to an infinite sequence $(x_n)_{n \ge 1}$ of integers by defining
> $$x_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} s_i x_i$$
> for each $n > N$.
> 
> Show that there are either finitely many strictly positive terms or finitely many strictly negative terms in the infinite sequence $(x_n)$.
