# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2003 — Nível 2 — Fase 3 — Quesito 1

*Count 2x2 grids with integers 1–9 under ordering constraints*

> In a $2 \times 2$ table, as shown below, we write integers from $1$ to $9$ obeying the following rule: $A > B$, $C > D$, $A > C$, and $B > D$.
> 
> $$\begin{array}{|c|c|}\hline A & B \\ \hline C & D \\ \hline\end{array}$$
> 
> a) How many different tables exist such that $B = C$?
> 
> b) How many different tables exist in total?
