*Length DE from incircle and A-excircle tangent-line intersections*

> The incircle of triangle $ABC$ touches sides $BC, CA, AB$ at points $P, Q, R$ respectively, and the excircle in angle $A$ touches side $BC$, line $CA$, line $AB$ at points $S, T, U$ respectively. Let $I$ be the incenter of triangle $ABC$. Let $D$ be the intersection of line $PQ$ and line $ST$, and let $E$ be the intersection of line $PR$ and line $SU$. Given $AI = 3$, $IP = 1$, $PS = 2$, find the length of segment $DE$. Here $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$, and the excircle in angle $A$ of triangle $ABC$ is the circle tangent to side $BC$, to the extension of side $AB$ beyond $B$, and to the extension of side $AC$ beyond $C$.
