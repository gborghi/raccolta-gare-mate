*Given c>-2, a sum of square-root terms equal to sqrt(c+2) times the sum of the x_i implies c=2 or all x_i equal.*

> A real number $c > -2$ is given. Prove that if positive numbers $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$ satisfy
> $$\sqrt{x_1^2 + cx_1 x_2 + x_2^2} + \sqrt{x_2^2 + cx_2 x_3 + x_3^2} + \cdots + \sqrt{x_n^2 + cx_n x_1 + x_1^2} = \sqrt{c+2}\,(x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_n),$$
> then $c = 2$ or $x_1 = x_2 = \cdots = x_n$.
