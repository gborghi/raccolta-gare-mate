*Frogs on line segments, Geoff controls placement, parity of n*

> There are $n \ge 2$ line segments in the plane such that every two segments cross, and no three segments meet at a point. Geoff has to choose an endpoint of each segment and place a frog on it, facing the other endpoint. Then he will clap his hands $n - 1$ times. Each time he claps, each frog will immediately jump forward to the next intersection point on its segment. Frogs never change the direction of their jumps. Geoff wishes to place the frogs in such a way that no two of them ever occupy the same intersection point at the same time.
> 
> (a) Prove that Geoff can always fulfil his wish if $n$ is odd.
> 
> (b) Prove that Geoff can never fulfil his wish if $n$ is even.
