*For square-free even n and prime p with p<2 sqrt(n), p not dividing n, p dividing n+k^2, write n as ab+bc+ca with distinct positive integers.*

> Let $n$ be a square-free positive even number, $k$ be an integer, $p$ be a prime number, satisfying $p < 2\sqrt{n}$, $p \nmid n$, $p \mid n + k^2$. Prove that $n$ can be written as $n = ab + bc + ca$, where $a$, $b$, $c$ are distinctive positive integers.
