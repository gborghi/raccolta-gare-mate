- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q01|Quesito 1]] — Find all real triples solving a symmetric quadratic system.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q02|Quesito 2]] — Find positive integer pairs making both k^2+4m and m^2+5k perfect squares.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q03|Quesito 3]] — In a convex quadrilateral with AB=CD that is not a parallelogram, the projections of AB and CD onto line MN (M,N midpoints of diagonals) both equal MN.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q04|Quesito 4]] — Determine the number of sequences of length n with terms in {0,...,9} such that among any three consecutive terms at least two are equal.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q05|Quesito 5]] — In an acute triangle with angle C = 45 degrees, circumcenter O and orthocenter H, the line through O perpendicular to CO meets AC and BC at K and L; prove OK+KH=OL+LH.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q06|Quesito 6]] — Prove a three-term reciprocal inequality bounded above by a symmetric AM-type expression.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q07|Quesito 7]] — In a tetrahedron ABCD, Q is the intersection of the bisector of angle ABC with AC and P is symmetric to D about Q; prove a triangle with sides RR'=BC/2 condition exists. (See warnings: parts of this problem are illegible.)
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q08|Quesito 8]] — For a prime p, show there is a permutation of 1..p-1 whose partial products are all distinct modulo p.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q09|Quesito 9]] — With F(k) the product of all divisors of k, prove or disprove that two distinct naturals have equal F.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q10|Quesito 10]] — In an acute triangle, two chains of perpendicular feet on the sides give triangles PQR and STU; prove they are congruent.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q11|Quesito 11]] — Count permutations of {1,...,6n-1} satisfying two index-difference monotonicity conditions.
- [[src_pol_2007_r1__Q12|Quesito 12]] — Express a polynomial positive on [a,b] as P^2 plus (x-a)(b-x) times a sum of squares.
