*Find the largest real k such that an inequality holds for all positive reals*

> Find the largest real number $k$ such that for every triple of positive real numbers $a, b, c$ satisfying $a^2 > bc$, the inequality
> $$(a^2 - bc)^2 > k(b^2 - ca)(c^2 - ab)$$
> holds.
