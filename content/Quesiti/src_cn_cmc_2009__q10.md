*Sequence satisfying linear recurrence; general term and sum of first n terms*

> It is known that $p \ne 0$, $q \ne 0$ are real numbers, and the equation $x^2 - px - q = 0$ has two real roots $\alpha$ and $\beta$. The sequence $\{a_n\}$ satisfies $a_{n+2} = p \cdot a_{n+1} + q \cdot a_n$ for $n = 1, 2, \ldots$
> 
> (1) If $\alpha \ne \beta$, find the general expression of $\{a_n\}$ in terms of $\alpha$ and $\beta$.
> 
> (2) If $p = 1$, $q = \dfrac{1}{4}$, find the sum of the first $n$ terms of $\{a_n\}$.

> 
> (1) Se $\alpha \ne \beta$, trovare l'espressione generale di $\{a_n\}$ in termini di $\alpha$ e $\beta$.
> 
> (2) Se $p = 1$, $q = \dfrac{1}{4}$, si trova la somma dei primi termini $n$ di $\{a_n\}$.
