*Recurrence from quadratic roots; prove integer values and gcd = 1*

> Let $\alpha$ and $\beta$ be the roots of the quadratic equation $x^2 + mx - 1 = 0$, where $m$ is an odd integer. Let $\lambda_n = \alpha^n + \beta^n$, for $n \ge 0$. Prove that
> 
> (a) $\lambda_n$ is an integer; and
> 
> (b) $\gcd(\lambda_n, \lambda_{n+1}) = 1$.
