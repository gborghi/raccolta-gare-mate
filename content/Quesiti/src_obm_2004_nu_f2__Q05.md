*Compute limit of ratio of winning positions in floor-division game*

> Let $m\ge 2$ be an integer. Arnaldo and Bernaldo play the following game: each player, alternately, receives a positive integer $N_i$ and passes to the other player either $N_{i+1}=N_i-1$ or $N_{i+1}=\left\lfloor N_i/m\right\rfloor$. Whoever receives a positive integer $N_0$ and passes zero wins the game.
> 
> Let $A_m$ (resp. $B_m$) be the set of values of $N_0$, $N_0<n$, for which Arnaldo (resp. Bernaldo) has a winning strategy.
> 
> Compute $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{|A_m|}{|B_m|}$ as a function of $m$.
