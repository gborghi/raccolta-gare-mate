*999 circular squares with reals; find minimum m for sequential property*

> Find the minimum positive integer $m$ for which the following statement holds:
> 
> Real numbers are written in each of 999 squares arranged in a circle. For any square $A$ and any positive integer $k \le m$, at least one of the following holds:
> \begin{itemize}
> \item The difference between the number written in the square $k$ steps clockwise from $A$ and the number written in $A$ equals $k$.
> \item The difference between the number written in the square $k$ steps counterclockwise from $A$ and the number written in $A$ equals $k$.
> \end{itemize}
> Then there exists a square $S$ such that, letting $x$ be the real number written in $S$, for every positive integer $k$ with $k < 999$, at least one of the following holds:
> \begin{itemize}
> \item The number written in the square $k$ steps clockwise from $S$ equals $x + k$.
> \item The number written in the square $k$ steps counterclockwise from $S$ equals $x + k$.
> \end{itemize}
