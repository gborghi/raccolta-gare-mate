*Quadrilateral ABCD with midpoints P,Q of CD,AB; lines meet; prove area ADX+area BCY=area PXQY.*

> $ABCD$ is a quadrilateral and $P$, $Q$ are mid-points of $CD$, $AB$ respectively. Let $AP$, $DQ$ meet at $X$, and $BP$, $CQ$ meet at $Y$. Prove that
> $$\text{area of } ADX + \text{area of } BCY = \text{area of quadrilateral } PXQY.$$
