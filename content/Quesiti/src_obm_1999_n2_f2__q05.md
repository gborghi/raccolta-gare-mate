*Adding two already-reduced fractions A/B + C/D over their lcm denominator; prove the resulting fraction is already in lowest terms.*

> A mathematics teacher gave his students the addition $\dfrac{A}{B} + \dfrac{C}{D}$, where $A$, $B$, $C$ and $D$ are positive integers and the fractions are already simplified to the maximum (that is, the denominators are coprime with their respective numerators). After simplifying it to the minimum (that is, writing the result with the least common denominator and then reducing the result), show that the fraction the students will find as result is already simplified.
