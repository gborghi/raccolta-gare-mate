# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2024 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 4

*Circumcircle diameter endpoint lies on circumcircle of auxiliary triangle via angle chase*

> Let triangle $ABC$ satisfy $AB < AC$. Let $O$ be the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$, and let $M$ be the midpoint of arc $BC$ not containing $A$ on the circumcircle. Let $D$ be the point on the extension of side $AB$ beyond $B$ such that $BD = BM$. Let $E$ be the point on side $AB$ (between $A$ and $B$) such that $CE = CM$. Let the circumcircle of $\triangle ABE$ meet the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ again at a point $X$ (other than $B$). Let $U$ and $V$ be the intersections of line $AX$ with the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ (so $UV$ is a chord, with $U$ on arc $AB$ not containing $C$ and $V$ on arc $BC$ not containing $A$, or as appropriate). Show that segment $UV$ is a diameter of the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ if and only if $D$, $O$, $X$ are collinear.
> 
> (More precisely: Let $E$ be the point on segment $AB$ with $CE = CM$. The circumcircle of $\triangle ABE$ meets the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ at $B$ and at another point $X$. Let $U$, $V$ be the two intersection points of line $AX$ with the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$. Prove that $UV$ is a diameter of the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ if and only if $D$, $O$, $X$ are collinear, where $D$ is on the ray from $A$ through $B$ beyond $B$ with $BD = BM$.)
