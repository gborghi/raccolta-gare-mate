*Total path length of a light ray reflecting between two mirrors at 30 degrees.*

> Two mirrors form an angle of $30^\circ$ at point $V$. A light ray, coming from a source $S$, is emitted parallel to one of the mirrors and is reflected by the other at point $A$, as shown in the figure. After a certain number of reflections, the ray returns to $S$. If $SA=AV=1$ metre, the distance travelled by the light ray, in metres, is
> 
> (A) 2 (B) $2+\sqrt{3}$ (C) $1+\sqrt{2}+\sqrt{3}$ (D) $\sqrt{2}\left(1+\sqrt{3}\right)$ (E) $5\sqrt{3}$
