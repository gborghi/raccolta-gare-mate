# Coupe Animath Printemps 2021 — Quesito 8

*n ants on the edges of a unit cube: two at geodesic distance at most 1, for n=13 and n=9*

> $n$ ants are placed on the edges of a cube whose edges have length $1$. For every positive real $d$, two ants are said to be at distance $d$ if the first ant must travel a distance of at least $d$ to reach the second ant, moving only along the edges of the cube.
> 
> 1. Suppose that $n = 13$. Prove that there exist, among the $13$ ants, two ants at distance less than or equal to $1$.
> 2. Suppose that $n = 9$. Prove that there exist, among the $9$ ants, two ants at distance less than or equal to $1$.
