*Sequence of power sums vanishing infinitely often*

> Consider the sequence $\{c_n\}$, where
> $$c_1 = a_1 + a_2 + \cdots + a_k,$$
> $$c_2 = a_1^2 + a_2^2 + \cdots + a_k^2,$$
> $$\vdots$$
> $$c_n = a_1^n + a_2^n + \cdots + a_k^n,$$
> in which $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_k$ are real numbers not all equal to zero. Suppose that an infinite number of terms of the sequence $\{c_n\}$ are equal to zero. Find all natural numbers $n$ for which $c_n = 0$.
