*Distinct remainders set: divisibility by n*

> Let be given a set $\{r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_n\}$ of natural numbers that give distinct remainders when divided by a natural number $n$. Prove that if $k \ge n/2$, then for every integer $n$ there exist indices $i$ and $j$ (not necessarily distinct) such that $r_i + r_j - n$ is divisible by $n$.
