*Two isosceles triangles sharing apex P; midpoints of arcs of two circles*

> In the plane, let $PAB$ and $PCD$ be triangles with $PA = PB$ and $PC = PD$. Suppose $P$, $A$, $C$ are collinear (in that order) and $B$, $P$, $D$ are collinear (in that order). Let $S_1$ be the circumcircle of $\triangle PAC$ (passing through $A$ and $C$) and $S_2$ be the circumcircle of $\triangle BPD$ (passing through $B$ and $D$), and suppose $S_1$ and $S_2$ meet at two distinct points $X$ and $Y$. Let $Z$ be the midpoint of the arc of $S_1$ not containing the other intersection, and $W$ be the midpoint of the arc of $S_2$ not containing the other intersection (i.e., $Z$ and $W$ are the midpoints of arcs $XY$ on $S_1$ and $S_2$ respectively, on the sides that do not contain $P$). Here $ZW$ denotes the length of segment $ZW$. Show that triangle $PXY$ has its circumcircle centered at the midpoint of $ZW$.
