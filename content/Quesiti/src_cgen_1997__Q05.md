# Concours Général des Lycées 1997 — Matematica — Quesito 5

*For a fixed oriented angle alpha, find the locus Gamma of point C seeing AB under that angle, show the centroid G and incenter I describe arcs, then choose C minimizing GI and maximize that minimum over alpha*

> In the plane, let $A$ and $B$ be two distinct points. For every point $C$ exterior to the line $(AB)$, denote by $G$ the isobarycenter (centroid) of triangle $ABC$ and by $I$ the center of its inscribed circle.
> \textbf{1.} Let $\alpha$ be a real number such that $0 < \alpha < \pi$. What is the set $\Gamma$ of points $C$ such that
> $$\left(\overrightarrow{CA},\ \overrightarrow{CB}\right) = \alpha + 2k\pi,$$
> $k$ being an integer? When $C$ describes $\Gamma$, show that $G$ and $I$ describe two arcs of a circle, which you will specify.
> \textbf{2.} Suppose now that $\dfrac{\pi}{3} < \alpha < \pi$. How must $C$ be chosen in $\Gamma$ so that the distance $GI$ is minimal?
> \textbf{3.} Denote by $f(\alpha)$ the minimal distance $GI$ of the previous question. Express $f(\alpha)$ in terms of $a = AB$ and $\alpha$. Determine the maximal value of $f(\alpha)$ when $\alpha$ describes $\left]\dfrac{\pi}{3};\ \pi\right[$.
