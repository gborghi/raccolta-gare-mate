*Prove the sequence a_n = sum_{i=1}^{n} floor(n/i) satisfies a_{n+1}>a_n and a_{n+1}=a_n both infinitely often*

> Let $\lfloor x \rfloor$ denote the greatest integer less than or equal to the real number $x$. Consider the sequence $a_1, a_2, \ldots$ defined by
> $$a_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left\lfloor \frac{n}{i} \right\rfloor$$
> for integers $n \ge 1$. Prove that $a_{n+1} > a_n$ for infinitely many $n$, and $a_{n+1} = a_n$ for infinitely many $n$.
> 
> [Here are some examples of the use of $\lfloor\cdot\rfloor$: $\lfloor 2 \rfloor = 2$, $\lfloor 1.7 \rfloor = 1$, $\lfloor -1.7 \rfloor = -2$.]
