*Convex pentagon with equal-angle conditions; prove AS perpendicular to CD where S is the intersection of diagonals BD and CE.*

> A convex pentagon $ABCDE$ is such that $\angle ABD = \angle ACE$, $\angle ACB = \angle ACD$, $\angle ADC = \angle ADE$, and $\angle ADB = \angle AEC$. The diagonals $BD$ and $CE$ intersect at $S$. Prove that $AS$ is perpendicular to $CD$.
