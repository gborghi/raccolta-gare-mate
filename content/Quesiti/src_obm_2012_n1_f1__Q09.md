*Fill 2×3 table with distinct positive integers so each row product is 210*

> In the $2 \times 3$ table below, we write a positive integer in each cell so that the product of the numbers in each row equals 210, and all six numbers in the table are distinct. In how many ways can this be done?
> 
> $$\begin{array}{|c|c|c||c|}\hline & & & 210 \\ \hline & & & 210 \\ \hline\end{array}$$
> 
> (A) 6 \quad (B) 12 \quad (C) 60 \quad (D) 120 \quad (E) infinitely many
