*Express cyclic relations between roots of a cubic in terms of coefficients*

> The roots of the equation $x^3 + bx + c = 0$ ($bc \ne 0$, $b$ and $c$ real) are $\alpha$, $\beta$ and $\gamma$. Determine $p$, $q$ and $r$ in terms of $b$ and $c$ so that
> $$\beta = p\alpha^2 + q\alpha + r, \quad \gamma = p\beta^2 + q\beta + r, \quad \alpha = p\gamma^2 + q\gamma + r$$
> and state a condition which ensures that $p$, $q$ and $r$ are real.
