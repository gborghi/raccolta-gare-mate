*Minimum number of test problems realizing an equivalence*

> A class has $30$ students assigned attendance numbers $1,2,\dots,30$. A test consisting of several problems was given to this class. After grading, the teacher noticed that the following two propositions about a subset $S$ of $\{1,2,\dots,30\}$ are equivalent: (1) for every problem, there exists an element $k$ of $S$ such that the student with attendance number $k$ answered that problem correctly; (2) $S$ contains all multiples of $2$ from $1$ to $30$, or all multiples of $3$ from $1$ to $30$, or all multiples of $5$ from $1$ to $30$. Find the smallest possible number of problems on the test.
