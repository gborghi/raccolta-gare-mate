*Square paper cut into two pairs of congruent triangles, find shortest segment dividing triangle*

> A square piece of paper $ABCD$ of side length 2 and centre $O$ is cut in half to form two equal pieces $ABC$ and $CDA$. If the triangle $ABC$ has to be cut into two parts which would also be congruent to each other, one would naturally cut along the line of symmetry $BO$. However, there are other ways of doing this. Find, with justification, the length and location of the shortest segment on which divides the triangle $ABC$ into two congruent parts.
