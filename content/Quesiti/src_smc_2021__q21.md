*Given 2^m + 2^k = p and 2^m − 2^k = q, express 2^(m+k) in terms of p and q.*

> The numbers $m$ and $k$ satisfy the equations $2^m + 2^k = p$ and $2^m - 2^k = q$. What is the value of $2^{m+k}$ in terms of $p$ and $q$?
>
> - **(A)** $\dfrac{p^2 - q^2}{4}$
> - **(B)** $\dfrac{pq}{2}$
> - **(C)** $p + q$
> - **(D)** $\dfrac{(p-q)^2}{4}$
> - **(E)** $\dfrac{p+q}{p-q}$
