*Prove triangle ABC similar to triangle DEF formed by circle intersections*

> Let $ABC$ be an acute triangle with $AB < BC$ and $AC < BC$. Let $\Gamma$ be its circumcircle. Let $\Gamma_1$, $\Gamma_2$, $\Gamma_3$ be circles centered at $A$, $B$, $C$ with radii $AB$, $BC$, $CA$ respectively. Let $D$, $E$, $F$ be the intersection points of $\Gamma_1 \cap \Gamma_2$, $\Gamma_2 \cap \Gamma_3$, $\Gamma_3 \cap \Gamma_1$ respectively that are different from $A$. Prove that triangle $ABC$ and triangle $DEF$ are similar. (Here $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$.)
