*Positive integer n such that the number of divisors of 2n equals twice the number of divisors of n; conclude what n is.*

> Consider the positive integer $n$ such that the number of positive divisors of the double of $n$ is equal to the double of the number of positive divisors of $n$. We can conclude that $n$ is (A) a prime number (B) an even number (C) an odd number (D) a perfect square (E) an integer power of $2$
