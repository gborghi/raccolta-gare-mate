*Properties of f(x)=(e^x−1)^(1/x) for x>0*

> Consider the function $f(x) = \left(e^x - 1\right)^{1/x}$, defined for $x > 0$.
> 
> **a)** Show that $f(x)$ is strictly increasing.
> 
> **b)** Let $V(y)$ be defined by $V(y) = x$ if and only if $f(x) = y$. Given $0 < a < b < c$, real numbers, consider the equation $a^b + b^a = c^c$. Express $x$ in terms of $a$, $b$, and $c$ using elementary functions and $V$.
