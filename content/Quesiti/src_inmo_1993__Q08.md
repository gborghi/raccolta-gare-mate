*Bijection from {1,...,n} to itself iterated M times equals identity*

> Let $f$ be a bijective function from $A = \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ to itself. Prove that there is a positive integer $M$ such that $f^M(i) = i$ for each $i \in A$, where $f^M = f \circ f \circ \cdots \circ f$ ($M$ times).
