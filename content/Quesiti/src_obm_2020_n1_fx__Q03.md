# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2020 — Nível 1 — Fase x — Quesito 3

*Sums of the initial decimal digits of 7/33 and 11/41 after the decimal point: reach exactly 1202, show 2020 is impossible, reach at least 2021*

> When dividing an integer by another integer, we may obtain an integer result or not. For example, $12/3 = 4$ is an integer and $5/4 = 1{,}25$ is not an integer. There exist divisions in which the result is not an integer and, even using decimal places after the comma, the division never ends and we have a number with an infinite decimal representation. For example, $1/3 = 0{,}333\ldots$ so that the decimal representation of $1/3$ is infinite.
> 
> a) Juca observed that $7/33 = 0{,}212121\ldots$ We can observe the sums of the digits of the initial decimal places after the comma: $2$, $2+1 = 3$, $2+1+2 = 5$, and so on. How many initial decimal places after the comma of the decimal representation of $7/33$ would Juca need to sum in order to obtain exactly $1202$?
> 
> b) Show that it is not possible for Juca to sum the digits of some quantity of initial decimal places after the comma of the decimal representation of $7/33$ in order to obtain exactly $2020$.
> 
> c) Juca observed the decimal representation of $11/41$. How many initial decimal places after the comma would Juca need to sum in order to obtain a result greater than or equal to $2021$?
