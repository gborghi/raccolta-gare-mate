*Find all positive integer pairs p,q allowing indefinite blackboard operation avoiding multiples*

> Let $p$ and $q$ be positive integers. First, write the integer $1$ on a blackboard, then repeat the following operation:
> 
> \textbf{Operation:} Choose one of $p$ or $q$, and write on the blackboard the number obtained by adding the chosen value to the number currently on the blackboard.
> 
> Find all pairs $(p, q)$ of positive integers for which it is possible to repeat the operation indefinitely (for any number of steps, in any order of choices) without ever writing a multiple of $p$ or a multiple of $q$ on the blackboard.
