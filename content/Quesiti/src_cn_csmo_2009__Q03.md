*Prove inequality involving square roots of expressions in positive reals*

> Let $x$, $y$, $z$ be positive numbers, and $\sqrt{a} = x(y-z)^2$, $\sqrt{b} = y(z-x)^2$, $\sqrt{c} = z(x-y)^2$. Prove that $a^{\frac{1}{2}} + b^{\frac{1}{2}} + c^{\frac{1}{2}} \ge 2(ab + bc + ca)$. (Posed by Tang Lihua)
