# British Mathematical Olympiad 1979-80 — Round 2 — Quesito 4

*Bridge tournament: show two people must play as partners*

> Given a set of $n$ people, it is desired to arrange a series of bridge games so that each of the $n$ people play as opponents in exactly one game. In each game the $n$ people are divided into groups of 4, where $n = 4m$, $m$ is a positive integer. Prove that, in this arrangement, at least two people must play as partners (i.e. on the same side) in more than one game.
