*Four distinct positive integers A, B, C with three quadratics all having real or all non-real roots*

> Show that among any four distinct positive numbers one can choose three, say $A$, $B$, $C$, such that the three quadratic equations
> $$Bx^2 + x + C = 0$$
> $$Cx^2 + x + A = 0$$
> $$Ax^2 + x + B = 0$$
> either all have real roots or all have non-real roots.
