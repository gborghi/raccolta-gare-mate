*Functional equation for I(u) defined by an integral, then compute I(u)*

> For all real $u$, let $I(u) = \int_0^u \ln(1 - 2a\cos x + a^2)\,dx$.
> 
> a) Prove that $I(u) = I(-u) = \frac{1}{2}I(u^2)$. [Note: the original uses $I(u) = \frac{1}{2}I(u^2)$ with the substitution $a \mapsto a^2$; the precise statement is: prove $I(u) = I(-u)$ and $I(u) = \frac{1}{2}I(u^2)$.]
> 
> b) Compute $I(u)$ for all $u \in \mathbb{R}$.
