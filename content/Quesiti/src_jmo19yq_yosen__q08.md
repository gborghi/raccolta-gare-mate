*Least-degree polynomial in identity f(x^3)+g(x)=f(x)+x^5 g(x)*

> Let $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ both be nonzero polynomials with real coefficients satisfying $$f(x^3)+g(x)=f(x)+x^5 g(x).$$ Among the polynomials $f(x)$ that can occur in this way, find one of smallest degree.
