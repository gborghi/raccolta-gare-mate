*Partition {1,...,19} into two subsets so the sum of one equals the product of the other*

> (40 marks) Given set $A = \{1, 2, \ldots, 19\}$, does there exist non-empty subsets $S_1$, $S_2$ of set $A$ satisfying the following conditions?
> (1) $S_1 \cap S_2 = \varnothing$, $S_1 \cup S_2 = A$;
> (2) $S_1$ and $S_2$ both have at least four elements;
> (3) the sum of all elements of $S_1$ is equal to the product of all elements of $S_2$.
> Prove your conclusion.
