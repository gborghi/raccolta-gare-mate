*Characterize all real functions satisfying f(x)(f(x)-x)=0 for all x*

> If $f : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ is a function such that, for all $x \in \mathbb{R}$,
> $$f(x)\bigl(f(x) - x\bigr) = 0,$$
> then:
> 
> A) $f$ is the zero function.
> B) $f$ is the identity function, i.e., $f(x) = x$ for all real $x$.
> C) $f$ is either the zero function or the identity function.
> D) There are exactly $4$ possible functions $f$.
> E) There are infinitely many functions $f$.
