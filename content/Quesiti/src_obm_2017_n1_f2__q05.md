*Distance inequality involving rows of a real d×n matrix and subspace supremum*

> Let $d \le n$ be positive integers and $A$ a real $d \times n$ matrix. We view $A$ as a linear transformation $\mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^d$ given by $v \mapsto Av$ (viewing elements of $\mathbb{R}^n$ as column vectors). Let $\sigma(A)$ be the supremum over all $d$-dimensional subspaces $W$ of $\mathbb{R}^n$ of $\inf_{w \in W,\, |w|=1} |Aw|$. For each $i \in \{1, \ldots, d\}$, let $r(i) \in \mathbb{R}^n$ be the $i$-th row-vector of $A$, and for $j \in \{1, \ldots, d\}$ with $j \ne i$, let $d(r(i), r(j))$ denote the Euclidean distance between $r(i)$ and $r(j)$.
> 
> For each $i \in \{1, \ldots, d\}$, let $e_i$ be the $i$-th element of the canonical basis of $\mathbb{R}^d$. Prove that
> $$\sigma(A) \le \min_{i} \min_{j : j \ne i} d(r(i), r(j)) \le \sqrt{n} \cdot \sigma(A).$$
> 
> **Remark.** $|\cdot|$ denotes the Euclidean norm; $A^T$ is the transpose of $A$; $d(r(i), r(j))$, $1 \le j \le d$, $j \ne i$, denotes the distance of $r(i)$ from $r(j)$.
