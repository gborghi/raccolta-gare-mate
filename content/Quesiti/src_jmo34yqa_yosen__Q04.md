*Minimum n so that 5-round stone replacement always ends on a black stone*

> Let $n$ be an integer with $0 \le n \le 5^5$. Place $n$ black stones and $5^5 - n$ white stones in a row, then repeat the following operation 5 times:
> 
> Divide the row of stones into groups of 5 from left to right. For each group, replace all 5 stones in that group with 5 copies of the color that appears more often among those 5 stones.
> 
> Find the minimum value of $n$ such that, regardless of the initial arrangement, the single remaining stone is always black.
