*n^2 with last two digits removed is again a perfect square*

> a) Find a strictly positive integer $n$ such that, if one writes $n^2$ and removes its last two digits, the resulting number is still the square of an integer.
> b) Find all strictly positive integers $n$ that are not multiples of $10$ such that, writing $n^2$ and deleting its last two digits, one again obtains the square of an integer (one considers that a number with zero digits is equal to zero).
