*Find medal-threshold triples so exactly 2 participant counts give a fixed no-medal count*

> At a mathematics competition, each participant receives at most one medal: gold, silver, or bronze. When $n$ people participate, the number of gold medals awarded is $\left\lfloor \dfrac{n}{a} \right\rfloor$, the number of silver medals is $\left\lfloor \dfrac{n}{b} \right\rfloor$, and the number of bronze medals is $\left\lfloor \dfrac{n}{c} \right\rfloor$, where $a, b, c$ are positive integers with $a \ge b \ge c$. No person receives more than one medal. For a given non-negative integer $k$, suppose that for every integer $n \ge 3$, the number of values of $n$ for which exactly $k$ people receive no medal is exactly $2$. Find all triples $(a, b, c)$ of positive integers satisfying this condition. (Here $\lfloor r \rfloor$ denotes the greatest integer not exceeding the real number $r$.)
