# Pre-Regional Mathematical Olympiad (PRMO) 2018 — Quesito 23

*Largest n for Cauchy-Schwarz-type inequality with denominators b/29+c/31 etc.*

> What is the largest positive integer $n$ such that
> $$\frac{a^2}{\dfrac{b}{29}+\dfrac{c}{31}} + \frac{b^2}{\dfrac{c}{29}+\dfrac{a}{31}} + \frac{c^2}{\dfrac{a}{29}+\dfrac{b}{31}} \ge n(a+b+c)$$
> holds for all positive real numbers $a, b, c$?
