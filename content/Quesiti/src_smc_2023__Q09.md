*Crossnumber with squares, cubes and powers*

> A cross-number has the following clues. Across: 1. A square; 3. A fourth power. Down: 1. Twice a fifth power; 2. A cube. The grid is a $3\times3$ square with the centre cell shaded (blocked); cell 1 is top-left, cell 2 is top-right, cell 3 is bottom-left, and the top-middle cell is marked $*$. When completed correctly, the cross-number is filled with four three-digit numbers. What digit is $*$?
>
> - **(A)** $0$
> - **(B)** $1$
> - **(C)** $2$
> - **(D)** $4$
> - **(E)** $6$
