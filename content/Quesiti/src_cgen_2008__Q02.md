*Minimising over lines: existence and uniqueness of the line minimising the sum of vertical segment lengths $AA'+BB'+CC'$, and the line minimising the sum of distances from the three vertices of a triangle.*

> 1. The plane is referred to an orthonormal frame $(O;\vec{i},\vec{j})$. We consider a triangle $ABC$ no side of which is parallel to the axis of ordinates $Oy$. To every line $\mathscr{D}$ not parallel to $Oy$ we associate the points $A'$, $B'$ and $C'$, intersections of $\mathscr{D}$ with the parallels to $Oy$ drawn through $A$, $B$ and $C$ respectively.
> Show that there exists a unique line $\mathscr{D}$ for which the sum $s$ of the lengths $AA'+BB'+CC'$ is minimal, and characterise it.
> 
> 2. Show that there exists a line $\mathscr{D}$ for which the sum $s_1$ of the distances from $A$, $B$ and $C$ to $\mathscr{D}$ is minimal. Show that this line is unique if $ABC$ is not isosceles, and characterise it.
