*Distribute 24 each of two candies among 3 people avoiding a condition*

> There are two kinds of candy, $A$ and $B$, with $24$ pieces of each. We distribute all of these among three people $X, Y, Z$ with none left over. Among such distributions, how many are there for which there do NOT exist two people satisfying the following condition? Condition: one of the two people receives $a$ pieces of $A$ and $b$ pieces of $B$, the other receives $a'$ pieces of $A$ and $b'$ pieces of $B$, and both $a \le a'$ and $b \le b'$ hold.
