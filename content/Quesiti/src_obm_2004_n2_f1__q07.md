*Difference p-q between same-flavor and different-flavor candy probabilities*

> There are 1002 banana candies and 1002 apple candies in a box. Lara takes, without seeing, two candies from the box. Let $p$ be the probability that the two candies are of the same flavor and $q$ the probability that the candies are of different flavors. What is the difference between $p$ and $q$?
> 
> **A)** $0$ $\quad$ **B)** $\dfrac{1}{2004}$ $\quad$ **C)** $\dfrac{1}{2003}$ $\quad$ **D)** $\dfrac{2}{2003}$ $\quad$ **E)** $\dfrac{1}{1001}$
