*Positive reals summing to 1; inequality involving sum of |xi-yi| and min ratios*

> Let $n$ be a strictly positive integer and $x_1, \ldots, x_n, y_1, \ldots, y_n$ be strictly positive reals such that $x_1 + \cdots + x_n = y_1 + \cdots + y_n = 1$. Show that:
> $$|x_1 - y_1| + |x_2 - y_2| + \cdots + |x_n - y_n| \le 2 - \min_{1 \le i \le n} \frac{x_i}{y_i} - \min_{1 \le i \le n} \frac{y_i}{x_i}$$
> 
> For reals $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$, $\displaystyle\min_u a_u$ denotes the smallest of the $n$ numbers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$.
