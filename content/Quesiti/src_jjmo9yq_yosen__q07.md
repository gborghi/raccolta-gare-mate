*Smallest integer n ≥ 11 with exactly 23 perfect squares in [n, n+2011]*

> Find the smallest positive integer $n \ge 11$ such that among the integers $n, n+1, n+2, \ldots, n+2011$, there are exactly $23$ perfect squares.
