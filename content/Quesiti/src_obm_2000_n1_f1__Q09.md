# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2000 — Nível 1 — Fase 1 — Quesito 9

*Two-digit number reversed gives odd; difference equals cube*

> A certain two-digit number $N$ is the square of a natural number. Reversing the digits of that number, one obtains an odd number. The difference between the two numbers is the cube of a natural number. We can assert that the sum of the digits of $N$ is:
> 
> (A) $7$ \quad (B) $10$ \quad (C) $13$ \quad (D) $9$ \quad (E) $11$
