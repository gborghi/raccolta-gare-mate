*Circulant 3x3 matrices with det=1: rational entries infinite, integer entries finite*

> Consider matrices of the form
> $$M = \begin{pmatrix} a & b & c \\ c & a & b \\ b & c & a \end{pmatrix} \qquad \text{with} \quad \det M = 1.$$
> Prove that:
> 
> (a) There are infinitely many matrices of the above form with all entries $a, b, c$ rational.
> 
> (b) There are only finitely many matrices of the above form with all entries $a, b, c$ integers.
