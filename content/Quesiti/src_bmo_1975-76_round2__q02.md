*Root of real equation lies in given interval*

> The real number $t$ is a root of the equation
> $$x^n + a_1 x^{n-1} + \cdots + a_{n-1} x + a_n = 0, \quad (n \geq 2),$$
> where the coefficients are real and satisfy $-1 \leq a_k \leq 1$, $(2 \leq k \leq n)$.
> 
> Prove that
> $$-(1 + \sqrt{5}) \leq t \leq \frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{5}).$$
