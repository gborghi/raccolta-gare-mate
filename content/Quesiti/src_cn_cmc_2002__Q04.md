*Number of points P on ellipse such that triangle PAB has prescribed area*

> The straight line $\dfrac{x}{a} + \dfrac{y}{b} = 1$ intersects the ellipse $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2} + \dfrac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$ at two points $A$ and $B$. There is a point $P$ on the ellipse such that the area of $\triangle PAB$ is equal to 3. There are/is \quad (\quad) such point/points.
> 
> (A) 1 \qquad (B) 2 \qquad (C) 3 \qquad (D) 4
