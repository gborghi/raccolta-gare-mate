*Seven distinct tetrominoes; prove they tile a 4x7 rectangle*

> A tetromino is one formed of four unit squares connected by common edges.
> 
> (i) If we do not distinguish between the possible rotations of a tetromino, prove that there are seven distinct tetrominoes.
> 
> (ii) Prove or disprove the statement: It is possible to pack all seven distinct tetrominoes into a $4 \times 7$ rectangle without overlapping.
