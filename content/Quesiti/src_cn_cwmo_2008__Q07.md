*Find the greatest k allowing three k-element sets of distinct nonnegative integers with componentwise sums equal to n*

> For a given positive integer $n$, find the greatest positive integer $k$, such that there exist three sets of $k$ distinct nonnegative integers, $A = \{x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_k\}$, $B = \{y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_k\}$ and $C = \{z_1, z_2, \ldots, z_k\}$ with $x_i + y_i + z_i = n$ for any $1 \leqslant i \leqslant k$. (Posed by Li Shenghong)

**Metodo:** [[method_conteggio|Conteggio]], [[method_estremalita|Estremalità]], [[method_casework|Casework]]

**Area:** [[Combinatoria, Logica e Probabilita]], [[Aritmetica e Teoria dei Numeri]]
**Risposta:** $k = \left\lfloor \frac{2n}{3} \right\rfloor + 1$
