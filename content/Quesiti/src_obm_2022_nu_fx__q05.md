*Upper density product condition implies modular intersection*

> Given $X \subseteq \mathbb{N}$, define $d(X)$ as the largest $c \in [0,1]$ such that for all $a < c$ and $n_0 \in \mathbb{N}$, there exist $m, r \in \mathbb{N}$ with $r \ge n_0$ and $|X \cap [m, m+r]| / r \ge a$. Let $E, F \subseteq \mathbb{N}$ with $d(E) \cdot d(F) > 1/4$. Prove that for any prime $p$ and $k \in \mathbb{N}$, there exist $m \in E$ and $n \in F$ with $m \equiv n \pmod{p^k}$.

Let $E, F \subseteq \mathbb{N}$ con $d(E) \cdot d(F) > 1/4$. Prove che per ogni primo $p$ e $k \in \mathbb{N}$ esistono $m \in E$ e $n \in F$ con $m \equiv n \pmod{p^k}$.
