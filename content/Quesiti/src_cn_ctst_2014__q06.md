*Bound on number of unordered factorizations via largest prime factor*

> For a positive integer $k > 1$, let $f(k)$ denote the number of ways of factoring $k$ into a product of positive integers each greater than $1$ (the order of factors is not counted; for example, $12 = 2 \times 6 = 3 \times 4 = 2 \times 2 \times 3$, so $f(12) = 4$). Prove that if $p$ is the largest prime divisor of $n$, then
> $$f(n) \le \frac{n}{p}.$$
> 
> (Proposed by Ya Yijun)
