*Permutation of nonempty subsets with consecutive intersection size 1*

> Let $n \in \mathbb{N}^*$, $n \geq 3$, and $G_n = \{1, 2, \cdots, n\}$. Prove that there is a permutation $Q_1, Q_2, \cdots, Q_{2^n - 1}$ of all the nonempty subsets of $G_n$, such that $|Q_i \cap Q_{i+1}| = 1$ for $i = 1, 2, \cdots, 2^n - 2$.
