*Minimum digit sum for antichain of {1,2}-digit integers*

> Let $n_1, n_2, \ldots, n_k$ be pairwise distinct positive integers satisfying:
> (1) In the decimal representation of each $n_i$, each digit belongs to $\{1, 2\}$.
> (2) For any $i \ne j$, $n_i$ cannot be obtained from $n_j$ by adding some digits on the right.
> Find the minimum value of $\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{k} S(n_i)$, where $S(m)$ denotes the sum of digits of $m$ in decimal representation.
