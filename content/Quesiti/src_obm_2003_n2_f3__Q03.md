*Prove BX=CX iff PQ is diameter of circumscribed circle*

> Triangle $ABC$ is inscribed in circle $S$ and $AB < AC$. The line through $A$ perpendicular to $BC$ meets $S$ at $P$ ($P \neq A$). The point $X$ lies on segment $AC$ and the line $BX$ meets $S$ at $Q$ ($Q \neq B$).
> 
> Prove that $BX = CX$ if and only if $PQ$ is a diameter of $S$.
