# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2017 — Yosen (Preliminary) — Quesito 8

*Length XE from two circumcircles meeting at X*

> On side $BC$ of triangle $ABC$ there are two points $D$ and $E$, with the four points $B,D,E,C$ lying in this order. We have $\angle BAD=\angle ACE$ and $\angle ABD=\angle CAE$. Let $X$ be the second intersection point (other than $A$) of the circumcircle of triangle $ABE$ and the circumcircle of triangle $ADC$, and let $F$ be the intersection of $AX$ and $BC$. Given $BF=5$, $CF=6$, and $XD=3$, find the length of segment $XE$. (If the answer contains a radical $\sqrt{XY}$, give the value $XY$ under the radical.)
