*Prove sum of reciprocals of circumradii equals 2/R*

> Let $\triangle ABC$ be a non-isosceles acute triangle, and point $O$ is the circumcenter. Let $A'$ be a point on the line $AO$ such that $\angle BAA' = \angle CA'A$. Construct $A'A_1 \perp AC$, $A'A_2 \perp AB$ with $A_1$ on $AC$, $A_2$ on $AB$ respectively. $AH_1$ is perpendicular to $BC$ at $H_1$. Write $R_A$ as the circumradius of $\triangle H_1 A_1 A_2$. Similarly define $R_B$ and $R_C$. Prove that
> $$\frac{1}{R_A} + \frac{1}{R_B} + \frac{1}{R_C} = \frac{2}{R},$$
> where $R$ is the circumradius of $\triangle ABC$.
