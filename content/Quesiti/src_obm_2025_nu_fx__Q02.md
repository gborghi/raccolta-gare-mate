*Integer sequences; infinitely many N and M with ratio conditions*

> Determine whether there exist integers $a, b, c, d$ such that the sequences $(a_n)_{n \ge 0}$ and $(\beta_n)_{n \ge 0}$ defined by
> $$a_0 = \beta_0 = 0;$$
> $$a_1 = \beta_1 = 1;$$
> $$a_{n+2} = a\, a_{n+1} + b\, a_n \text{ for } n \ge 0;$$
> $$\beta_{n+2} = c\, \beta_{n+1} + d\, \beta_n \text{ for } n \ge 0;$$
> satisfy $\beta_n \ne 0$ for all $n \ge 1$, and both of the following conditions:
> 
> (i) There exist infinitely many natural numbers $N$ such that
> $$1 - \frac{1}{2^{2025}} < \left|\frac{a_N}{\beta_N}\right| < 1 + \frac{1}{2^{2025}}.$$
> 
> (ii) There exist infinitely many natural numbers $M$ such that
> $$\left|\frac{a_M}{\beta_M}\right| > 2^{2025}.$$
