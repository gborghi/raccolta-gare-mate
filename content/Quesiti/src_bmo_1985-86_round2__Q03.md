*Family of 2-element sets with intersection condition; existence proof*

> $P_1, P_2, \ldots, P_n$ ($n \ge 2$) are sets each having two elements. $P_i$ and $P_j$ ($i \ne j$) have an element in common if and only if $\{i, j\}$ is one of the sets $P_k$. Prove that one of $1, 2, \ldots, n$ is in exactly two of the sets $P_k$.
