*Finitely many integer arrays satisfying weighted factorial sum zero*

> (Test Paper A, Problem 1) Given positive integer $k$ ($k \geq 2$) and $k$ non-zero real numbers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_k$, prove that there are at most finitely many $k$-element integer arrays $(n_1, n_2, \ldots, n_k)$ satisfying that $n_1, n_2, \ldots, n_k$ are pairwise distinct and
> $$a_1 \cdot n_1! + a_2 \cdot n_2! + \cdots + a_k \cdot n_k! = 0.$$
