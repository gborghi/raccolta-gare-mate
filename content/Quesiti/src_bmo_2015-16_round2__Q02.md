# British Mathematical Olympiad 2015-16 — Round 2 — Quesito 2

*Hockey teams: find largest chain Benjamin can always guarantee*

> Alison has a bag of 20 hockey teams, ordered by how good she thinks they are. She will choose three teams to list, and she will then choose either to tell him which she thinks is the strongest team of the three, or which she thinks is the weakest team of the three. Benjamin may do this as many times as he likes. Determine the largest $N$ such that Benjamin can always find a sequence $T_1, T_2, \ldots, T_N$ of teams with the property that he knows that $T_1$ is better than $T_2$, $T_2$ is better than $T_3$, $\ldots$, and $T_{N-1}$ is better than $T_N$, for each $1 \le i < N$.
