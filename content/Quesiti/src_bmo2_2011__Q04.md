*Largest parallelogram-free subset of integer grid*

> Let $G$ be the set of points $(x, y)$ in the plane such that $x$ and $y$ are integers in the range $1 \le x, y \le 2011$. A subset $S$ of $G$ is said to be parallelogram-free if there is no proper parallelogram all of whose vertices are in $S$. Note that a proper parallelogram is one where no vertex lies on the same line. Find the largest possible size of a parallelogram-free subset of $G$.
