*Tree of islands sending letters: fixed point or mutual exchange exists*

> There are $2010$ islands, joined by $2009$ bridges. Any two islands are either joined by exactly one bridge or not joined at all, and the two endpoints of each bridge are two distinct islands. Moreover, from any island one can travel to any other island by crossing some bridges. (Thus the bridges form a connected graph, namely a tree.)
> 
> Now each island sends one letter to some island (it is allowed for an island to send a letter to itself). It then turns out that the following fact holds:
> 
> Whenever island $A$ and island $B$ are joined by a bridge, the island to which $A$ sends its letter and the island to which $B$ sends its letter are either joined by a bridge or are the same island.
> 
> Prove that at least one of the following statements (1), (2) holds:
> (1) There exists an island that sent a letter to itself.
> (2) There exist two islands, joined by a bridge, that exchanged letters with each other (each sent its letter to the other).
