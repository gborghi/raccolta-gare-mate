*Three vessels, coprime capacities m and n, pour to get any k liters*

> We are given three vessels without the scale: two of them of capacities $m$ and $n$ liters are empty, and the third one of capacity $m + n$ liters is full of water, where $m$ and $n$ are coprime positive integers. Prove that for any $k = 1, 2, \ldots, m + n - 1$, pouring out from one vessel into another, we can obtain exactly $k$ liters of water in the third vessel.
