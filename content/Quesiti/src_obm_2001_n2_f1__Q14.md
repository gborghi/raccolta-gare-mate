*Number of boys in a class given division remainders and 15 girls*

> Counting the students of a class $4$ by $4$ leaves a remainder of $2$, and counting them $5$ by $5$ leaves a remainder of $1$. Knowing that $15$ of the students are girls and that the number of girls is greater than the number of boys, the number of boys in this class is:
> 
> (A) $7$ \quad (B) $8$ \quad (C) $9$ \quad (D) $10$ \quad (E) $11$
