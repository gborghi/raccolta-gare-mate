*Prove D lies on circle AQP in acute triangle configuration*

> In an acute-angled triangle $ABC$ with $AB < AC$, the incentre is $I$ and the perpendicular bisector of $BC$ meets $BI$ at $P$ and $CI$ at $Q$. The circles $BIQ$ and $CIP$ meet again at $X$. The lines $AX$ and $BC$ meet at $D$.
> 
> Prove that $D$ lies on the circle $AQP$.
