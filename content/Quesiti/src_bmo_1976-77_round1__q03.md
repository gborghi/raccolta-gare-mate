*Schur-type inequality and a sixth-degree symmetric inequality*

> (i) Prove that if $x$, $y$, $z$ are non-negative real numbers, then
> $$x(x-y)(x-z) + y(y-z)(y-x) + z(z-x)(z-y) \ge 0.$$
> (ii) Hence or otherwise show that for all real numbers $a$, $b$, $c$
> $$a^6 + b^6 + c^6 + 3a^2 b^2 c^2 \ge 2(b^3 c^3 + c^3 a^3 + a^3 b^3).$$
