*Largest M so that any 10 numbers in [1,M] contain three forming a quadratic with no real roots*

> (Test Paper B, Problem 3) Try to find the largest positive number $M > 1$ with the following property: for any 10 different real numbers chosen from the interval $[1, M]$, three of them can be selected, denoted from smallest to largest by $a < b < c$, such that the quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ has no real roots.
