*Good subset of regular n-gon (n prime) has exactly four elements*

> Let $n \ge 3$ be a natural number and let $A_1 A_2 \cdots A_n$ be a regular polygon. Let $X = \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$. A subset $\{i_1, i_2, \ldots, i_k\}$ of $X$, with $k \ge 3$ and $i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_k$, is called a \textit{good subset} if the angles of the polygon $A_{i_1} A_{i_2} \cdots A_{i_k}$, when arranged in order, are in arithmetic progression. If $n$ is a prime, show that a proper good subset of $X$ contains exactly four elements.
