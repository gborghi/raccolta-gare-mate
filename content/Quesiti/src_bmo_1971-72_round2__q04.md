*Find all polynomials p such that a·p(a²)+b·p(b²)+c·p(c²)=k·abc for triangle sides*

> When $k = 1$, find all polynomials $p$ in one variable such that
> $$a \cdot p(a^2) + b \cdot p(b^2) + c \cdot p(c^2) = k \cdot abc,$$
> where $a, b, c$ are the lengths of the sides $BC$, $CA$, $AB$ of triangle $ABC$. Prove your result. What is the effect of altering $k$?
