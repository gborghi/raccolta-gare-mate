*Angle bisectors, parallel conditions; prove AB=AC from collinearity*

> Let $ABC$ be a triangle. The lines $r$ and $s$ are the internal bisectors of $\angle ABC$ and $\angle BCA$, respectively. The points $E$ on $r$ and $D$ on $s$ are such that $AD \parallel BE$ and $AE \parallel CD$. The lines $BD$ and $CE$ meet at $F$. Show that if the points $A$, $F$, $E$ are collinear, then $AB = AC$.
