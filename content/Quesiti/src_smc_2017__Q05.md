*One light-year is ~6×10^12 miles; a galaxy is 13.4 thousand million light-years away. Roughly how many miles away is the galaxy?*

> One light-year is nearly $6 \times 10^{12}$ miles. In 2016, the Hubble Space Telescope set a new cosmic record, observing a galaxy $13.4$ thousand million light-years away.
> 
> Roughly how many miles is that?
>
> - **(A)** $8 \times 10^{20}$
> - **(B)** $8 \times 10^{21}$
> - **(C)** $8 \times 10^{22}$
> - **(D)** $8 \times 10^{23}$
> - **(E)** $8 \times 10^{24}$
