*Three lines CD, AQ, BR concur in parallelogram configuration with circumcircles*

> Let $ABCD$ be a parallelogram with $AC = BC$, and let $P$ be a point on the extension of side $AB$ beyond $B$. The circumcircle of triangle $ACD$ meets segment $PD$ at a point $Q$ other than $D$, and the circumcircle of triangle $APQ$ meets segment $PC$ at a point $R$ other than $P$. Prove that the three lines $CD$, $AQ$, and $BR$ are concurrent. Here $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$.
