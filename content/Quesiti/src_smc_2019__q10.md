*Find n in {10,19,20,21,30} such that neither 6n-1 nor 6n+1 is prime*

> Which of the following five values of $n$ is a counterexample to the statement in the box below?
> 
> \emph{For a positive integer $n$, at least one of $6n - 1$ and $6n + 1$ is prime.}
>
> - **(A)** 10
> - **(B)** 19
> - **(C)** 20
> - **(D)** 21
> - **(E)** 30
