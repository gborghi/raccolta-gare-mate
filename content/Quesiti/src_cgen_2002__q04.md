*Quatrieme partie: 3D construction of pseudo-rectangle triangles giving the surface x^2+y^2=z^2+1, and integer points on it.*

> This part takes place in space referred to a direct orthonormal frame $(O;\vec{u},\vec{v},\vec{w})$ associated with coordinates $(x,y,z)$.
> 
> In the plane of equation $z = 0$, let $(C)$ be the circle of center $O$ and radius $1$, and let $T$ and $P$ be two distinct points such that the line $(TP)$ is tangent to the circle $(C)$ at $T$. Let $B$ and $C$ be the intersections of the line $(OP)$ with the circle $(C)$, and let $(D)$ be the line perpendicular to the plane of equation $z = 0$ passing through $P$.
> 
> **1.**
>   a. Show that there exist two points $A$ and $A'$ belonging to the line $(D)$ such that the triangles $ABC$ and $A'BC$ are pseudo-rectangle respectively at $A$ and $A'$; give a simple construction of these two points.
>   b. Show that the coordinates of these two points satisfy the equality $x^2 + y^2 = z^2 + 1$.
> 
> **2.** Let $(H)$ be the set of the points $A$ and $A'$ as $T$ and $P$ vary.
>   a. What is the intersection of the set $(H)$ with a plane orthogonal to $\vec{w}$?
>   b. What is the intersection of the set $(H)$ with a plane containing the line $(O;\vec{w})$?
>   c. Show that the set $(H)$ is contained in a union of lines that one will specify.
> 
> **3.** This last question deals with the integer points of the set $(H)$, that is, the elements of $(H)$ whose three coordinates are integers.
>   a. Let $(x,y,z)$ be the triple of coordinates of such a point. Show that $x$ or $y$ is odd. From now on, denote by $\mathcal{S}$ the set of triples $(x,y,z)$ of strictly positive natural integers such that $x$ is odd and $x^2 + y^2 = z^2 + 1$.
>   b. Let $d$ be a fixed strictly positive integer. Prove that the set of elements $(x,y,z)$ of $\mathcal{S}$ such that $\gcd(x+1, y+z) = d$ is empty if $d$ is odd, and an infinite set if $d$ is even.
>   c. Let $m$ be an odd natural integer greater than or equal to $3$. How many elements $(x,y,z)$ of $\mathcal{S}$ are there such that $x = m$? Determine these elements when $m = 3, 5, 7, 9$.
