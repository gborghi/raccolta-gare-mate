*Sequence where a_i - i contains every integer exactly once*

> Matthew writes down a sequence $a_1, a_2, a_3, \ldots$ of positive integers. Each $a_n$ is the smallest positive integer, different from all previous terms in the sequence, such that the mean of the terms $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ is an integer. Prove that the sequence defined by $a_i - i$ for $i = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$ contains every integer exactly once.
