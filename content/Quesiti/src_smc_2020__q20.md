*Find 5^(x+y) given two exponential equations in x and y*

> The real numbers $x$ and $y$ satisfy the equations $4^y = \dfrac{1}{8(\sqrt{2})^{x+2}}$ and $9^x \times 3^y = 3\sqrt{3}$. What is the value of $5^{x+y}$?
>
> - **(A)** $5\sqrt{5}$
> - **(B)** $5$
> - **(C)** $\sqrt{5}$
> - **(D)** $\dfrac{1}{5}$
> - **(E)** $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{5}}$
