*For S = integers of the form a^2+5b^2 with coprime a,b, and a prime p≡3 mod 4: if some multiple of p is in S, prove 2p is in S.*

> Let $S$ be the set of positive integers which can be written in the form $a^2 + 5b^2$ for some co-prime integers $a$ and $b$. Let $p$ be a prime congruent to $3$ modulo $4$. Prove that if some integral multiple of $p$ belongs to $S$, then $2p$ belongs to $S$ as well.
