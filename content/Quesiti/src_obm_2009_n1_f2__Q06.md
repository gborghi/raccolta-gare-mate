# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2009 — Nível 1 — Fase 2 — Quesito 6

*Family of numbers that all share at least one common digit; find the largest possible number of members*

> We say that two or more numbers belong to the same family when they all possess at least one digit in common. For example, the numbers $72$, $32$, $25$ and $22$ belong to the same family, since they all possess the digit $2$, whereas the numbers $123$, $245$ and $568$ do not belong to the same family, since there is no digit that appears in all three of these numbers. Determine the largest quantity of members of a family whose elements have at least one digit in common.
