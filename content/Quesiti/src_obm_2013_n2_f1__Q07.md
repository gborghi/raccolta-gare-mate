# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2013 — Nível 2 — Fase 1 — Quesito 7

*Divisibility-by-7 trick: count multiples via doubling last digit; find concatenated number*

> Dalvenibon (another alias of student $D$) found a friend to teach him a trick to check divisibility by 7: erase the units digit of the number and subtract twice that digit from the remaining number. For example, for 1001: remove the last digit 1, and compute $100 - 2\cdot 1 = 98$, which is a multiple of 7. Repeating, $9 - 2\cdot 8 = 9 - 16 = -7$, a multiple of 7. This confirms 1001 is a multiple of 7. Similarly 98: $9 - 2\cdot 8 = -7$, a multiple of 7. Using this method to list all positive multiples of 7 less than 100 in increasing order and concatenating them, what is the resulting number?
> 
> (A) 102112 \quad (B) 270280 \quad (C) 833823 \quad (D) 929925 \quad (E) 923823
