*For a prime p, prove p divides some n^2-n+3 iff p divides some m^2-m+25.*

> Let $p$ be a prime number. Prove that there exists $n \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that $p \mid n^2 - n + 3$ if and only if there exists $m \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that $p \mid m^2 - m + 25$.
