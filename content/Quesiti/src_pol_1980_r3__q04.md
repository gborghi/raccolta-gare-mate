*Decompose any three-variable polynomial into symmetric and antisymmetric parts*

> Show that for every polynomial $W$ in three variables there exist polynomials $U$ and $V$ such that:
> $$W(x, y, z) = U(x, y, z) + V(x, y, z),$$
> $$U(x, y, z) = U(y, x, z),$$
> $$V(x, y, z) = -V(y, x, z).$$
