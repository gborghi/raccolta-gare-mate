# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2003 — Nível 3 — Fase 1 — Quesito 22

*Count true statements about triangle T and derived triangles T' and sqrt(T)*

> Let $T = (a, b, c)$ be a triangle, where $a$, $b$, $c$ are the side lengths. Define $T' = (a^2, b^2, c^2)$ and $\sqrt{T} = (\sqrt{a}, \sqrt{b}, \sqrt{c})$, and let $\tilde{T} = $ the square root of $T$. Consider the following statements:
> 
> 1. $T'$ is always a triangle.
> 2. If $T$ is equilateral, then $T'$ is equilateral.
> 3. $T'$ is a right triangle if and only if $T$ is a right triangle.
> 4. $\sqrt{T}$ is a triangle for every triangle $T$.
> 5. All angles of $\sqrt{T}$ are acute.
> 
> The number of true statements is:
> 
> A) $1$ \quad B) $2$ \quad C) $3$ \quad D) $4$ \quad E) $5$
