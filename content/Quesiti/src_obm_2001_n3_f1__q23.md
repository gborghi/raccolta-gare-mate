*f:Z to Z, f(x)=x/10 if 10 divides x else x+1; a0=2001, smallest n with a_n=1*

> Let $f$ be a function from $\mathbb{Z}$ to $\mathbb{Z}$ defined by $f(x) = x/10$ if $x$ is divisible by 10 and $f(x) = x + 1$ otherwise. If $a_0 = 2001$ and $a_{n+1} = f(a_n)$, what is the smallest value of $n$ for which $a_n = 1$?
> 
> **A)** 20 $\quad$ **B)** 38 $\quad$ **C)** 93 $\quad$ **D)** 2000 $\quad$ **E)** $a_n$ is never equal to 1
