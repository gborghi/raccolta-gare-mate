*Two circles tangent to two lines; find TU given PT=1 and a midpoint condition*

> In the plane there are two lines $l$ and $m$ that intersect at point $S$. There is a point $P$ on line $m$ distinct from $S$. Two distinct circles $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are each tangent to line $m$ at point $P$, and are tangent to line $l$ at points $T$ and $U$ respectively. Let $Q$ be the intersection of line $PU$ with $\alpha$ other than $P$. When segment $PT$ passes through the midpoint of segment $SQ$ and the length of segment $PT$ is $1$, find the length of segment $TU$.
