*Prove inequalities for x-intercepts and ratio sums of coordinate sequences*

> In a planar rectangular coordinate system, a sequence of points $\{A_n\}$ on the positive half of the $y$-axis and a sequence of points $\{B_n\}$ on the curve $y = \sqrt{2x}$ ($x \ge 0$) satisfy the condition $|OA_n| = |OB_n| = \dfrac{1}{n}$. The $x$-intercept of line segment $A_nB_n$ is $a_n$, $n \in \mathbb{N}$. Prove that
> 
> (1) $a_n > a_{n+1} > 4$, $n \in \mathbb{N}$;
> 
> (2) There is $n_0 \in \mathbb{N}$ such that for any $n > n_0$,
> $$\frac{b_2}{b_1} + \frac{b_3}{b_2} + \cdots + \frac{b_{n+1}}{b_n} < n - 2\,004,$$
> where $b_n$ is the $y$-coordinate of $B_n$, i.e., $B_n = (b_n,\, \sqrt{2b_n})$ with $b_n > 0$.
