*Sequence defined by recurrence; prove 2^k divides a_n iff 2^k divides a_{2^n}*

> Define a sequence $\langle a_n \rangle_{n \ge 0}$ by $a_0 = 0$, $a_1 = 1$ and
> $$a_n = 2a_{n-1} + a_{n-2}$$
> for $n \ge 2$.
> \begin{itemize}
> \item[(a)] For every $m > 0$ and $0 \le j \le m$, prove that $2a_m$ divides $a_{m+j} + (-1)^j a_{m-j}$.
> \item[(b)] Suppose $2^k$ divides $n$ for some natural numbers $n$ and $k$. Prove that $2^k$ divides $a_n$.
> \end{itemize}
