*n x n grid, 2x2 operation from one black cell; find all n fully colorable*

> Let $n$ be an integer with $n \ge 2$, and consider an $n \times n$ grid of cells. Initially exactly one cell in the top row is colored black, and all other cells are white. The following operation may be repeated: choose a $2 \times 2$ block of cells in which exactly one cell is currently colored black, and color the remaining $3$ cells of that block black. Determine all $n$ for which it is possible, by repeating this operation, to reach a state in which every cell of the grid is black.
