*Count permutations of 1–6 with no initial segment equal to $\{1,\ldots,n\}$*

> Find the number of permutations (arrangements)
> $$p_1, p_2, p_3, p_4, p_5, p_6$$
> of $1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6$ with the property:
> 
> For no integer $n$, $1 \le n \le 5$, do $p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n$ form a permutation of $1, 2, \ldots, n$.
