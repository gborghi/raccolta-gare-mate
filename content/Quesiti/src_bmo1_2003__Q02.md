*Circumcircle, altitude foot, midpoint, and diameter condition*

> The triangle $ABC$, where $AB < AC$, has circumcircle $S$. The perpendicular from $A$ to $BC$ meets $S$ again at $P$. The point $X$ lies on the line segment $AC$, and $BX$ meets $S$ again at $Q$. Show that $BX = CX$ if and only if $PQ$ is a diameter of $S$.
