*Regular n-gon with labeled vertices, parity of positive edges and crossings differ*

> Let $n \ge 4$ be an even number. At the vertices of a regular $n$-gon we write in an arbitrary way $n$ distinct real numbers. Starting from one edge, we name all the edges in a clockwise way by $e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n$. An edge is called \textit{positive} if the difference of the numbers at its endpoint and its start point is positive. A set of two edges $\{e_i, e_j\}$ ($1 \le i < j \le n$) is called \textit{crossing} if $j - i$ is odd, the two edges have no common vertex, and the four vertices of $e_i$ and $e_j$ alternate around the polygon. Prove that the number of crossings between positive edges and the number of positive edges have different parity.
