*Binary strings game: find minimum whiteboard count for B to win*

> Let $n$ and $k$ be positive integers with $n > k$. Players A and B play the following game. Find the smallest positive integer $m$ such that B can always win. (B is told the values of $n$ and $k$ at the start of the game.)
> 
> - A chooses an $n$-digit binary string $X$, and writes on the blackboard all binary strings that can be obtained from $X$ by flipping exactly $k$ digits.
> - B sees the strings on the blackboard, then writes $m$ numbers on the whiteboard.
> - B wins if $X$ is among the numbers written on the whiteboard.
> 
> Here an $n$-digit binary string is a sequence of $n$ symbols each equal to $0$ or $1$. For example, the $3$-digit binary strings are $000, 001, 010, 011, 100, 101, 110, 111$, giving $8$ strings in total.
