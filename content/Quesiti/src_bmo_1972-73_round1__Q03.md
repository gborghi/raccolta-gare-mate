# British Mathematical Olympiad 1972-73 — Round 1 — Quesito 3

*String on hemisphere-cylinder surface; show it does not lie in a plane*

> A curve consisting of the quarter-circle $x^2 + y^2 = r^2$, $x \ge 0$, $y \ge 0$, together with the line segment $z = r$, $-h \le y \le 0$, is rotated about $z = 0$ to form a surface of revolution which is like a hemisphere on a cylinder. A string is stretched tightly over the surface from the point on the curve $(r\sin\alpha, r\cos\alpha)$ to the point $(r, -h)$. Show that the string does not lie in the plane if $\tan\alpha > \dfrac{r}{h}$.
> 
> [You may assume spherical triangle formulae such as $\cos a = \cos b\cos c + \sin b\sin c\cos A$ or $\sin a/\sin A = \sin b/\sin B$. In a spherical triangle the sides $a, b, c$ are arcs of great circles and are measured by the angles they subtend at the centre of the sphere.]
