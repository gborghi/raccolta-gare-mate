*Integer map (x,y)→(x²+y, x−y) with x+y=p²; count distinct treasure locations*

> Long John Silverman has a map on which the point $P$ has integer coordinates $(x, y)$ with $x > 0$, $y > 0$, and $x + y = p^2$ for some prime $p$. On the map the point $(x, y)$ is mapped to the point $(x^2 + y,\, x - y)$ where $x > y > 0$. He has indicated on the map that the three numbers $x + y$, $x^2 + y$, and $x - y$ are all distinct. Find how many different places the treasure may be hidden.
