*Given positive reals whose sum equals their product, and positive b_i with a_i <= b_i, prove the sum of b_i is at most their product.*

> Let $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ be positive real numbers such that $\sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i = \prod_{i=1}^{n} a_i$, and let $b_1, \ldots, b_n$ be positive real numbers such that $a_i \le b_i$ for all $i$. Prove that $\sum_{i=1}^{n} b_i \le \prod_{i=1}^{n} b_i$.
