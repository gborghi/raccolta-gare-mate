# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2025 — Nível Universitário — Fase x — Quesito 4

*Matrices satisfying AB=P(A) imply A invertible and AB=BA*

> Let $A, B \in M(n, \mathbb{C})$, i.e., $A, B$ are $n \times n$ matrices with entries in $\mathbb{C}$, and let $P \in \mathbb{C}[X]$ be a non-constant polynomial such that $P(0) \ne 0$ and $AB = P(A)$. Prove that the matrix $A$ is invertible and that $A$ and $B$ commute (that is, $AB = BA$).
