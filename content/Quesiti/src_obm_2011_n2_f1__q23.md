*Number of ways to arrange 2011 bullets in equal rows leaving 2 over*

> Esmeralda has $2011$ bullets (candies) and wants to arrange them into rows, each row with the same quantity of bullets, in such a way that the number of rows is one more than the quantity of bullets in each row, leaving over $2$ bullets. How many possibilities are there? (See warnings: this statement is partly garbled in the source.)
> 
> (A) $1$ \quad (B) $2$ \quad (C) $5$ \quad (D) $7$ \quad (E) $11$
