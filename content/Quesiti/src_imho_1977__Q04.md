*Four real constants; prove $f(\theta)\ge 0$ implies $A^2+B^2\le 1$*

> Four real constants $a$, $b$, $A$, $B$ are given, and
> $$f(\theta) = 1 - a\cos\theta - b\sin\theta - A\cos 2\theta - B\sin 2\theta.$$
> Prove that if $f(\theta) \ge 0$ for all real $\theta$, then $a^2 + b^2 \le 2$ and $A^2 + B^2 \le 1$.
