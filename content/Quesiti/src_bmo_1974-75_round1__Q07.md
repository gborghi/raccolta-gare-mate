# British Mathematical Olympiad 1974-75 — Round 1 — Quesito 7

*Prove unique real sequence x_1,...,x_n satisfying a sum of squares identity*

> Prove that there is only one set of real numbers $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$ such that
> $$(1-x_1)^2 + (x_1-x_2)^2 + (x_2-x_3)^2 + \cdots + (x_{n-1}-x_n)^2 + x_n^2 = \frac{1}{n+1}.$$
