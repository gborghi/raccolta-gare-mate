*Triangle with circumscribed circle; perpendiculars from D to sides; midpoints perpendicular*

> Let $ABC$ be a triangle. Let $D$ be a point on the circumscribed circle of the triangle, and let $E$ and $F$ be the feet of the perpendiculars from $D$ to $AB$ and $DC$, respectively. Let $M$ be the midpoint of side $BC$. Suppose that $N$ is the midpoint of $EF$. Prove that lines $NA$ and $NM$ are perpendicular.
> 
> (Note: Assume that point $N$ is distinct from point $M$.)
