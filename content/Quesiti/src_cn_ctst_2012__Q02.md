*Existence of k integers satisfying product inequality for all real interpolants*

> Prove that, for any given integer $k \ge 2$, there exist $k$ distinct positive integers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_k$, such that for any real numbers $b_1, b_2, \ldots, b_k$ satisfying $a_i \le b_i \le 2a_i$, $1 \le i \le k$, and any non-negative integers $c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_k$, we have
> $$k \prod_{i=1}^{k} b_i^{c_i} < \prod_{i=1}^{k} b_i,$$
> provided $\prod_{i=1}^{k} b_i^{c_i} < \prod_{i=1}^{k} b_i$.
> (posed by Chen Yonggao)
