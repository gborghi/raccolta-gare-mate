*Escribed circles of triangle; tangents form a triangle T; prove orthocentre result*

> Consider the three escribed circles of the triangle $ABC$, that is, the three distinct circles each of which touches one side of triangle $ABC$ internally but the other two externally. Each pair of escribed circles has just one common internal tangent from a side of the triangle, and the three such common tangents form a triangle $T$. Prove that IS is perpendicular to a side of $T$.
