*Sum of reciprocals of three quadratic values built from roots alpha,beta equals 0.*

> (Part B, Problem 2.) One has the identity $(x-2)(x-3)+(x-3)(x+1)+(x+1)(x-2)=3(x-\alpha)(x-\beta)$, where $\alpha,\beta$ are its roots. Substituting $x=-1,2,3$ gives $(\alpha+1)(\beta+1)=4$, $(\alpha-2)(\beta-2)=-1$ and $(\alpha-3)(\beta-3)=\dfrac{4}{3}$. Compute $\dfrac{1}{(\alpha+1)(\beta+1)}+\dfrac{1}{(\alpha-2)(\beta-2)}+\dfrac{1}{(\alpha-3)(\beta-3)}$. [Source is the solutions sheet; statement reconstructed from the solution, which yields $\frac{1}{4}-1+\frac{3}{4}=0$.]
