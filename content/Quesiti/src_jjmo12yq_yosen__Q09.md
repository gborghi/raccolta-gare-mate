*Min pieces so any start can be reduced to one piece at the center*

> On a $55 \times 55$ grid, one piece is placed on each of $n$ distinct cells. Regardless of the initial placement of the pieces, by repeating the operation below it is possible to reach a state in which a piece is placed on the central cell. Find the smallest value of $n$ for which this is possible.
> Operation: Choose one piece and fix a direction of movement among up, down, left, right. Then, as long as the next cell to move into is neither outside the grid nor a cell already occupied by a piece, repeatedly move the chosen piece one cell at a time in that direction.
