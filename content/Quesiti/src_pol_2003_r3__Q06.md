*For even n, show there exists a permutation of {1,...,n} where each x_{i+1} is one of four expressions in x_i.*

> Let $n$ be an even positive integer. Show that there exists a permutation $(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)$ of the set $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$, such that for each $i \in \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$,
> $$x_{i+1} \text{ is one of the numbers } 2x_i,\ 2x_i - 1,\ 2x_i - n,\ 2x_i - n - 1,$$
> where $x_{n+1} = x_1$.
