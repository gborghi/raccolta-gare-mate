*For a natural number c, define a sequence by a_1=1 and a_{n+1}=d(a_n)+c, where d(m) is the number of positive divisors of m. Show there is k such that a_k, a_{k+1}, ... is periodic.*

> Given a natural number $c$, we define the sequence $(a_n)$ by $a_1=1$ and
> $$a_{n+1}=d(a_n)+c \quad \text{for } n=1,2,\ldots,$$
> where $d(m)$ denotes the number of positive divisors of $m\in\mathbb{N}$. Show that there is a positive integer $k$ such that the sequence $a_k, a_{k+1}, a_{k+2}, \ldots$ is periodic.
