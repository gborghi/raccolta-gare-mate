*Max of good cells minus bad cells in a grid*

> In each cell of a $101\times101$ grid, a real number is written (numbers may repeat).
> 
> - If the real number written in a cell is greater than both the real number in the cell one to its right and the real number in the cell one above it, that cell is called a good cell. If there is no cell to its right or above, it is not a good cell.
> - If the real number written in a cell is smaller than both the real number in the cell one to its right and the real number in the cell one above it, that cell is called a bad cell. If there is no cell to its right or above, it is not a bad cell.
> 
> Note that a cell may be a good cell and a bad cell at the same time. What is the largest possible value of (number of good cells) $-$ (number of bad cells)?
