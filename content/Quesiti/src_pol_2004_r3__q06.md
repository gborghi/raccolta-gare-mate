*Largest number of consecutive terms divisible by m in a recursive sequence*

> An integer $m > 1$ is given. The infinite sequence $x_1, x_2, x_3, \ldots$ is defined by
> $$x_i = \begin{cases} 2^i & \text{for } i < m, \\ x_{i-1} + x_{i-2} + \cdots + x_{i-m+1} & \text{for } i \ge m. \end{cases}$$
> Find the largest natural number $k$ such that there exist $k$ successive terms of this sequence which are divisible by $m$.

*Meno numero di termini consecutivi divisibili per m in una sequenza ricorsiva*
