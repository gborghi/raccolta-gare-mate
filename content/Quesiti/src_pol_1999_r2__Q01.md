*Show no increasing g,h give f=g-h for a function with f(1/n)=(-1)^n.*

> Let $f : (0,1) \to \mathbb{R}$ be a function such that $f(1/n) = (-1)^n$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$. Prove that there are no increasing functions $g, h : (0,1) \to \mathbb{R}$ such that $f = g - h$.
