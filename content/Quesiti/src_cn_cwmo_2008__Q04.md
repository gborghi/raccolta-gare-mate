*Determine boundedness conditions for a sequence defined by x_{n+1}=a x_n^n + b*

> Given an integer $n \geqslant 2$, and two real numbers $a$, $b$ with $a > 0$ and $b \neq 0$, the sequence $\{x_n\}$ is such that $x_1 = b$ and $x_{n+1} = ax_n^n + b$, $n = 1, 2, \ldots$. Prove that:
> (1) When $b < 0$ and $n$ is even, the sequence $\{x_n\}$ is bounded if and only if $ab^{n-1} \geqslant 2$;
> (2) When $b < 0$ and $n$ is odd, or when $b > 0$, the sequence $\{x_n\}$ is bounded if and only if $ab^{n-1} \leqslant \frac{(n-1)^{n-1}}{n^n}$.
> (Posed by Zhu Huawei and Fu Yunhao)
