# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2008 — Quesito 8

*Find class sizes given tulip-planting constraints and counts 52, 64*

> A middle school decided to have students plant tulip bulbs. Each student plants at least one bulb. Students of the same grade plant the same number of bulbs, and each student plants the same number of bulbs as students of other grades do. The total number of bulbs that can be planted is at most $100$; the number of bulbs whose total is less than $100$ is $6$, and the two smallest such numbers are $52$ and $64$.
> 
> Find all possible combinations of the number of students in each of grades 1, 2, and 3 (i.e., middle-school years 1, 2, 3).
