*Two intersecting circles, tangents and a transversal; ratio of radii*

> There are two circles $O_1, O_2$ that intersect at two distinct points $P$ and $Q$. The tangent to circle $O_1$ at $P$ meets circle $O_2$ again at a point $R$ (distinct from $P$), and the tangent to circle $O_2$ at $Q$ meets circle $O_1$ again at a point $S$ (distinct from $Q$). Furthermore, line $PR$ and line $QS$ intersect at a point $X$. Given $XR = 9$ and $XS = 2$, find how many times the radius of $O_2$ is compared with the radius of $O_1$ (i.e. the ratio of the radius of $O_2$ to that of $O_1$). Here, $YZ$ denotes the length of segment $YZ$.
