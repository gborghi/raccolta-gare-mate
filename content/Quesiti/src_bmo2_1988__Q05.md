*Point on segment between two skew lines lies on fixed circle*

> $L$ and $M$ are two skew lines in space, ie they neither meet nor are parallel. $A$, $B$ are the points on $L$, $M$ respectively such that $AB$ is perpendicular to both $L$ and $M$. Points $P$ on $L$, $Q$ on $M$ vary so that
> $$P \neq A, \quad Q \neq B, \quad PQ \text{ is of constant length}.$$
> Show that the centre of the sphere through $A$, $B$, $P$, $Q$ lies on a fixed circle with centre the midpoint of $AB$.
