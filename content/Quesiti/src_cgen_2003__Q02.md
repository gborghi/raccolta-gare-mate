*First part: determine orthocentric sets with 3 and 4 elements, study the orthocentric set generated by 4 concyclic points, and find the orthocentre set of a circle and of a disc.*

> Premiere partie.
> 
> 1. Determine the orthocentric subsets with 3 elements.
> 
> 2. Determine the orthocentric subsets with 4 elements.
> 
> 3. Let $X$ be a set of four points on a circle and $Y=\mathcal{H}(X)$.
>    a. Show that $Y$ is obtained from $X$ by a simple transformation.
>    b. Determine $\mathcal{H}(Y)$.
> 
> 4.
>    a. Let $\Gamma$ be a circle of strictly positive radius; determine $\mathcal{H}(\Gamma)$.
>    b. Let $D$ be a disc of strictly positive radius; determine $\mathcal{H}(D)$.
