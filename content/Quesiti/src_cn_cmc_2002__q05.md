*Count order-preserving surjections between two 50-element sets*

> If there are two sets of real numbers $A = \{a_1, a_2, \cdots, a_{50}\}$ and $B = \{b_1, b_2, \cdots, b_{50}\}$. If there is a mapping $f$ from $A$ to $B$ such that every element in $B$ has an inverse image and $f(a_1) \le f(a_2) \le \cdots \le f(a_{50})$, then the number of such mappings is \quad (\quad).
> 
> (A) $C_{50}^1$ \qquad (B) $C_{50}^2$ \qquad (C) $C_{50}^3$ \qquad (D) $C_{50}^{25}$
