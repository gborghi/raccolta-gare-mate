# British Mathematical Olympiad 1989-90 — Round 1 — Quesito 5

*Diagonals of convex quadrilateral meet at O; prove PQ perpendicular to RS*

> The diagonals of a convex quadrilateral $ABCD$ intersect at $O$. The centroids of triangles $AOB$ and $BOC$ are $P$ and $Q$; the orthocentres of triangles $AOB$ and $COD$ are $R$ and $S$.
> 
> Prove that $PQ$ is perpendicular to $RS$.
> 
> [The centroid is the intersection of the lines from each vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side; the orthocentre is the intersection of the altitudes.]
