# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2025 — Nível 2 — Fase x — Quesito 4

*Collinearity DEF and segment equality ID=IF using incenter and A-excenter*

> Let $ABC$ be a triangle with incenter $I$ and $A$-excenter (the excenter opposite to vertex $A$) equal to $K$. Let $D$ be the foot of the internal bisector from vertex $A$, let $E$ be the circumcenter of triangle $BDK$, and let $F$ be the second intersection of $AC$ with the circumscribed circle of triangle $BEC$.
> 
> (a) Prove that the points $D$, $E$, $F$ are collinear.
> 
> (b) Prove that $ID = IF$.
> 
> \textit{Recall:}
> \begin{itemize}
> \item The incenter of $ABC$ is the intersection of the internal bisectors of $\angle A$, $\angle B$, and $\angle C$.
> \item The $A$-excenter of $ABC$ is the intersection of the internal bisector of $\angle A$ with the external bisectors of $\angle B$ and $\angle C$.
> \end{itemize}
