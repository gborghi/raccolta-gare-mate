*Partition integers 1–10 into pairs with five distinct prime sums; extend to 1–20*

> a) Is it possible to partition the integers $1, 2, 3, \ldots, 10$ into five pairs such that the five pairwise sums are five different prime numbers?
> 
> b) Is it possible to partition the integers $1, 2, 3, \ldots, 20$ into ten pairs such that the ten pairwise sums are ten different prime numbers?
