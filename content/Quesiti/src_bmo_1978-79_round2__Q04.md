*Prove binary-expansion alternating sum equals $(-1)^n n!$*

> Let $b(k)$ denote the number of $1$'s in the binary expansion of the non-negative integer $k$. For example $b(13) = 3$ since $13$ is $1101$ in binary notation. Prove that for all positive integers $n$,
> $$\sum_{k=0}^{2^n - 1} (-1)^{b(k)} k^n = (-1)^n (n!).$$
