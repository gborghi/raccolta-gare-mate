*Maximum number of regions of a circle divided by 2n+1 radii and one line*

> A circle is divided by $2n + 1$ radii into $2n + 1$ congruent sectors. The maximum number of regions of the circle determined by these radii and by one straight line is:
> 
> (A) $3n$ \quad (B) $3n+1$ \quad (C) $3n+2$ \quad (D) $3n+3$ \quad (E) $4n$
