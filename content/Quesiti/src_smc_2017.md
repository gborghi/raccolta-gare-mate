- [[src_smc_2017__Q01|Quesito 1]] — Which of 2017-2, 2017-1, 2017, 2017+1, 2017+2 is prime?
- [[src_smc_2017__Q02|Quesito 2]] — Earthworm Dave: 40 cm long, 26 g mass. Find mass per unit length in g/cm.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q03|Quesito 3]] — Five integers 2,5,6,9,14 rearranged so sum of first three equals sum of last three; find the middle number.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q04|Quesito 4]] — Which expression equals 2017 - 1/2017?
- [[src_smc_2017__Q05|Quesito 5]] — One light-year is ~6×10^12 miles; a galaxy is 13.4 thousand million light-years away. Roughly how many miles away is the galaxy?
- [[src_smc_2017__Q06|Quesito 6]] — Circles in a diagram connected by line segments; find minimum number of colours needed so no two adjacent circles share a colour.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q07|Quesito 7]] — Find k such that sqrt(2)+sqrt(8)+sqrt(18)=sqrt(k).
- [[src_smc_2017__Q08|Quesito 8]] — Which of 1^{-1}, 4^{-1/2}, 6^0, 8^{2/3}, 16^{3/4} is not an integer?
- [[src_smc_2017__Q09|Quesito 9]] — An n×(n+1) rectangle is tiled by k×(k+1) rectangles for k=1 to 8; find n.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q10|Quesito 10]] — Rectangle divided into three congruent smaller rectangles each similar to the large one; find ratio of longer to shorter side.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q11|Quesito 11]] — Two teenagers: difference of squares of ages = 4 times sum of ages; sum = 8 times difference. Find age of older.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q12|Quesito 12]] — Square and regular decagon share an edge; one side of square extended meets extended edge of decagon; find angle x.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q13|Quesito 13]] — Logic puzzle with four people: Isobel, Josh, Genotan, Tegan; exactly one is guilty and the other three tell the truth. Who is guilty?
- [[src_smc_2017__Q14|Quesito 14]] — In a diagram, all marked angles (dots) equal x degrees; find x.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q15|Quesito 15]] — Square PQRS with PT=1, QU=2, RV=3, SW=4 where T,U,V,W lie on edges; TUVW form a quadrilateral with area half that of PQRS. Find PQ.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q16|Quesito 16]] — Two right-angled triangles inside a square; perpendicular sides of larger triangle are 15 and 20; find area of shaded quadrilateral.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q17|Quesito 17]] — Amy, Beth and Claire share sweets by each giving 1/3 to the next; all end up equal. Find Claire's original count.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q18|Quesito 18]] — Arithmetic mean A and geometric mean G of positive x>y satisfy A:G=5:4. Find x:y.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q19|Quesito 19]] — Circle radius 1 touches three sides of 2×4 rectangle; diagonal intersects circle at P and Q. Find length of chord PQ.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q20|Quesito 20]] — Square PQRS edge length 1; four quarter-circle arcs (TRU centred S, VPW centred Q); find perimeter of shaded region.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q21|Quesito 21]] — How many pairs (x,y) of positive integers satisfy 4^x = y^2 + 15?
- [[src_smc_2017__Q22|Quesito 22]] — Regular octagon and inscribed square formed by four diagonals; square edges have length 1. Find area of octagon.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q23|Quesito 23]] — Parabola y=x^2 reflected in line y=x+2; find equation of reflected parabola.
- [[src_smc_2017__Q24|Quesito 24]] — Set of n straight lines in the plane where each line meets exactly 10 others; which value cannot be n?
- [[src_smc_2017__Q25|Quesito 25]] — Regular nonagon N; at each vertex a perpendicular line segment produces smaller nonagon S (shaded). Find area(S)/area(N).
