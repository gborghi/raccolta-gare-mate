*Count valid A/V colorings of a board with no two equal in adjacent row/column cells.*

> (Part B, Problem 4.) Each cell of an $n\times n$ board is to be colored with one of the letters $A$ or $V$, so that no two horizontally or vertically consecutive cells carry the same letter (each row and each column is an alternating sequence). Once the first row is fixed, the rest of the board is determined: wherever two consecutive equal letters appear in a row, the two cells immediately below them are forced to the other letter, and so on. Determine the number of valid colorings. [Source is the solutions sheet; statement reconstructed from the solution. The general count is $2^{n+1}-2$ in one case plus $2^{n+1}$ in the other; for the specific board of the problem the answer is $30+32=62$.]
