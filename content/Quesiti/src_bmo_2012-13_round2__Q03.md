# British Mathematical Olympiad 2012-13 — Round 2 — Quesito 3

*Prove n+s has more 0s than 1s in binary, for 2013-digit integers with more 0s*

> Consider the set of positive integers which, when written in binary, have exactly $2013$ digits and more $0$s than $1$s. Let $n$ be the number of such integers and let $s$ be their sum. Prove that, when written in binary, $n + s$ has more $0$s than $1$s.
