*Triangle with concyclic quadrilateral; prove four associated points are concyclic*

> Let $\triangle ABC$ be a triangle with $AB \ne AC$. Let $D$ and $E$ be points on sides $AB$ and $AC$ respectively (not including the endpoints) such that the four points $B$, $C$, $D$, $E$ lie on a common circle. Let $F$ be the intersection of line $DE$ and line $BC$. Let $G$ be the intersection of line $AF$ with the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ other than $A$. Let $H$ be a point on segment $DE$, and let $I$ be the intersection of line $AH$ with the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ other than $A$. Prove that the four points $F$, $G$, $H$, $I$ are concyclic. (Here $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$.)
