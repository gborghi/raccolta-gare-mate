*Zigzag chain of equal segments between two lines through A; angle B1AB2=1 degree, max n*

> In the figure below, $AB_1 = B_1 B_2 = B_2 B_3 = \cdots = B_{n-1} B_n$. The points $B_1, B_3, B_5, \ldots$ belong to one straight line and the points $B_2, B_4, B_6, \ldots$ belong to another straight line. All points $B_1, B_2, B_3, \ldots, B_n$ are distinct. Knowing that the angle $\angle B_1 A B_2$ measures $1^\circ$, what is the largest possible value of $n$?
> 
> (A) $6$ \quad (B) $7$ \quad (C) $10$ \quad (D) $90$ \quad (E) $n$ can be arbitrarily large

Sapendo che l'angolo $\angle B_1 A B_2$ misura $1^\circ$, quale è il più grande valore possibile di $n$?

> (A) $6$ \quad (B) $7$ \quad (C) $10$ \quad (D) $90$ \quad (E) $n$ può essere arbitrariamente grande
