# British Mathematical Olympiad 1981-82 — Round 2 — Quesito 2

*Sequence of primes; prove 5 never appears*

> The sequence $p_1, p_2, \ldots$ is defined as follows:
> 
> $p_1 = 2$, and for $n \geq 2$, $p_n$ is the largest prime divisor of
> $$p_1 p_2 \cdots p_{n-1} + 1.$$
> 
> Prove that $5$ is not a member of this sequence.
