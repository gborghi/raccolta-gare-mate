*Prove every positive integer n has a chapa with exactly n digits*

> We say that a positive integer is a **chapa** if it is formed only by non-zero digits and the sum of the squares of its digits is also a perfect square. For example, 221 is a chapa because $2^2 + 2^2 + 1^2 = 9 = 3^2$ and all its digits are non-zero; 403 is not a chapa because $4^2 + 0^2 + 3^2 = 5^2$ but one of its digits is zero; and 5 is not a chapa because $1^2 + 2^2 = 5$ is not a perfect square (note: if all digits of 403 are non-zero then $1^2 + 2^2 = 5$ is not a perfect square).
> 
> Prove that for every positive integer $n$, there exists a chapa with exactly $n$ digits.
