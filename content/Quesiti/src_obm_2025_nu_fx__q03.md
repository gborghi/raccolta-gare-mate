*Minimum-modulus elements under invertible linear maps on R^2*

> Given a nonempty set $X \subset \mathbb{R}^k$, $k \in \{2, 3\}$, we say that $q$ is of **minimum modulus** in $X$ if $q \in X$ and $|q| \le |p|$ for all $p \in X$.
> 
> (a) Let $A : \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be an invertible linear transformation, and let
> $$\Lambda = A(\mathbb{Z}^2) = \{A(x,y) \mid x, y \in \mathbb{Z}\}.$$
> Suppose $u$ is of minimum modulus in $\Lambda \setminus \{(0,0)\}$ and $v$ is of minimum modulus in $\Lambda \setminus \{tu \mid t \in \mathbb{R}\}$. Prove that $\Lambda = \{ku + mv \mid k, m \in \mathbb{Z}\}$.
> 
> (b) Let $B : \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be an invertible linear transformation, and let
> $$\Gamma = B(\mathbb{Z}^3) = \{B(x,y,z) \mid x, y, z \in \mathbb{Z}\}.$$
> Suppose $u$ is of minimum modulus in $\Gamma \setminus \{(0,0)\}$, $v$ is of minimum modulus in $\Gamma \setminus \{tu \mid t \in \mathbb{R}\}$, and $w$ is of minimum modulus in $\Gamma \setminus \{tu + sv \mid t, s \in \mathbb{R}\}$. Prove that $\Gamma = \{ku + mv + nw \mid k, m, n \in \mathbb{Z}\}$.
