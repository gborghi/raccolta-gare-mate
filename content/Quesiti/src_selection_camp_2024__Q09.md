*Three permutations of 1..n approximating 2 sqrt(n) by sums of square roots*

> Let $n$ be a positive integer. Prove that there exist permutations $(a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n)$, $(b_1, b_2, \ldots, b_n)$, $(c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_n)$ of $(1, 2, \ldots, n)$ such that for every integer $k$ with $1 \le k \le n$,
> $$\left| \sqrt{a_k} + \sqrt{b_k} + \sqrt{c_k} - 2\sqrt{n} \right| < 2023.$$
> Here $(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)$ is a permutation of $(1, 2, \ldots, n)$ if each integer from $1$ to $n$ appears exactly once among $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$.
