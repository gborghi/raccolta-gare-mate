*Perimeter of a rectangle formed by rearranging pieces cut from a unit square*

> A square with perimeter 4 cm can be cut into two congruent right-angled triangles and two congruent trapezoids as shown in the first diagram in such a way that the four pieces can be rearranged to form the rectangle shown in the second diagram. What is the perimeter, in centimetres, of this rectangle?
>
> - **(A)** $2\sqrt{5}$
> - **(B)** $4\sqrt{2}$
> - **(C)** $5$
> - **(D)** $4\sqrt{3}$
> - **(E)** $3\sqrt{7}$
