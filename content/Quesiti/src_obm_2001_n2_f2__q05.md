*Interchangeable sets of 4 distinct nonzero digits; find all*

> We say that a set $A$ formed by $4$ distinct and nonzero digits is **interchangeable** if we can form pairs of numbers from the digits of $A$, each pair using $2$ digits of $A$, in such a way that in each pair the product of the two numbers formed is always the same. For example, the set $\{1,2,3,6\}$ is interchangeable because $21 \cdot 36 = 12 \cdot 63$. Find all interchangeable sets.
