*Cubic with three real roots; minimal absolute value root bounds ratio b/a*

> Let $a$ and $b$ be positive numbers for which the cubic equation $x^3 - ax + b = 0$ has three (not necessarily distinct) real roots. If $\alpha$ is the one with minimal absolute value, prove that
> $$\frac{b}{a} < \alpha < \frac{3b}{2a}.$$
