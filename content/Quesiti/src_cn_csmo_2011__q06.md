*Prove sum of distances from n points to any point on segment AB does not exceed max of sums to endpoints*

> Given $n$ points $P_1, P_2, \ldots, P_n$ on a plane, let $M$ be any point on segment $AB$ on the plane. Denote by $|P_i M|$ the distance between $P_i$ and $M$, $i = 1, 2, 3, \ldots, n$. Prove that $$\sum_{i=1}^{n} |P_i M| \leq \max\!\left\{\sum_{i=1}^{n} |P_i A|,\; \sum_{i=1}^{n} |P_i B|\right\}.$$ (posed by Jin Mengwei)
