*Integers x, y with xy = px + qy (p, q constant positive); when y is maximum, find the expression equal to y - x.*

> The numbers $x$, $y$, $p$ and $q$ are all integers. $x$ and $y$ are variable and $p$ and $q$ are constant and positive. The four integers are related by the equation $xy = px + qy$. When $y$ takes its maximum possible value, which expression is equal to $y - x$?
>
> - **(A)** $pq - 1$
> - **(B)** $(p-1)(q-1)$
> - **(C)** $(p+1)(q-1)$
> - **(D)** $(p-1)(q+1)$
> - **(E)** $(p+1)(q+1)$
