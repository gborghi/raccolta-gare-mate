*Length BE for E the intersection of the diagonals of a cyclic quadrilateral*

> Let $ABCD$ be an inscribed quadrilateral (whose vertices belong to a circumference) with $AB = 4$, $BC = 8\sqrt{3}$, $AC = 4\sqrt{13}$ and $AD = 2\sqrt{13}$. Being $E$ the intersection of the diagonals $AC$ and $BD$, the length of the segment $BE$ is:
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{12\sqrt{3}}{7}$ (B) $\dfrac{13\sqrt{3}}{7}$ (C) $2\sqrt{3}$ (D) $\dfrac{15\sqrt{3}}{7}$ (E) $\dfrac{16\sqrt{3}}{7}$
