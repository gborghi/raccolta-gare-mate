*Isosceles triangle PQR; tangent to circumcircle at Q meets extension of PR at X; find RX*

> In the isosceles triangle $PQR$, with $PQ = PR = 3$ and $QR = 2$, the tangent to its circumscribed circle at the point $Q$ meets the prolongation of the side $PR$ at $X$. The value of $RX$ is:
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{16}{5}$ (B) $\dfrac{12}{5}$ (C) $\dfrac{8}{3}$ (D) $\dfrac{9}{2}$ (E) $\dfrac{9}{4}$
