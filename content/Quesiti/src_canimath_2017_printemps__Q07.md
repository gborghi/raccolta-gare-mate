*Friendship alignment: at most 2 friends yields spacing ≤2017; 3 friends may fail*

> A mathematics camp has exactly one million students; some pairs of students are friends (if $A$ is a friend of $B$, then $B$ is a friend of $A$).
> 
> a) Suppose that each student has at most two friends. Show that it is possible to line up all the students in a row such that between any two friends there are at most $2017$ other students between them.
> 
> b) Suppose now that each student has at most three friends. Show that this is not necessarily possible.
