# IMO 1961 (III International Olympiad) — Quesito 4

*almeno un rapporto <=2 e uno >=2*

> Consider triangle $P_1 P_2 P_3$ and a point $P$ within the triangle. Lines $P_1 P$, $P_2 P$, $P_3 P$ intersect the opposite sides in points $Q_1$, $Q_2$, $Q_3$ respectively. Prove that, of the numbers
> $$\frac{P_1 P}{PQ_1}, \quad \frac{P_2 P}{PQ_2}, \quad \frac{P_3 P}{PQ_3}$$
> at least one is $\leq 2$ and at least one is $\geq 2$.
