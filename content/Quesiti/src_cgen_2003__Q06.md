*Fifth part: an iteration $X_{n+1}=\mathcal{H}(X_n)$ starting from four points in general position, using a similitude result relating the hyperbola $xy=1$ and the degenerate locus $xy=0$; show the minimal period $m$ is $1$ or $2$ and characterize the initial sets achieving each.*

> Cinquieme partie.
> 
> Denote by $Y_1$ the hyperbola of equation $xy=1$ and by $Y_0$ the set of equation $xy=0$, that is the union of the axes $(O,\vec{u})$ and $(O,\vec{v})$.
> 
> One admits the following result:
> "Given four points $A$, $B$, $C$ and $D$ of the plane, there exists a similitude $s$ such that $s(A)$, $s(B)$, $s(C)$ and $s(D)$ all belong to $Y_1$, or else all belong to $Y_0$."
> 
> Let $A_0$, $B_0$, $C_0$ and $D_0$ be four points, three by three non-collinear, and let $X_0=\{A_0,B_0,C_0,D_0\}$. One defines by recurrence $X_{n+1}=\mathcal{H}(X_n)$ for every natural integer $n$. One supposes that there exists a strictly positive integer $n$ such that $X_n=X_0$ and one denotes by $m$ the smallest integer having this property.
> 
> 1. Show that $m=1$ or $m=2$.
> 
> 2. Determine the sets $X_0$ such that $m=1$, then those such that $m=2$.
