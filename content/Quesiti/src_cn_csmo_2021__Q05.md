*Arrange 2021 integers on a line/circle with consecutive-43 divisibility; count valid sequences*

> To celebrate the 43rd anniversary of the restoration of mathematical competitions, a math lover arranges the first $2021$ positive integers $1, 2, \ldots, 2021$ into a sequence $\{a_n\}$, such that any $43$ consecutive numbers add up to a multiple of $43$.
> (1) Prove that if the two ends of $\{a_n\}$ are joined to form a circle, then any $43$ consecutive numbers on the circle also add up to a multiple of $43$.
> (2) Determine the number of all such sequences $\{a_n\}$.
