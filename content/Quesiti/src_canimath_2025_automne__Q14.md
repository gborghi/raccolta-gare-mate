*Show triangle DYZ is isosceles in a midpoint/semicircle configuration*

> Let $ABC$ be a triangle that is not isosceles at $A$. Let $D$, $E$ and $F$ be the midpoints of the sides $[BC]$, $[AC]$ and $[AB]$ respectively. The circle of diameter $[BC]$ cuts the half-lines $[DE)$ and $[DF)$ at $P$ and $Q$ respectively. The lines $(BP)$ and $(CQ)$ cut each other at $X$. The line $(AX)$ cuts the lines $(DE)$ and $(DF)$ at $Y$ and $Z$ respectively. Show that the triangle $DYZ$ is isosceles.
