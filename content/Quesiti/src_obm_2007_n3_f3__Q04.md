*Game on a 2007x2007 board: Arnaldo removes 2x2 blocks, Bernaldo removes single squares; can Bernaldo always win?*

> $2007^2$ equal little squares are arranged forming a $2007 \times 2007$ board. Arnaldo and Bernaldo play the following game: each of Arnaldo's moves consists of removing 4 little squares that form a $2 \times 2$ square. Each of Bernaldo's moves consists of removing only 1 little square. The players play alternately, with Arnaldo being the first to play. When Arnaldo can no longer make his move, Bernaldo takes for himself all the little squares remaining on the board at the end. The one who has more little squares wins. Is it possible for Bernaldo to win, no matter how Arnaldo plays?
