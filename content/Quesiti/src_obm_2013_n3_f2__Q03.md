*Evaluate sum involving 1/(n(n+1)) to find A+B*

> Observe that
> $$\frac{1}{n(n+1)} = \frac{1}{n} - \frac{1}{n+1}.$$
> Thus we can compute the series
> $$\sum_{n=2}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n(n+1)} = \frac{1}{1 \cdot 2} + \frac{1}{2 \cdot 3} + \frac{1}{3 \cdot 4} + \cdots = \left(1 - \frac{1}{2}\right) + \left(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3}\right) + \left(\frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{4}\right) + \cdots = 1.$$
> Knowing that $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = 1 + \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{9} + \cdots = \frac{\pi^2}{6}$, the value of
> $$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n(n+1)^2} = \frac{1}{1 \cdot 2^2} + \frac{1}{2 \cdot 3^2} + \frac{1}{3 \cdot 4^2} + \cdots$$
> is of the form $A - \dfrac{\pi^2}{B}$, where $A$ and $B$ are positive integers. Determine the value of $A + B$.
