*Infinite sequence of positive integers: prove a_m = a_{m+1} for some M*

> Let $a_1, a_2, \ldots$ be an infinite sequence of positive integers. Suppose that there is an integer $N > 1$ such that, for each $n \ge N$, the number
> $$\frac{a_1}{a_2} + \frac{a_2}{a_3} + \cdots + \frac{a_{n-1}}{a_n} + \frac{a_n}{a_1}$$
> is an integer. Prove that there is a positive integer $M$ such that $a_m = a_{m+1}$ for all $m \ge M$.
