# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2006 — Nível Universitário — Fase 2 — Quesito 2

*Count 2x2 integer matrices with determinant kn+1 mod n*

> Prove that, for every integer $n \ge 2$, the number of $2 \times 2$ matrices with integer entries belonging to the set $\{0, 1, 2, \ldots, n-1\}$ whose determinant has the form $kn + 1$ for some integer $k$ is equal to
> $$n^3 \cdot \prod_{\substack{p \text{ prime} \\ p \mid n}} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right).$$
