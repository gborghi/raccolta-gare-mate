*Pair 0..13 into 7 pairs so the product of the pair-sums is a perfect square*

> Rémi distributes the integers $0, 1, \ldots, 13$ into seven pairwise disjoint pairs of integers. For each pair, he writes on the board the sum of its two elements. Prove that Rémi can proceed in such a way that the product of the seven numbers written on the board is a perfect square.
> 
> *A perfect square is an integer that can be written in the form $n^2$, where $n$ is an integer.*
