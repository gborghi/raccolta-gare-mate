*Points divide each side of a square into n equal parts; joined to form small squares and triangles. How many small squares when n=7?*

> Points are drawn on the sides of a square, dividing each side into $n$ equal parts (so, in the example shown, $n = 4$). The points are joined in the manner indicated, to form several small squares (24 in the example shown) and some triangles. How many small squares are formed when $n = 7$?
>
> - **(A)** 56
> - **(B)** 84
> - **(C)** 140
> - **(D)** 840
> - **(E)** 5040
