# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2005 — Nível 3 — Fase 1 — Quesito 14

*Numbers 1..12 around a circle with neighbor sums triangular; find number opposite 12*

> Esmeralda adores the triangular numbers (i.e., the numbers $1$, $3$, $6$, $10$, $15$, $21$, $28$, ...), so much so that she decided to place the numbers $1$, $2$, $3$, ..., $12$ around a circle in such a way that the sum of each pair of neighboring numbers is a triangular number. She left $12$ out at first. The number opposite to $12$ in her arrangement (the original number) is:
> (A) $1$ (B) $4$ (C) $3$ (D) $6$ (E) $10$
