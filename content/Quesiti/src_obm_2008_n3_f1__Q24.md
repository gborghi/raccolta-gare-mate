# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2008 — Nível 3 — Fase 1 — Quesito 24

*10 people of different heights in a line, decreasing from the tallest to both ends; count arrangements*

> Consider $10$ people, all of different heights, who must stand in a line in such a way that, starting from the tallest person, the heights must decrease toward both sides of the line (if the tallest person is the first or the last of the line, all the people from her onward must be in decreasing order of height). Obeying these conditions, in how many ways can these people stand in a line?
> 
> (A) $256$ (B) $768$ (C) $1260$ (D) $512$ (E) $2560$
