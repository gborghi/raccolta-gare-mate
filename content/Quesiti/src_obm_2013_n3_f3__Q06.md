*Incircle of ABC touches sides; reflections of intersection point lie on line through incenter and circumcenter*

> The incircle of triangle $ABC$ touches sides $BC$, $CA$, and $AB$ at points $D$, $E$, and $F$ respectively. Let $P$ be the intersection point of lines $AD$ and $BE$. The reflections of $P$ with respect to lines $EF$, $FD$, and $DE$ are $X$, $Y$, and $Z$ respectively. Prove that lines $AX$, $BY$, and $CZ$ have a common point lying on the line $IO$, where $I$ is the incenter and $O$ is the circumcenter of triangle $ABC$.
