- [[src_smc_2022__Q01|Quesito 1]] — Simplify a product-of-differences expression involving fifth powers and factorial-style denominators.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q02|Quesito 2]] — Find the smallest prime that is the sum of five different primes.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q03|Quesito 3]] — Count the number of parallelograms in a figure of a regular hexagon divided into triangles.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q04|Quesito 4]] — Two symmetrically placed squares of sides 2 and 5; find the ratio of the area of the small square to that of the shaded region.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q05|Quesito 5]] — Evaluate the sum of five reciprocals 1/1.01 + 1/1.1 + 1/1 + 1/11 + 1/101.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q06|Quesito 6]] — Evaluate the expression 4^800 / 8^400.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q07|Quesito 7]] — Find the minimum total number of stamps when buying first-class (85p) and second-class (66p) stamps, one of each type at a time, so the total cost is an exact integer number of pounds.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q08|Quesito 8]] — Regular outer hexagon of area 216 with an inner pattern; find the area of the shaded region.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q09|Quesito 9]] — Calculate the distance light travels in one nanosecond at speed 3×10^8 m/s.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q10|Quesito 10]] — Find the value of x satisfying (1+2x+3x^2)/(3+2x+x^2) = 3.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q11|Quesito 11]] — In a number triangle where each disc equals the product of the two discs below it and the top disc is 2022, find n.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q12|Quesito 12]] — Find the sum of the digits of the integer equal to 6666666^2 - 3333333^2.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q13|Quesito 13]] — Three rugs (combined area 90 m^2) cover a 60 m^2 floor; exactly two layers cover 12 m^2; find the area covered by exactly three layers.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q14|Quesito 14]] — Square KLMN contains nested squares PQRS and TUVW, each obtained by dividing the previous side in ratio 1:2; find the shaded fraction.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q15|Quesito 15]] — Hare and tortoise race 100 m; when hare finishes tortoise is 75 m behind; hare turns back; how far from finish do they meet?
- [[src_smc_2022__Q16|Quesito 16]] — Identify which of five graphs could be a sketch of the curve sqrt(x) + sqrt(y) = 1.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q17|Quesito 17]] — Regular octagon of side 1 with four corner equilateral triangles (side 1) removed; find the area of the resulting shape.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q18|Quesito 18]] — Given two simultaneous equations in 3^x and 3^y, find the value of 3^x + 3^y.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q19|Quesito 19]] — Count pairs of real numbers satisfying x^2 - y = 2022 and y^2 - x = 2022 simultaneously.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q20|Quesito 20]] — Find the area of a square inscribed inside a quadrant of a circle of radius 10.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q21|Quesito 21]] — Logo with two straight edges of length 2, two small semicircles (diameter 2) and two large semicircles; find the shaded area.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q22|Quesito 22]] — Count pairs of integers (x, y) satisfying sqrt(x - sqrt(x+23)) = 2*sqrt(2) - y.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q23|Quesito 23]] — Three squares (areas 10, 10, 40) with vertices on sides of triangle FIL; find the area of triangle FIL.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q24|Quesito 24]] — Integers x, y with xy = px + qy (p, q constant positive); when y is maximum, find the expression equal to y - x.
- [[src_smc_2022__Q25|Quesito 25]] — Drinks carton (pyramid from four congruent triangles, QP = RS = 4 cm, PR = 10 cm); find the volume in cm^3.
