*Type-W triangles (perpendicular medians from A and B): geometric loci, then integer-sided solutions and prime divisors of u^2-uv-v^2*

> Throughout this exercise, given a non-degenerate triangle $ABC$, one denotes by $a$, $b$, $c$ the lengths of the sides $BC$, $CA$, $AB$ respectively. One says that a triangle is of type $\mathscr{W}$ if its medians issued from $A$ and from $B$ are perpendicular.
> 
> ## 3.1 — Geometry
> 
> **1.** Show that there exists a triangle $ABC$ such that one has the relations:
> $$c^2 = \frac{b^2}{2} = \frac{a^2}{3}.$$
> Establish that such a triangle is right-angled at $A$ and that it is of type $\mathscr{W}$.
> 
> **2.** In this question one fixes the points $A$ and $B$ and one considers the set $\Gamma$ of points $C$ such that the triangle $ABC$ is of type $\mathscr{W}$.
> 
> **a.** Determine the set of points $G$, isobarycentres of $A$, $B$, $C$, as $C$ describes $\Gamma$.
> 
> **b.** Deduce the set $\Gamma$.
> 
> **c.** Determine the set of values taken by the ratio $\dfrac{b}{a}$.
> 
> **d.** Determine the set of points $H$, orthocentres of the triangles $ABC$, as $C$ describes $\Gamma$ (one will work in a frame $(O, \vec{i}, \vec{j})$ in which $A$ and $B$ have respective coordinates $(-1, 0)$ and $(1, 0)$, and one will determine a function $f$ such that one deduces that $H$ is the union of the two curves of equations $y = f(x)$ and $y = -f(x)$).
> 
> **3.** In this question one fixes the points $A$ and $C$ and one considers the set $\Gamma'$ of points $B$ such that the triangle $ABC$ is of type $\mathscr{W}$.
> 
> **a.** Determine the set $\Gamma'$.
> 
> **b.** Determine the set of values taken by the ratio $\dfrac{b}{a}$.
> 
> **c.** Determine the triangles $ABC$ of type $\mathscr{W}$ having minimal circumradius.
> 
> **d.** Determine the set of points $H$, orthocentres of the triangles $ABC$, as $B$ describes $\Gamma'$ (one will work in a frame $(O, \vec{i}, \vec{j})$ in which $C$ and $A$ have respective coordinates $(-1, 0)$ and $(-5, 0)$).
> 
> **4. a.** Show that a triangle $ABC$ is of type $\mathscr{W}$ if and only if one has the relation:
> $$(\star) \quad a^2 + b^2 = 5c^2.$$
> 
> **b.** Given strictly positive reals $a$, $b$, $c$ verifying the relation $(\star)$, give a necessary and sufficient condition bearing on the ratio $\dfrac{b}{a}$ for $a$, $b$, $c$ to be the lengths of the sides of a triangle of type $\mathscr{W}$.
> 
> ## 3.2 — Arithmetic
> 
> ### 3.2.1 — Two families of triangles
> 
> In the rest of the exercise, one proposes to search for the triangles of type $\mathscr{W}$ whose sides have integer lengths, beginning by searching for the set $\mathscr{T}$ of triples $(a, b, c)$ of strictly positive natural integers verifying the relation $(\star)$.
> 
> One remarks that for a triple $(a, b, c)$ of strictly positive integers to be an element of $\mathscr{T}$, it suffices that there exists a strictly positive integer $m$ such that the triple $(ma, mb, mc)$ is an element of $\mathscr{T}$. One can thus limit oneself to searching for the set $\mathscr{T}_1$ of elements of $\mathscr{T}$ without common prime factor.
> 
> **1. a.** Show that if $(a, b, c)$ is an element of $\mathscr{T}_1$ then the integers $a$, $b$, $c$ are pairwise coprime.
> 
> **b.** Establish that if $(a, b, c)$ is an element of $\mathscr{T}_1$ then $a$ and $b$ are of different parities.
> 
> **c.** Show that if $(a, b, c)$ is an element of $\mathscr{T}_1$ then $c$ is divisible neither by $3$, nor by $4$, nor by $5$.
> 
> **d.** Let $(a, b, c)$ be an element of $\mathscr{T}_1$. Show that $b^2 - 4a^2$ and $a^2 - 4b^2$ are multiples of $5$. Deduce that there exists a pair of integers $(\alpha, \beta)$ such that one has $\begin{cases} 2a + b = 5\alpha \\ -a + 2b = 5\beta \end{cases}$ or $\begin{cases} 2a - b = 5\alpha \\ a + 2b = 5\beta \end{cases}$. Verify then that one has $\alpha^2 + \beta^2 = c^2$, and that $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are coprime.
> 
> **e.** If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are integers coprime to each other, are the integers $a$ and $b$ that are associated to them by the above relations coprime to each other?
> 
> One now admits the following result: the triples $(x, y, z)$ of strictly positive integers without common prime factor verifying $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$ and such that $y$ is even are given by $x = u^2 - v^2$, $y = 2uv$ and $z = u^2 + v^2$, where $u$ and $v$ are strictly positive integers, coprime, of different parities and such that $u > v$, determined in a unique way.
> 
> **2.** One will say that a triangle is of type $\mathscr{W}_e$ if it is of type $\mathscr{W}$ and if the lengths $a$, $b$ and $c$ of its sides are integers without common prime factor. Show that for every triangle of type $\mathscr{W}_e$ there exist strictly positive integers $u$ and $v$, coprime, of different parities and verifying $u > v$, such that one of the two following relations holds:
> 
> $$(1) \quad (a, b, c) = \bigl( 2(u^2 - uv - v^2),\; u^2 + 4uv - v^2,\; u^2 + v^2 \bigr)$$
> $$(2) \quad (a, b, c) = \bigl( 2(u^2 + uv - v^2),\; -u^2 + 4uv + v^2,\; u^2 + v^2 \bigr)$$
> 
> **3.** Determine the sets of pairs $(u, v)$ of positive integers such that relation $(1)$ (respectively $(2)$) leads to a triangle of type $\mathscr{W}_e$.
> 
> **4.** Establish that a triangle of type $\mathscr{W}_e$ is given by only one of the two relations $(1)$ or $(2)$. One thus classifies the triangles of type $\mathscr{W}_e$ into two disjoint categories, which one will denote $\mathscr{W}_1$ and $\mathscr{W}_2$.
> 
> **5.** Give the lengths of the sides of the triangles of type $\mathscr{W}_e$ whose « small » side $c$ has a length less than or equal to $50$.
> 
> ### 3.2.2 — Integers of the form $u^2 - uv - v^2$ and their divisors
> 
> One proposes to study the prime factors greater than or equal to $7$ of the integers $a$ and $b$, lengths of the sides $BC$ and $CA$ of a triangle of type $\mathscr{W}_e$.
> 
> **1.** One denotes by $\omega$ and $\omega'$ the solutions of the equation $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$. Establish the relation $u^2 - uv - v^2 = (u - \omega v)(u - \omega' v)$. Deduce that the set of integers of the form $u^2 - uv - v^2$, where $u$ and $v$ describe the relative integers, is stable under multiplication. What can be said of the set of integers of the form $u^2 + 4uv - v^2$, where $u$ and $v$ are arbitrary relative integers?
> 
> **2.** Let $p = 2q + 1$ be a prime number strictly greater than $5$ which divides an integer of the form $u^2 - uv - v^2$, where $u$ and $v$ are coprime relative integers.
> 
> **a.** Establish the congruences $(2u - v)^2 \equiv 5v^2$ modulo $p$ and $(u + 2v)^2 \equiv 5u^2$ modulo $p$.
> 
> **b.** Deduce that $5^q \equiv 1$ modulo $p$.
> 
> **c.** Let $j$ be an integer between $1$ and $q$; one denotes by $r_j$ the remainder of the division of $5j$ by $p$. If $r_j \le q$ one sets $f(j) = r_j$ and $\varepsilon(j) = 1$; in the contrary case one sets $f(j) = p - r_j$ and $\varepsilon(j) = -1$, so that one has in all cases $1 \le f(j) \le q$ and $5j \equiv \varepsilon(j) f(j)$ modulo $p$. Show that the integers $f(1), f(2), \ldots, f(q)$ are pairwise distinct, and deduce that the number of integers $j$, between $1$ and $q$ and such that $\varepsilon(j) = -1$, is even.
> 
> **d.** For every real number $x$, one denotes by $\lfloor x \rfloor$ its integer part, namely the greatest relative integer less than or equal to $x$. Show that $\left\lfloor \dfrac{4p}{10} \right\rfloor - \left\lfloor \dfrac{3p}{10} \right\rfloor + \left\lfloor \dfrac{2p}{10} \right\rfloor - \left\lfloor \dfrac{p}{10} \right\rfloor$ is even, then that $p \equiv \pm 1$ modulo $10$.
> 
> **3.** Let $(a, b, c)$ be the lengths of the sides of a triangle of type $\mathscr{W}_e$.
> 
> **a.** Show that all the odd prime factors of $a$ are congruent to $1$ or to $9$ modulo $10$.
> 
> **b.** What can be said of the prime factors of $b$?
