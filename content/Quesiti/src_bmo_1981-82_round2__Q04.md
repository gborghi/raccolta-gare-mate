*Two points on curve x^n - ay^n = b; area of triangle bound*

> $P_1(x_1, y_1)$, $P_2(x_2, y_2)$ are two points on the curve $x^n - ay^n = b$ for which $x > 0$, $y > 0$. Here $a$ and $b$ are positive constants and $n$ is an integer $> 1$. Prove that if $y_1 < y_2$, and $\Delta$ is the area of triangle $OP_1P_2$, then
> $$by_2 > 2ny_1^{n-1} a^{1-\frac{1}{n}} \Delta.$$
