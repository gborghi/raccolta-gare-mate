*Faulty divisibility-by-7 rule (digit sum): how often correct for multiples of 7 under 100*

> Student $D$ (we use this codename to protect the student's identity) did not pay attention in class and did not learn how to check, without performing the division, whether a number is a multiple of $7$ or not. So, $D$ decided to use the following rule: he sums the digits and checks whether the result is a multiple of $7$. For how many positive integers less than $100$ will this incorrect method indicate that a number is a multiple of $7$, the number actually being a multiple of $7$?
> 
> (A) $0$ \quad (B) $1$ \quad (C) $2$ \quad (D) $3$ \quad (E) $4$
