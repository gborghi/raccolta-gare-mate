*Prove one of two Diophantine equations has an odd solution*

> Suppose positive integers $m$, $n$, $k$ satisfy $mn = k^2 + k + 3$. Prove that at least one of the following Diophantine equations
> $$x^2 + 11y^2 = 4m \quad \text{and} \quad x^2 + 11y^2 = 4n$$
> has a solution $(x_0, y_0)$ with $x_0$, $y_0$ being odd numbers.
