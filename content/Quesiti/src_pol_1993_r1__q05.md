*Prove that a line determined by two squares built on a triangle passes through a fixed point.*

> Let $A$ and $C$ be distinct points in the plane. For every point $B$ one constructs squares $ABKL$ and $BCMN$ outside the triangle $ABC$. Prove that the lines $LM$ pass through a fixed point as $B$ varies in the same halfplane determined by $AC$.
