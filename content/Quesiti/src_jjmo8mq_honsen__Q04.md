# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2010 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 4

*Concyclicity of A, incenter I, and two circle points P, Q*

> Consider a triangle $ABC$. A circle $O$ passing through the points $B$ and $C$ meets the segments $AB$ and $AC$ (excluding their endpoints) at points $D$ and $E$ respectively, and $AD + AE = BC$ holds. Let $I$ be the incenter of triangle $ABC$, and let the lines $BI$ and $CI$ meet the circle $O$ at the points $P$ and $Q$ (other than $B$ and $C$) respectively. Show that $A$, $I$, $P$, $Q$ lie on a single circle.
> 
> Here, $XY$ denotes the length of the segment $XY$.
