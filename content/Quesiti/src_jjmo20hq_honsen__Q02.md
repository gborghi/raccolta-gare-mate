*Stones on a circle, make a cell with n-1 stones in n-2 moves*

> Let $n$ be an integer with $n \ge 3$. There are $n$ cells arranged on a circle, and one stone is placed on each cell. The following operation is performed: choose a cell $A$ that contains at least one stone, and an integer $k$ with $1 \le k \le n-1$. Then move all the stones on the cell that is $k$ steps counterclockwise from $A$ to the cell that is $k$ steps clockwise from $A$. Find all $n$ for which it is possible, by performing this operation exactly $n-2$ times appropriately, to obtain a cell containing $n-1$ stones.
