*Total distance travelled by a light ray reflecting between two mirrors at 30 degrees*

> Two mirrors form an angle of $30^\circ$ at point $V$. A ray of light, starting from a point $S$, parallel to one of the mirrors, strikes the other mirror at point $A$, as the figure shows. After a certain number of reflections, the ray returns to $S$. If $SA = AV = 1$ m, what distance, in meters, is travelled by the ray of light?
> 
> **A)** $2$ $\quad$ **B)** $2 + \sqrt{3}$ $\quad$ **C)** $1 + \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3}$ $\quad$ **D)** $\sqrt{2}\,(1 + \sqrt{3})$ $\quad$ **E)** $5\sqrt{3}$
