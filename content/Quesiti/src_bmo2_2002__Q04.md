*N unit spheres arranged so each touches exactly two others; bound tangent length*

> Suppose that $B_1, \ldots, B_N$ are $N$ spheres of unit radius arranged in space so that each sphere touches exactly two others externally. Let $P$ be a point outside all these spheres, and let the $N$ points of contact be $C_1, \ldots, C_N$. The length of the tangent from $P$ to the sphere $B_i$ $(1 \le i \le N)$ is denoted by $t_i$. Prove that the product of the quantities $t_i$ is no more than the square of the distance $PC$.
