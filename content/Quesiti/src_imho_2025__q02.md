*Tangent line through orthocentre in two-circle configuration*

> Let $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ be circles with centres $M$ and $N$, respectively, such that the radius of $\Omega$ is less than the radius of $\Gamma$. Suppose circles $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ intersect at two distinct points $A$ and $B$. Line $MN$ intersects $\Omega$ at $C$ and $D$, such that points $C$, $M$, $N$ and $D$ are on the line in that order. Let $P$ be the circumcentre of triangle $ACD$. Line $AP$ intersects $\Omega$ again at $E \ne A$. Line $AP$ intersects $\Gamma$ again at $F \ne A$. Let $H$ be the orthocentre of triangle $PAB$.
> 
> Prove that the line through $H$ parallel to $AP$ is tangent to the circumcircle of triangle $BEF$.
> 
> (The *orthocentre* of a triangle is the point of intersection of its altitudes.)
