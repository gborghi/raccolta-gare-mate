*Angle of a parallelogram such that perpendiculars to its sides form another parallelogram with equal angles*

> Drawing four lines perpendicular to the sides of a parallelogram, we obtain a region bounded by these four lines which is also a parallelogram. We can state that one of the angles of the original parallelogram, in order for the two parallelograms to have equal angles, is:
> (A) $30^\circ$ (B) $45^\circ$ (C) $60^\circ$ (D) $75^\circ$ (E) $90^\circ$
