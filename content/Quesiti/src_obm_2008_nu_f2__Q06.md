# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2008 — Nível Universitário — Fase 2 — Quesito 6

*Count ordered tuples with product at most n; bound f(n) between two powers*

> For a positive integer $n$, let $f(n)$ be the number of products of integers greater than $1$ whose result is at most $n$; that is, $f(n)$ is the number of $k$-tuples $(a_1,a_2,\ldots,a_k)$ where $k$ is some natural number, $a_1\ge 2$ is an integer, and $a_1\cdot a_2\cdots a_k\le n$ for every $i$ and $a_1\cdot a_2\cdots a_k\le n$ (counting the empty $0$-tuple $()$, whose product is $1$, as one term). So, for example, $f(1)=1$ by the $0$-tuple; $f(6)=9$ by the $0$-tuple $()$, the $1$-tuples $(2),(3),(4),(5),(6)$, the $2$-tuples $(2,2),(2,3)$, and $(3,2)$.
> 
> Let $\alpha>1$ be such that $\displaystyle\sum_{m=2}^{\infty}\frac{1}{m^{\alpha}}=\frac{1}{2}$.
> 
> (a) Prove that there exists a constant $K>0$ such that $f(n)\le K\cdot n^{\alpha}$ for every positive integer $n$.
> 
> (b) Prove that there exists a constant $c>0$ such that $f(n)\ge c\cdot n^{\alpha}$ for every positive integer $n$.
