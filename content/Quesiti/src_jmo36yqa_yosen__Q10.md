*Smallest n for existence of convex 101-gon with grid vertices sharing consecutive coordinates*

> Let $n$ be a positive integer. In the $xy$-plane, call a point **good** if both its $x$-coordinate and $y$-coordinate are integers in $\{0, 1, \ldots, n\}$. We say that a convex $101$-gon $P_1 P_2 \cdots P_{101}$ satisfies the conditions if:
> - Every vertex $P_i$ is a good point.
> - For every $1 \le i \le 101$, consecutive vertices $P_i$ and $P_{i+1}$ share the same $x$-coordinate or the same $y$-coordinate (indices modulo $101$, with $P_{102} = P_1$).
> 
> A convex $101$-gon is a non-self-intersecting $101$-gon all of whose interior angles are less than $180^\circ$. Find the smallest value of $n$ for which such a convex $101$-gon exists.
