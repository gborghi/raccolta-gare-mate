*Two-player game on {0,...,p-1} building a base-10 number: first player has winning strategy mod p*

> Let $p$ be a prime. Two players alternately perform the following action:
> 
> Action: Choose a number $i$ from $\{0, 1, \ldots, p-1\}$ that has not been chosen before, then choose an integer between $0$ and $9$ (inclusive) to assign to $i$.
> 
> The game ends when all elements of $\{0, 1, \ldots, p-1\}$ have been chosen. Let $a_i$ be the integer assigned to $i$, and set $M = a_0 + a_1 \cdot 10 + \cdots + a_{p-1} \cdot 10^{p-1}$. If $M$ is divisible by $p$, the first player wins; otherwise the second player wins. Prove that the first player has a winning strategy.
