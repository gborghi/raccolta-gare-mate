*Area of triangle ABM where M is on AD and BMC is a non-isosceles right triangle; AB=2, CD=4, BC=6.*

> In the drawing alongside, segments $AB$ and $CD$ are perpendicular to segment $BC$. Knowing that point $M$ belongs to segment $AD$ and that triangle $BMC$ is a right (non-isosceles) triangle, what is the area of triangle $ABM$? (From the figure: $AB=2$, $CD=4$, $BC=6$.)
> 
> (A) 1 (B) $\frac{6}{5}$ (C) $\frac{7}{5}$ (D) $\frac{8}{5}$ (E) $\frac{9}{5}$
