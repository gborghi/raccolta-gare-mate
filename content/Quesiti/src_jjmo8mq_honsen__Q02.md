# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2010 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 2

*Largest k so truncating a perfect square keeps squares*

> Find the largest nonnegative integer $k$ for which there exists a positive integer $n$ satisfying the following condition.
> 
> Condition: $n$ is a perfect square with at least $k+1$ digits, and for every positive integer $i$ with $i \le k$, the number obtained from $n$ by deleting its last $i$ digits is also a perfect square.
