*D,E,F on sides BC,CA,AB of acute triangle; circumcircles of AEF, BFD, CDE meet at P; given three ratio conditions prove AD,BE,CF are altitudes.*

> Points $D, E, F$ are taken on the respective sides $BC, CA, AB$ of an acute-angled triangle $ABC$. The circumcircles of the triangles $AEF$, $BFD$, $CDE$ meet at point $P$. Prove that if
> $$\frac{PD}{PE} = \frac{BD}{AE}, \quad \frac{PE}{PF} = \frac{CE}{BF}, \quad \frac{PF}{PD} = \frac{AF}{CD},$$
> then $AD$, $BE$, $CF$ are the altitudes of triangle $ABC$.
