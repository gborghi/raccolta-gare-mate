*greedy doubling sequence is eventually constant*

> Let $N$ be a positive integer. Positive integers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_N$ are given, none of which is a multiple of $2^{N+1}$. For each integer $n \ge N+1$, define $a_n$ as follows:
> 
> Among $k = 1, 2, \ldots, n-1$, choose $k$ for which the remainder of $a_k$ upon division by $2^n$ is smallest, and set $a_n = 2a_k$. If there are several such $k$, choose the largest of them.
> 
> Show that there exists a positive integer $M$ such that $a_n = a_M$ holds for all $n \ge M$.
