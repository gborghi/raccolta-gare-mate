*Finitely many segments on a regular tetrahedron of edge 1 joining every pair of vertices by a polygonal path; can total length be less than 1+sqrt(3)?*

> On the surface of a regular tetrahedron of edge length $1$ are given finitely many segments such that every two vertices of the tetrahedron can be joined by a polygonal line consisting of given segments. Can the sum of the lengths of the given segments be less than $1 + \sqrt{3}$?
