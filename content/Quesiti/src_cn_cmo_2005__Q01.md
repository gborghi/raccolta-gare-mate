*Trig inequalities: simultaneous x exists iff sum condition*

> Let $\theta_i \in \left(-\dfrac{\pi}{2}, \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)$, $i = 1, 2, 3, 4$. Prove that there exists $x \in \mathbb{R}$ such that the two inequalities
> $$\cos^2\theta_1 \cos^2\theta_2 - (\sin\theta_1 \sin\theta_2 - x)^2 \geq 0,$$
> $$\cos^2\theta_3 \cos^2\theta_4 - (\sin\theta_3 \sin\theta_4 - x)^2 \geq 0$$
> hold simultaneously if and only if
> $$\sum_{i=1}^{4} \sin^2\theta_i \leq 2\left(1 + \prod_{i=1}^{4} \sin\theta_i + \prod_{i=1}^{4} \cos\theta_i\right).$$
> (Posed by Li Shenghong)
