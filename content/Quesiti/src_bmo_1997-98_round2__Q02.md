# British Mathematical Olympiad 1997-98 — Round 2 — Quesito 2

*Triangle ABC with isosceles sub-triangle and equilateral point; prove circumcircles touch*

> A triangle $ABC$ has $\angle BAC = 64^\circ$ and $\angle ABC = 76^\circ$. A line $PQ$ is parallel to $AB$, where $P$ is on $AC$ and $Q$ is on $BC$. The triangle $PQC$ is isosceles with $PQ = PC$. A point $D$ outside the triangle $PQC$ is such that $\triangle PDC$ is equilateral. $R$ is the point on $DC$ separated from $P$ by $AC$.
> 
> Prove that the circumcircle of $\triangle ADE$ touches the circumcircle of $\triangle PQR$.
