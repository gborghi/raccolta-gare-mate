*Self-map on {1,...,n}: unique g satisfying f(g(f(m)))=g(f(g(m))) implies f and f∘f have same image*

> Let $n$ be a positive integer. Let $S = \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$, and let $\mathcal{A}$ be the set of all functions from $S$ to $S$. Let $f$ be a function in $\mathcal{A}$. Suppose that the only function $g \in \mathcal{A}$ satisfying
> $$f(g(f(m))) = g(f(g(m))), \quad m = 1, 2, \ldots, n$$
> is $g = f$ itself. Prove that
> $$\{f(m) \mid m = 1, 2, \ldots, n\} = \{f(f(m)) \mid m = 1, 2, \ldots, n\}.$$
