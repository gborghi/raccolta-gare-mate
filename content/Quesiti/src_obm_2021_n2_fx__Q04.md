*Right triangle ABC (right angle at B) with inscribed square BDEF; incircles of CFE and EDA have radii c and b; inside the square two circles of radius b are tangent and two of radius a are tangent. Find a/c*

> In the figure below we have a triangle $ABC$, right-angled at $B$, and $BDEF$ is a square inscribed in this triangle. The circles inscribed in the triangles $CFE$ and $EDA$ have radii $c$ and $b$, respectively. Inside the square, two circles of radius $b$ and two circles of radius $a$ are drawn. The two circles of radius $b$ are tangent to each other and the two circles of radius $a$ are tangent to each other. Recall that when two circles are tangent, the center is collinear with the point of tangency.
> 
> Determine the ratio $\dfrac{a}{c}$.
