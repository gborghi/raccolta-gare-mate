*Cubic with all positive real roots: bounds on coefficients*

> Suppose $a$ and $b$ are real numbers such that the roots of the cubic equation $ax^3 - x^2 + bx - 1 = 0$ are all positive real numbers. Prove that:
> (i) $0 < 3ab \le 1$ and (ii) $b \ge \sqrt{3}$.
