*1000 red/blue dots on circle; find minimum faults for adjacency*

> An artist arranges 1000 dots evenly around a circle, with each dot being either red or blue. A critic looks at the artwork and counts *faults*: each time two red dots are adjacent is one fault, and each time two blue dots are exactly two apart (that is, they have exactly one dot in between them) is another.
> 
> What is the smallest number of faults the critic could find?
