*Sum-free set: some a_i fails to divide neighbors' sum*

> Let $n$ be an integer with $n \ge 3$. Let $S$ be a set consisting of $n$ mutually distinct positive integers such that the sum of any two distinct elements of $S$ is never an element of $S$. Write $S = \{a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n\}$ with $a_1 < a_2 < \cdots < a_n$. Prove that there exists an integer $i$ with $2 \le i \le n-1$ such that $a_{i-1} + a_{i+1}$ is not divisible by $a_i$.
