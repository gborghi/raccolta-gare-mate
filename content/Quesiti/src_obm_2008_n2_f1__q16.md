*Magic sum of an order-7 magic square with numbers 1 to 49*

> The figure below is an example of a magic square of order $4$. The sum of the $4$ numbers in each line, column or diagonal is $34$. We then say that the magic sum of this magic square is $34$. Suppose there exists a magic square of order $7$, formed by the integers from $1$ to $49$. Determine its magic sum.
> (A) $175$ \quad (B) $2450$ \quad (C) $1225$ \quad (D) $190$ \quad (E) $100$
