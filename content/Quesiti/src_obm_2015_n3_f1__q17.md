*Min radius r so every circle of radius 1 centered at a lattice point is cut by a radius-r circle*

> At every lattice point (point with both coordinates integers), we construct a circle of radius $r$. The minimum value of $r$ for which some circle of radius 1 (centered at a lattice point) is entirely contained inside one of the radius-$r$ circles centered at a different lattice point is:
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - 1$ \quad (B) $1 - \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ \quad (C) $\sqrt{2} - 1$ \quad (D) $\dfrac{1}{2}$ \quad (E) $\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
