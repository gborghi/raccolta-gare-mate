*Function on interval from quadratic roots; max-min and trig inequality*

> Suppose that $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are different real roots of the equation $4x^2 - 4tx - 1 = 0$ ($t \in \mathbf{R}$). $[\alpha,\, \beta]$ is the domain of the function $f(x) = \dfrac{2x - t}{x^2 + 1}$.
> 
> (1) Find $g(t) = \max f(x) - \min f(x)$.
> 
> (2) Prove that for $u_i \in \left(0,\, \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)$ ($i = 1,\, 2,\, 3$), if $\sin u_1 + \sin u_2 + \sin u_3 = 1$, then
> $$\frac{1}{g(\tan u_1)} + \frac{1}{g(\tan u_2)} + \frac{1}{g(\tan u_3)} < \frac{3}{4}\sqrt{6}.$$
