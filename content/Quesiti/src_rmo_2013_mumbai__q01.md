*Isosceles triangle with circumcircle, prove BE parallel to AD*

> Let $ABC$ be an isosceles triangle with $AB = AC$ and let $\Gamma$ denote its circumcircle. A point $D$ is on the arc $AB$ of $\Gamma$ not containing $C$ and a point $E$ is on the arc $AC$ of $\Gamma$ not containing $B$ such that $AD = CE$. Prove that $BE$ is parallel to $AD$.
