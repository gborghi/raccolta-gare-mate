*Quadratic polynomial, P(s)=t, P(t)=s; prove b-st is a root*

> Let $P(x) = x^2 + ax + b$ be a quadratic polynomial with real coefficients. Suppose there are real numbers $s \neq t$ such that $P(s) = t$ and $P(t) = s$. Prove that $b - st$ is a root of the equation $x^2 + ax - b - at = 0$.
