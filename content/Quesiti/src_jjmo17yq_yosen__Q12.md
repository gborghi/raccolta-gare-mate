# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2019 — Yosen (Preliminary) — Quesito 12

*Distinct strings reachable in 4 operations from 'A'*

> On a blackboard a string made of the letters $A$ and $B$ is written. Consider the following operation: take the current string $w$, append one more letter (either $A$ or $B$), and then change exactly one of the $A$'s to a $B$ or one of the $B$'s to an $A$. For example, if $w = ABA$, the strings that can appear after one operation are $ABAABA$, $BBAABA$, $AAAABA$, $ABBABA$, $ABABBA$, $ABAAAA$, $ABAABB$ (among such results). Now suppose the string $A$ is written on the blackboard. Starting from this state, after performing the operation exactly $4$ times, how many distinct strings can appear on the blackboard?
