# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2006 — Nível 3 — Fase 1 — Quesito 3

*Amoeba colony starting yellow+red; each amoeba divides daily, each daughter independently keeps color or becomes red w.p. 1/2; find probability of exactly one yellow after 2006 days.*

> A colony of amoebas initially has one yellow amoeba and one red amoeba. Every day each amoeba divides into two identical amoebas. Each amoeba turns out the same color as its parent with probability $\frac{1}{2}$ and red with probability $\frac{1}{2}$. After 2006 days, what is the probability that the colony has exactly one yellow amoeba?
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{1}{2^{2006}}$ \quad (B) $\dfrac{1}{2006}$ \quad (C) $\dfrac{1}{2007}$ \quad (D) $\dfrac{1}{2006 \cdot 2007}$ \quad (E) $\dfrac{2006}{2007}$
