*Max honest count identifiable via villager liar-count reports*

> There are villagers numbered $1$ to $2015$ living in a village; among the $2016$ villagers some are honest and some are liars, with at least one honest villager. An honest villager always tells the truth, while a liar may state either truth or falsehood (you do not know which each liar does, nor who is honest). You know that the number of honest villagers is exactly $T$. Each villager knows, for every other villager, whether that villager is honest or a liar. On any day you may secretly give an instruction to one chosen villager; on the next day that villager writes down the count of liars among a specified set of villagers and, on the evening of the $i$-th day, delivers the paper to villager $i - k_i$ (with $1 \le i \le 2016$). An honest villager writes the true count; a liar may write any number. Determine the maximum value of $T$ for which there is a strategy that, no matter how the liars behave, lets you correctly identify all of the honest villagers. (Note: parts of this problem's mechanics are partly illegible in the scan; the final answer is $503$.)
