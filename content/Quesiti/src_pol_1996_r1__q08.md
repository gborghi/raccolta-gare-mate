*Light ray from center of square reflects off sides and returns to center for the first time; prove odd number of reflections*

> A ray of light starts from the center of a square and reflects from its sides (the angles of reflection and incidence are always equal). The ray never reaches a vertex of the square and, after some time, it returns to the center for the first time. Prove that the ray reflected from the sides of the square an odd number of times.
