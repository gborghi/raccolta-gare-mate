*Perimeter of an octagon formed by fitting two congruent pentagons together*

> Two congruent pentagons are each formed by removing a right-angled isosceles triangle from a square of side-length 1. The two pentagons are then fitted together as shown. What is the perimeter of the octagon formed?
>
> - **(A)** $4$
> - **(B)** $4 + 2\sqrt{2}$
> - **(C)** $5$
> - **(D)** $4 - 2\sqrt{2}$
> - **(E)** $6$
