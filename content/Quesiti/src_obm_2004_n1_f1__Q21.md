*True positive weight values for a street vendor's claim*

> A street vendor sells potatoes and, for weighing, uses a two-pan balance and weights of $1$ kg, $3$ kg, and $10$ kg. Consider the claim: "This vendor can weigh $n$ kilograms of potatoes." How many positive values of $n$ make this claim true?
> 
> (A) $7$ \quad (B) $10$ \quad (C) $12$ \quad (D) $13$ \quad (E) $14$
