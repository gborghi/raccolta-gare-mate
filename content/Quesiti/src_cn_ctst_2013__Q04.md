*Existence of n with binomial coefficient congruence mod prime power*

> Let $p$ be a prime, $a$ and $b$ be positive integers satisfying $p^{2^a - 1} < 2p^b$. Prove that for any non-negative integer $k$, there exists a positive integer $n < p^{a+b}$ such that $n \equiv k \pmod{p^a}$ and $\binom{n}{k} \equiv k \pmod{p^b}$. (posed by Yu Hongbing)
