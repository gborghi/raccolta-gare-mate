*Count triangulations of a cube into tetrahedra using only cube vertices*

> Let $S$ be a cube. In how many ways can $S$ be partitioned into a collection of tetrahedra $\{T_1, \ldots, T_k\}$ satisfying the following two conditions?
> \begin{itemize}
> \item[(1)] The vertices of each tetrahedron $T_i$ are all vertices of $S$.
> \item[(2)] For any $i \neq j$, if $T_i$ and $T_j$ share a common part, then that common part is either a common face, a common edge, or a common vertex of $T_i$ and $T_j$.
> \end{itemize}
