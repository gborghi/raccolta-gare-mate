# British Mathematical Olympiad 1972-73 — Round 1 — Quesito 7

*Life-expectancy of Draconian aged 70 from life-table model y = A(100-x)^B*

> The life-table issued by the Registrar-General of Draconia shows out of $10{,}000$ live births the number $(y)$ expected to be alive $x$ years later. When $x = 60$, $y = 3{,}025$. For $60 \le x \le 100$ the curve $y = A(100 - x)^B$ fits the figures in the table very closely, $A$ and $B$ being constants.
> 
> Determine the life-expectancy (in years correct to one decimal place) of a Draconian aged $70$.
> 
> N.B. At age $100$ all Draconians are put to death.
