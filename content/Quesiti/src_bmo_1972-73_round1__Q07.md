*Life-expectancy of a Draconian aged 70 from a life-table model*

> The life-table issued by the Registrar-General of Draconia shows out of $10{,}000$ live births the number $(y)$ expected to be alive $a$ years later. For $60 \le a \le 100$ the curve $y = Ax\,(100 - x) + \frac{B}{(x - 40)^2}$ fits the figures in the table very closely, $A$ and $B$ being constants.
> 
> Determine the life-expectancy (in years correct to one decimal place) of a Draconian aged $70$.
> 
> N.B. At age $100$ all Draconians are put to death.
