*Prime divisor p of a^3-3a+1 with p not 3 has the form 9k+1 or 9k-1*

> Let $a$ be a positive integer and $p$ a prime divisor of $a^3 - 3a + 1$ with $p \ne 3$. Prove that $p$ is of the form $9k + 1$ or $9k - 1$, with $k$ an integer.
