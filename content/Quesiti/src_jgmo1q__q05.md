*Circumcenters of ABC and PQR coincide; incircle tangency condition in acute triangle*

> Let $ABC$ be an acute triangle with $AB < AC$. Let $O$ be the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$, and let $XY$ denote the length of segment $XY$. Let $J$ be the second intersection of line $AO$ (extended beyond $O$) with the circumcircle of $\triangle BCJ$; more precisely, let $P$ be a point on segment $BC$ such that $A$, $O$, $P$ are collinear (i.e., $P$ is the foot of the $A$-altitude from $O$ to $BC$, or the intersection of line $AO$ with $BC$). On segment $BP$, choose a point $Q$ such that $AQ = AB$; on segment $CP$, choose a point $R$ such that $AR = AC$.
> 
> Prove that the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$ coincides with the circumcenter of $\triangle PQR$.
> 
> Moreover, let $\omega$ be a circle tangent to segments $BC$, $AB$ (extended), and $AC$ (extended) — i.e., an excircle or the incircle of $\triangle ABC$. The center of $\omega$ that is not the incenter is the center of the circumcircle of $\triangle PQR$ as shown above.
> 
> **Note:** Based on the scanned Japanese source, the full statement is: Let $ABC$ be an acute triangle with $AB < AC$. Let $O$ be the circumcenter. Let $XY$ denote the length of segment $XY$. Let $J$ be the point on line $AO$ outside triangle $ABC$ (on the far side from $A$). Let $P$ be the point on segment $BC$ at which line $AO$ meets $BC$. On segment $BP$, let $Q$ be the point with $AQ = AB$; on segment $CP$, let $R$ be the point with $AR = AC$. Prove that the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$ and the circumcenter of $\triangle PQR$ are the same point. In addition, the incircle of $\triangle ABC$ is tangent to $BC$, to half-line $AB$, and to half-line $AC$; among the circles tangent to these three lines, those that are not the incircle have their centers shown to coincide with the circumcenter of $\triangle PQR$.
