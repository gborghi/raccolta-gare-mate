*Integers with $k$ distinct terms summing to distinct values below $n$*

> Let $n$ be a nonzero natural number. We say that a nonzero natural number $k$ satisfies condition $C_n$ if there exist $2k$ natural numbers $a_1, b_1, \ldots, a_k, b_k$, all distinct, such that the sums $a_1 + b_1, \ldots, a_k + b_k$ are pairwise distinct and all strictly less than $n$.
> 
> 1. Show that if $k$ satisfies condition $C_n$, then $k \le \dfrac{2n-3}{5}$.
> 2. Show that $5$ satisfies condition $C_{14}$.
> 3. Suppose that $\dfrac{2n-3}{5}$ is an integer. Show that $\dfrac{2n-3}{5}$ satisfies condition $C_n$.
