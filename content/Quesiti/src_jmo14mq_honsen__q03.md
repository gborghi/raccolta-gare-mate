*Perpendicular planes; bisector meets circle, prove angle bisected*

> In space, there are two mutually perpendicular planes $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$. Let $A$ and $B$ be two distinct points on the line of intersection of $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$, and let $C$ be a point that lies on $\pi_2$ but not on $\pi_1$. Let $P$ be the intersection of the bisector of $\angle BCA$ with $AB$, and let $S$ be the circle in $\pi_1$ having $AB$ as a diameter. Show that, for any plane $\pi_3$ containing $CP$, if $D$ and $E$ denote the intersection points of $\pi_3$ with $S$, then $CP$ is the bisector of $\angle DCE$.
