*Value of bcos(c)/a in trigonometric functional equation*

> Let $f(x) = 3\sin x + 2\cos x + 1$. If real numbers $a$, $b$, $c$ are such that $af(x) + bf(x - c) = 1$ holds for any $x \in \mathbb{R}$, then $\dfrac{b\cos c}{a}$ equals $($\quad$)$.
> 
> (A) $-\dfrac{1}{2}$ $\quad$ (B) $\dfrac{1}{2}$ $\quad$ (C) $-1$ $\quad$ (D) $1$
