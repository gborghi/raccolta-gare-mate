- [[src_cgen_2003__Q01|Quesito 1]] — Preliminary questions: a vector identity, concurrency of altitudes (orthocentre), and a characterization of the orthocentre via the circumcentre.
- [[src_cgen_2003__Q02|Quesito 2]] — First part: determine orthocentric sets with 3 and 4 elements, study the orthocentric set generated by 4 concyclic points, and find the orthocentre set of a circle and of a disc.
- [[src_cgen_2003__Q03|Quesito 3]] — Second part: probabilistic questions on triangles whose vertices are among the $2n$ vertices of a regular polygon inscribed in a circle of radius $R$ centred at $O$ (right triangle probability, acute triangle probability, expectation of squared distance from $O$ to the orthocentre).
- [[src_cgen_2003__Q04|Quesito 4]] — Third part: coordinates of the orthocentre of a triangle with given coordinates; orthocentric set obtained from a line plus an external point; and properties of an orthocentric set contained in the union of the two coordinate axes.
- [[src_cgen_2003__Q05|Quesito 5]] — Fourth part: construction of remarkable orthocentric sets on the hyperbola $xy=k$; orthocentre conditions for points on the hyperbola; a quadratic with irrational roots and a similitude $z\mapsto(1-ri)z$; and that the integer-coordinate points of $X$ form an infinite orthocentric set.
- [[src_cgen_2003__Q06|Quesito 6]] — Fifth part: an iteration $X_{n+1}=\mathcal{H}(X_n)$ starting from four points in general position, using a similitude result relating the hyperbola $xy=1$ and the degenerate locus $xy=0$; show the minimal period $m$ is $1$ or $2$ and characterize the initial sets achieving each.
