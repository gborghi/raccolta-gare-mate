*Study of the limit of lambda_n (proportion of AD inside the circles): integral computations, inequalities, and convergence*

> Study of a limit.
> 
> Starting from the configuration studied in part 1, question 2, one defines $\lambda_n$ as the proportion of the segment $[AD]$ situated inside the circles $(\Gamma_k)$, for $1 \le k \le n-1$. Thus, one has $\lambda_n = \frac{1}{AD} \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} B_k C_k$.
> 
> 1. Calculation of integrals.
>    One defines the function $f$, from $[0,1]$ into $\mathbb{R}$, by: for every $x \in [0,1]$, $f(x) = \int_0^x \sqrt{1-t^2}\, dt$ then the function $F$, from $\left[0, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$ into $\mathbb{R}$, by: for every $x \in \left[0, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$, $F(x) = f(\sin x)$.
>    a. Show that the function $F$ is differentiable and calculate its derivative, denoted $F'$.
>    b. Show that, for every $x$ in $\left[0, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$, $F(x) = \int_0^x \cos^2 t\, dt$.
>    c. Without seeking to calculate the integrals, demonstrate the equality $\int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \cos^2(t)\, dt = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \sin^2(t)\, dt$ and deduce the value common to these two integrals.
>    d. Deduce that $\int_0^1 \sqrt{1-t^2}\, dt = \frac{\pi}{4}$; interpret this result geometrically.
> 
> 2.
>    a. Show that for every $n \ge 2$, $\lambda_n = \frac{2}{2n-1} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \sqrt{1 - \frac{k}{n} \cdot \frac{k-1}{n-1}}$.
>    b. Show that if $n \ge 2$ and $1 \le k \le n$, one has: $\left(\frac{k-1}{n}\right)^2 \le \frac{k}{n} \cdot \frac{k-1}{n-1} \le \left(\frac{k}{n}\right)^2$.
>    c. One sets $I_{n,k} = \int_{\frac{k-1}{n}}^{\frac{k}{n}} \sqrt{1-t^2}\, dt$. Show that for suitable values of $n$ and $k$, which one will specify, one has: $n I_{n,k+1} \le \sqrt{1 - \frac{k-1}{n-1} \cdot \frac{k}{n}} \le n I_{n,k-1}$.
> 
> 3. Demonstrate, from the results of questions 1 and 2 above, that the sequence $(\lambda_n)$ is convergent and calculate its limit.
