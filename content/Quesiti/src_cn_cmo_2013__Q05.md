*Prove divisibility of binary-coefficient polynomials transfers to all bases*

> For each positive integer $n$ and each integer $i$ $(0 \leq i \leq n)$, let $C_n^i \equiv c(n, i) \pmod{2}$, where $c(n, i) \in \{0, 1\}$, and define
> $$f(n, q) = \sum_{i=0}^{n} c(n, i)\,q^i.$$
> Let $m$, $n$ and $q$ be positive integers with $q + 1$ not a power of $2$. Suppose that $f(m, q) \mid f(n, q)$. Prove that $f(m, r) \mid f(n, r)$ for every positive integer $r$.
