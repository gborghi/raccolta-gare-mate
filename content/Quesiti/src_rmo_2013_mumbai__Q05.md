*Show gcd(b3,c3)=a2 for sequences defined by gcd and lcm*

> Let $a_1, b_1, c_1$ be natural numbers. Define
> $$a_2 = \gcd(b_1, c_1), \quad b_2 = \gcd(c_1, a_1), \quad c_2 = \gcd(a_1, b_1),$$
> and
> $$a_3 = \operatorname{lcm}(b_2, c_2), \quad b_3 = \operatorname{lcm}(c_2, a_2), \quad c_3 = \operatorname{lcm}(a_2, b_2).$$
> Show that $\gcd(b_3, c_3) = a_2$.
