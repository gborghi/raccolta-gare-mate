*Island-bridge graph: shelter assignment with parity conditions*

> Let $n \geq 2$ be an integer. There are $n$ islands arranged in a large lake, connected by some bridges; each bridge connects two islands directly. For any two islands, either they are connected by a bridge or they are not, and each bridge connects exactly two distinct islands. In order to prepare for a disaster, we decided to designate, for each island, one of the two islands at the ends of some bridge connected to that island as its designated shelter, with the condition that the bridge connecting an island directly to its shelter must exist. Each island must have at least one bridge connecting it to another island, and we choose the largest island as the shelter.
> 
> (1) Prove that if $n$ is odd, then there exists an island that cannot be chosen as a shelter for any island.
> 
> (2) Prove that if $n$ is even and there are more than $\dfrac{n^2}{4}$ bridges, then there exists an island that cannot be chosen as a shelter for any island.
