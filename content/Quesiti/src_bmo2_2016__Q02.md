*Alison and Benjamin token-ranking game, find largest N*

> Alison has $N$ lucky tokens, labelled $1$ to $N$. She thinks they are good, but refuses to share them. Benjamin may mention a team of three tokens to her; she will then choose either to tell him which she thinks is the weakest token of the three, or which she thinks is the strongest token of the three. Benjamin uses this information as many times as he likes. Determine the largest $N$ such that Benjamin can guarantee to be able to find a sequence $T_1, T_2, \ldots, T_N$ of tokens with the property that he knows that Alison thinks that $T_i$ is better than $T_{i+1}$ for each $1 \le i < N$.
