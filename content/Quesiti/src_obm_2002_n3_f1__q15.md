*Divisibility claims about x^3+y^3+z^3 given that x+y+z=0*

> Let $x$, $y$, $z$ be integers such that $x+y+z=0$. About $x^3+y^3+z^3$ the following statements are made:
> i) It is necessarily a multiple of 2.
> ii) It is necessarily a multiple of 3.
> iii) It is necessarily a multiple of 5.
> We can state that:
>
> - **(A)** only i) is correct.
> - **(B)** only ii) is correct.
> - **(C)** only i) and ii) are correct.
> - **(D)** only i) and iii) are correct.
> - **(E)** i), ii) and iii) are correct.
