*Find m+n for a linear equation with solution less than 60*

> Professor Piraldo gave Esmeralda an equation of the form $ax = b$, where $a$ and $b$ are real numbers. Esmeralda made an error and solved the equation $bx = a$ instead, obtaining a solution that is exactly equal to the correct solution minus $60$. If the correct solution has the form $m + \sqrt{n}$ with $m$ and $n$ integers, what is the value of $m + n$?
