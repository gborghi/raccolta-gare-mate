*Every necklace of 9 two-colour pearls contains two equivalent 5-pearl sequences*

> The sequence $BBBPBPPP$ possesses no two equivalent subsequences. Consider any sequence of $9$ pearls (each pearl black $B$ or white $P$). Case 1: if there are no three consecutive pearls of the same colour, then either the pearls strictly alternate (and any block of $5$ consecutive pearls contains two equivalent sequences), or there are two adjacent equal pearls and some neighbouring block of $5$ contains a sequence of type $PBBP$, giving two equivalent sequences. Case 2: if there are three consecutive pearls $BBB$ and a continuation at one extreme is not the opposite colour, two equivalent sequences appear immediately; if both continuations are the opposite colour and not at an extreme, the block $PBBBP$ contains two equivalent sequences; if $BBB$ sits at one extreme (say the left) with no three equal letters outside the extremes, the possible continuations $BBBPPB$, $BBBPBB$ give three equivalent sequences, while the only continuation avoiding two equivalent sequences is $BBBPBPPP$, after which any added pearl at the right extreme generates two equivalent sequences. Hence any sequence of $9$ pearls possesses two equivalent sequences. Prove this. (Note: original multiple-choice options are not reproduced in the source gabarito.)
