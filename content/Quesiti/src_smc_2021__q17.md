*Circle of radius r with right-angled isosceles triangle, one short side = diameter; find shaded area.*

> A circle of radius $r$ and a right-angled isosceles triangle are drawn such that one of the shorter sides of the triangle is a diameter of the circle. What is the shaded area?
>
> - **(A)** $\frac{r^2}{\sqrt{2}}$
> - **(B)** $r^2$
> - **(C)** $2\pi r$
> - **(D)** $\frac{r^2}{2}$
> - **(E)** $(\sqrt{2}-1)\pi r^2$

> - **(C)** $2\pi r$
> - **(D)** $\frac{r^2}{2}$
