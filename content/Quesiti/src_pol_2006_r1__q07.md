*Prime p>3 and positive integers a,b,c with a+b+c=p+1 and p | a^3+b^3+c^3-1; show one of a,b,c equals 1.*

> A prime number $p > 3$ and positive integers $a, b, c$ satisfy $a + b + c = p + 1$ and the number $a^3 + b^3 + c^3 - 1$ is divisible by $p$. Show that at least one of the numbers $a, b, c$ is equal to $1$.
