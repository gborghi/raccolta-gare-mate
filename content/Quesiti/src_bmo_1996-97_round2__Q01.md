# British Mathematical Olympiad 1996-97 — Round 2 — Quesito 1

*9-digit integers M, N; digit replacement divisible by 7; find d or prove*

> Let $M$ and $N$ be two 9-digit positive integers with the property that $N$ is obtained from $M$ by replacing the 'tens' digit of $M$ by the corresponding digit (i.e., the 'tens' digit of $N$); the resulting integer is a multiple of $7$.
> 
> Prove that the number obtained by replacing a digit of $N$ by the corresponding digit of $M$ is a multiple of $7$.
> 
> Find an integer $d \ge 0$ such that the above result concerning divisibility by $7$ remains true when $M$ and $N$ are two $d$-digit numbers.
