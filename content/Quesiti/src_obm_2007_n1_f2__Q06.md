*A column addition contains one wrongly written digit; correct it and compute a power.*

> In the addition below, on first inspection one may assume that the three digits on the right of all the numbers are written correctly, but one digit is written incorrectly. Among the digits, the $9$ is written incorrectly. By analysis, in the place of the wrong digit a $6$ must be placed, and a $2$ replaces it where required, so that the addition
> $$\begin{array}{r} 7\,4\,6\,5\,8\,6 \\ +\;8\,6\,9\,4\,3\,0 \\ \hline 1\,6\,1\,6\,0\,1\,6 \end{array}$$
> becomes correct. Making the substitution and checking that the rest fits, one obtains $a=2$. Determine the value of $a^6$ (we have $a^6=2^6=64$).
