*Quadratic satisfying mod condition on given points*

> $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_k$ are given points of the interval $[-1,1]$ such that
> $$x_1 = -1, \quad x_k = 1, \quad 0 < x_i - x_{i-1} \le \frac{1}{2} \quad (2 \le i \le k).$$
> The quadratic function $f$ of the form
> $$f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c,$$
> where $a, b, c$ are real constants, satisfies the condition
> $$|f(x_i)| \le 1 \quad (1 \le i \le k).$$
> Prove that
> $$|f(x)| \le \frac{5}{4}$$
> for all $x$ in $[-1,1]$. Show by means of an example that this proposition becomes false if $\frac{1}{2}$ is replaced by any smaller number.
