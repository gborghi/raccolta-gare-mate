*Study of condition (C_1): translating it via p=2n-1, q=2k-1 into p^2-q^2=16a^2 and characterizing solutions through sums of two squares*

> Study of the condition $(C_1)$.
> 
> We consider two integers $n$ and $k$ such that $1 \le k \le n-1$.
> 
> 1. One sets $p = 2n-1$ and $q = 2k-1$. Show that the couple $(n,k)$ verifies the condition $(C_1)$ if, and only if, $(p,q)$ is a couple of odd natural integers such that $q < p$, verifying the following condition $(C_2)$:
>    $(C_2)$: there exists $a \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $p^2 - q^2 = 16a^2$.
> 
> 2. Let $(p,q)$ be a couple of natural integers, such that there exist integers $u > 0$ and $v > 0$, of different parities, for which $p = u^2 + v^2$ and $q = u^2 - v^2$. Show that $(p,q)$ is a couple of odd natural integers such that $q < p$ verifying the condition $(C_2)$.
> 
> 3. We consider a couple $(p,q)$ of odd natural integers, coprime, such that $q < p$ and verifying the condition $(C_2)$. Show that there exist two natural integers $u$ and $v$ of different parities such that $p = u^2 + v^2$ and $q = u^2 - v^2$. Calculate then, as a function of $u$ and $v$, the value of the integer $a$ that intervenes in the condition $(C_2)$.
