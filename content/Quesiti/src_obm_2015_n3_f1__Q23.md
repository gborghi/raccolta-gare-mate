*Smallest side of triangle formed by tangents through bisector–circumcircle intersections*

> In triangle $ABC$, $AB = 40$, $AC = 42$, $BC = 58$. The internal angle bisectors from $A$, $B$, $C$ meet the circumscribed circle of $ABC$ again at $K$, $L$, $M$, respectively. The lines tangent to the circumscribed circle at $K$, $L$, $M$ form a triangle. Determine the smallest side of this triangle.
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{280}{3}$ \quad (B) $58$ \quad (C) $145$ \quad (D) $\dfrac{145}{7}$ \quad (E) $\dfrac{2900}{17}$
