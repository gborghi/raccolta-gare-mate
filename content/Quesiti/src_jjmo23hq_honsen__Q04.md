# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2025 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 4

*At most n-3 terms among a_1,...,a_n are p-th powers given divisibility*

> Let $p$ be a prime, $n$ be an integer with $n \ge 4$, and $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ be positive integers. Suppose that each of the $n - 1$ partial sums
> $$a_1 + a_2, \quad a_1 + a_2 + a_3, \quad \ldots, \quad a_1 + a_2 + \cdots + a_n$$
> is divisible by $p$. Prove that among $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$, at most $n - 3$ of them are \emph{$p$-th powers}.
> 
> Here, a \emph{$p$-th power} is a positive integer that can be expressed as $m^p$ for some positive integer $m$. Also, $p^0 = 1$ is regarded as a $p$-th power (i.e., $1 = 1^p$).
