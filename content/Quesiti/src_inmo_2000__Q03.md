*Equal ratios with abc nonzero: a+b+c=0 or a=b=c*

> If $a, b, c$ are real numbers such that $abc \neq 0$ and
> $$\frac{xb + (1-x)c}{a} = \frac{xc + (1-x)a}{b} = \frac{xa + (1-x)b}{c},$$
> then prove that either $a + b + c = 0$ or $a = b = c$.
