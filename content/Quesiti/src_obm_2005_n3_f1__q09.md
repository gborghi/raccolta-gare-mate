*Estimate volume of all platinum produced in history via density and tonnage*

> Platinum is a very rare metal, rarer and dearer than gold. Its density is $21.45\ \text{g/cm}^3$. Suppose the world production of platinum in the last $50$ years was about $110$ tonnes per year, and that before that it was negligible. Mark the alternative whose volume is closest to the volume of platinum produced in human history:
> (A) a shoebox (B) a swimming pool (C) a ten-storey building (D) Mount Pascoal (E) the Moon
