# Coupe Animath Printemps 2022 — Quesito 15

*Power-sum dominance for two exponents; prove inequality persists and a refined bound*

> Let $n$ be a strictly positive integer, $x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1}$ strictly positive reals, and $p < q$ two strictly positive integers. Suppose that
> $$x_{n+1}^p > x_1^p + \ldots + x_n^p.$$
> 
> 1) Show that $x_{n+1}^q > x_1^q + \ldots + x_n^q$.
> 
> 2) Show that
> $$\left(x_{n+1}^p - (x_1^p + \ldots + x_n^p)\right)^q < \left(x_{n+1}^q - (x_1^q + \ldots + x_n^q)\right)^p.$$
