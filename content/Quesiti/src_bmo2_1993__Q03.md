*Prove $PA\sin\alpha = PB\sin\beta = PC\sin\gamma$ for interior point*

> Let $P$ be an internal point of triangle $ABC$ and let $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$ be defined by
> $$\alpha = \angle BPC - \angle BAC, \quad \beta = \angle CPA - \angle CBA, \quad \gamma = \angle APB - \angle ACB.$$
> Prove that
> $$PA \cdot \sin \alpha = PB \cdot \sin \beta = PC \cdot \sin \gamma.$$
