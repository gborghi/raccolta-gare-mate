*Real sequence in [0,1) with prescribed absolute differences and zero endpoints*

> Given integer $n \ge 2$ and real numbers $r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_n$ in the interval $[0, 1)$, prove that there exist real numbers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ satisfying simultaneously the following conditions:
> (1) $a_0 = a_n = 0$;
> (2) $|a_i| \le 1$ for every $i = 0, 1, \ldots, n$;
> (3) $|a_i - a_{i-1}| = r_i$ for every $i = 1, 2, \ldots, n$.
