*Integral equation implying two equal-derivative points*

> Let $f:[0,1]\to[0,1]$ be increasing, differentiable, and invertible.
> 
> If $\displaystyle\int_0^1 f(x)\,dx = \int_0^1 f^{-1}(x)\,dx$, prove that there exist two distinct reals $a,b$ with $0\le a < b \le 1$ such that $f'(a)=f'(b)=1$.
> 
> (Note: $f^{-1}$ denotes the inverse function of $f$.)
