*Partition set into two subsets avoiding n-term arithmetic-mean condition*

> Given an integer $n \ge 3$, prove that the set $X = \{1, 2, 3, \ldots, n^2 - n\}$ can be divided into two non-intersecting subsets such that neither of them contains $n$ elements $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ with $a_1 < a_2 < \cdots < a_n$ and $a_k \le \dfrac{a_{k-1} + a_{k+1}}{2}$ for all $k = 2, \ldots, n-1$.
