*Prove uniqueness of reals satisfying a sum-of-squares identity*

> Prove that there is only one set of real numbers $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$ such that
> $$(1-x_1)^2 + (x_1-x_2)^2 + \ldots + (x_{n-1}-x_n)^2 + x_n^2 = \frac{1}{n+1}.$$
