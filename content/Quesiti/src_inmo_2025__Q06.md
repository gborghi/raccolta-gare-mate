*Prove infinitely many payable numbers exist for notes with b-1 copies*

> Let $b \ge 2$ be a positive integer. Anu has an infinite collection of notes with exactly $b - 1$ copies of a note with value $b^k$ for every integer $k \ge 1$. A positive integer $n$ is called payable if Anu can pay exactly $n^2 + 1$ rupees by using some collection of her notes. Prove that if there is a payable number, then there are infinitely many payable numbers.
