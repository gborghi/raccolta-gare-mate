*In triangle ABC with centroid G and D the midpoint of CA, the line through G parallel to BC meets AB at E; prove angle AEC = angle DGC iff angle ACB = 90 degrees.*

> In triangle $ABC$ the centroid is $G$ and $D$ is the midpoint of $CA$. The line through $G$ parallel to $BC$ meets $AB$ at $E$. Prove that $\angle AEC = \angle DGC$ if, and only if, $\angle ACB = 90^\circ$. (The centroid of a triangle is the intersection of the three medians, the lines which join each vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.)
