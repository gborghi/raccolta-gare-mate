*Five nonneg reals summing to 1: neighboring products on circle*

> (1) For any five nonneg real numbers $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$ and $e$ with their sum equal to $1$, it is always possible to arrange them around a circle such that there are always two neighboring numbers with their product not less than $\frac{1}{4}$.
> 
> (2) For any five nonneg real numbers $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$ and $e$ with their sum equal to $1$, it is always possible to arrange them around a circle such that there are two neighboring numbers with their product not greater than $\frac{1}{9}$.
> 
> (posed by Qian Zhanwang)
