# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2013 — Nível 3 — Fase 1 — Quesito 17

*Flea race: jumps of m and n cm (m<n) meeting first at 1 m; count pairs (m,n)*

> In a circus, the main attraction is the \emph{Flea Race}. Two fleas, $P_1$ and $P_2$, perfectly trained, jump along the same straight line, with constant velocities, starting from the same point at the same instant. Each jump of flea $P_1$ measures $m$ centimeters and each jump of flea $P_2$ measures $n$ centimeters, with $m < n$, both integers. However flea $P_1$ is faster than flea $P_2$, so that, independently of the velocity of $P_2$, $P_1$ can always catch up to it after some jumps. Supposing that, after the start, the fleas will be together, for the first time, at the end of $1$ meter, determine the number of possible pairs $(m, n)$.
> 
> (A) $12$ \quad (B) $24$ \quad (C) $36$ \quad (D) $48$ \quad (E) $100$
