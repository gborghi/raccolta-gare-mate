*Triangle ABC (3,4,5): ratio of inradius to radius of circle centered on BC tangent to AC*

> Given a triangle $ABC$ with sides $AB = 3$, $BC = 4$ and $AC = 5$. Let $R_1$ and $R_2$ be, respectively, the radii of the inscribed circle and of the circle with center on the side $BC$ that passes through $B$ and is tangent to the side $AC$. The ratio $\dfrac{R_1}{R_2}$ is equal to:
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{3}{4}$ (B) $\dfrac{2}{3}$ (C) $\dfrac{3}{2}$ (D) $\dfrac{8}{9}$ (E) $\dfrac{4}{5}$
