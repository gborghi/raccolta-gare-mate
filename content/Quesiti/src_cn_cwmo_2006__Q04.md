*Prove fractional parts of sqrt(a) summed are irrational*

> Assume that $a$ is a positive integer and not a perfect square. Prove that for any positive integer $n$, the sum
> $$S_n = \{\sqrt{a}\} + \{\sqrt{a}\}^2 + \cdots + \{\sqrt{a}\}^n$$
> is irrational, where $\{x\} = x - [x]$ and $[x]$ denotes the greatest integer less than or equal to $x$.
