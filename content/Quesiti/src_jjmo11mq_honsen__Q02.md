*Game pouring milk and tea to make two good milk teas*

> A cup containing $x$ grams of milk and $y$ grams of black tea is called a good milk tea if $y > 0$ and $\dfrac{y}{x+y} > \dfrac{3}{5}$.
> 
> Now there are $3$ empty cups. Player A and player B, with A going first, alternately perform the following operations:
> 
> - A's operation: pour a total of $60$ grams of milk into some of the cups.
> - B's operation: pour a total of $60$ grams of black tea into some of the cups, then choose one of the $3$ cups and empty its contents.
> 
> B's goal is to make two of the cups be good milk tea at the same time. Regardless of B's actions, can A keep preventing B from achieving this goal forever?
