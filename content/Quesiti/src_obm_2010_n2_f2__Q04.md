*Lottery tickets 100–999; prize = digit sum; minimum tickets to guarantee super-prize*

> In a city, the mayor organized a lottery with tickets numbered from $100$ to $999$. The prize for each ticket is determined by the sum of the digits of the ticket number. To ensure that no one receives more than three prizes, it was established that whoever draws three tickets with equal sums is entitled to a super-prize. What is the minimum number of tickets a citizen must buy to be certain of receiving a super-prize?
