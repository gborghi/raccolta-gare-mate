*Isosceles triangle, parallelogram; prove M is midpoint of DA*

> $ABP$ is an isosceles triangle with $AB = AP$ and $\angle PAB$ acute. $PC$ is the line through $P$ perpendicular to $BP$, and $C$ is a point on this line on the same side of $BP$ as $A$. (You may assume that $C$ is not on the line $AB$.) $D$ completes the parallelogram $ABCD$. $PC$ meets $DA$ at $M$.
> Prove that $M$ is the midpoint of $DA$.
