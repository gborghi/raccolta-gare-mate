# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2019 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 5

*Beautiful sets and a uniform bound on non-multiples*

> For a set $S$ consisting of positive integers, $S$ is called a \emph{beautiful set} if, for any distinct elements $x,y,z$ of $S$, at least one of them is a divisor of $x+y+z$. Prove that there exists an integer $N$ satisfying the following condition, and find the smallest such $N$:
> 
> For every beautiful set $S$, there exists an integer $n_S$ with $n_S\ge 2$ such that the number of elements of $S$ that are not multiples of $n_S$ is at most $N$.
