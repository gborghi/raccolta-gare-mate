*Characterize possible sizes of a perfect finite set of real numbers*

> A finite set $A$ of real numbers is called \emph{perfect} if it has at least two elements and, for every $b \in A$, the set $\{ab \mid a \in A,\, a \neq b\}$ equals $A$. For example, $\left\{1, 2, \tfrac{1}{2}\right\}$ is perfect. However, $\{1,2,3\}$ is not perfect. How many elements can a perfect set have?
> 
> (A) Only 3 or 4 \quad (B) Any quantity congruent to 3 or 4 modulo 4 \\ (C) Any odd quantity \quad (D) Any prime quantity \\ (E) Any quantity greater than 2
