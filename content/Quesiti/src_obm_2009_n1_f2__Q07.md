# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2009 — Nível 1 — Fase 2 — Quesito 7

*Figures built from right triangles with legs 6,8,10 by gluing equal sides; compare perimeters and maximize with three triangles*

> Carlinhos makes paper figures in the shape of right triangles with sides $6$ cm, $8$ cm and $10$ cm. In each triangle, the angle marked with $\circ$ is the smaller one. By making equal sides of these triangles coincide over a sheet, without overlapping, he draws the contour of each figure obtained (thick line), as in the examples alongside. The perimeter of a figure is the length of its contour.
> 
> a) What is the difference between the perimeters of figures $1$ and $2$ of the example?
> 
> b) With figures of three triangles, what is the largest perimeter that can be obtained?
