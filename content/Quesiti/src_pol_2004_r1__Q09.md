*For nonconstant integer polynomials $W_1,\dots,W_n$, some integer $a$ makes all $W_k(a)$ composite.*

> Let be given nonconstant polynomials $W_1(x),W_2(x),\dots,W_n(x)$ with integer coefficients. Prove that there exists an integer $a$ such that the numbers $W_1(a),W_2(a),\dots,W_n(a)$ are all composite.
