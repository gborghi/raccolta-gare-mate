# British Mathematical Olympiad 1998-99 — Round 2 — Quesito 1

*Partition {1,...,n} into two or three parts with equal sums*

> For each positive integer $n$, let $S_n$ denote the set consisting of the first $n$ natural numbers, that is
> $$S_n = \{1, 2, 3, \ldots, n\}.$$
> (i) For which values of $n$ is it possible to express $S_n$ as the union of two disjoint subsets so that the elements in the two subsets have equal sums?
> (ii) For which values of $n$ is it possible to express $S_n$ as the union of three non-empty disjoint subsets so that the elements in the three subsets have equal sums?
