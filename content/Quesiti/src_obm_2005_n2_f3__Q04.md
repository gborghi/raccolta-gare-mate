*Swimmer and log problem: time for log to travel from A to B*

> In his daily swimming training, Esmeraldinho swims several times between two points $A$ and $B$ situated on the same bank of a river, with a constant stroke rate. The current of the river flows from $A$ to $B$, and the current in the opposite direction is against Esmeraldinho. A log carried by the current passes point $A$ at the exact moment that Esmeraldinho sets off from $A$. Esmeraldinho reaches $B$ and immediately returns toward $A$. On the return trip, he crosses the log 6 minutes after leaving $A$. Esmeraldinho then reaches $A$ and immediately sets off again toward $B$, reaching $B$ 5 minutes after passing the log for the second time while going from $B$ to $A$. How many minutes does the log take to travel from $A$ to $B$?
