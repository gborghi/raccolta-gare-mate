*Representation of numbers in two bases; compare sizes via digits*

> Let $a$, $b$ be integers greater than 1, and let $a$ and $b$ be the bases of two number systems. $A_{n-1}$ and $A_n$ are numbers in the system with base $a$; $B_{n-1}$ and $B_n$ are the numbers in the system with base $b$; these are related as follows:
> $$A_n = x_n x_{n-1} \cdots x_1 x_0, \quad A_{n-1} = x_{n-1} x_{n-2} \cdots x_1 x_0,$$
> $$B_n = x_n x_{n-1} \cdots x_1 x_0, \quad B_{n-1} = x_{n-1} x_{n-2} \cdots x_1 x_0,$$
> $$x_n \neq 0, \quad x_{n-1} \neq 0.$$
> Prove that
> $$\frac{A_{n-1}}{A_n} < \frac{B_{n-1}}{B_n} \text{ if and only if } a > b.$$
