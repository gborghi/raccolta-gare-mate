*Partition lattice points with at least one coordinate not divisible by 4 into unit-distance pairs.*

> In a coordinate plane, consider the set of points with integer coordinates at least one of which is not divisible by 4. Prove that these points can be partitioned into pairs such that the distance between points in each pair equals 1.
> 
> In other words, an infinite chessboard, whose cells with both coordinates divisible by 4 are cut out, cannot be tiled by dominoes.
