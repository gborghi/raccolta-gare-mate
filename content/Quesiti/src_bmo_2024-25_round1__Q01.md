# British Mathematical Olympiad 2024-25 — Round 1 — Quesito 1

*Find happy n in [3,12]: circle of n integers, neighbours divide each other*

> We say that the positive integer $n \ge 3$ is happy if it is possible to arrange $n$ different positive integers in a circle such that two conditions are satisfied:
> (a) If integers $a$ and $b$ are neighbours, then either $a$ divides $b$ or $b$ divides $a$;
> (b) If different integers $a$ and $y$ are in the circle but are not neighbours, then neither divides the other.
> 
> Determine, with proof, which positive integers $n$ in the range $3 \le n \le 12$ are happy.
