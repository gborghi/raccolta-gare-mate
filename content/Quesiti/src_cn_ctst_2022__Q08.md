*Two isogonal conjugates on circumcircle: pedal lines meet on nine-point circle*

> In the oblique $\triangle ABC$ ($BC > AC > AB$). Let $P_1$, $P_2$ be two points in the plane satisfying: for $i = 1, 2$, the lines $AP_i$, $BP_i$, and $CP_i$ intersect the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ again at $D_i$, $E_i$, and $F_i$ respectively, with $D_i$ on arc $BC$ not containing $A$. It is known that the line $P_1P_2$ intersects the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ at two points $Q_1$ and $Q_2$. For $i = 1, 2$, let $X_i$ and $Y_i$ be the projections of $Q_i$ onto sides $AB$ and $AC$, respectively. The lines $X_1Y_1$ and $X_2Y_2$ meet at $W$. Prove that $W$ lies on the nine-point circle of $\triangle ABC$.
