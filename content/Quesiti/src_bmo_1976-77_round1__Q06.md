# British Mathematical Olympiad 1976-77 — Round 1 — Quesito 6

*Decompose 26(x+...+x^n) as sum of permutation polynomials; find all n*

> The polynomial $26(x + x^2 + x^3 + \cdots + x^n)$, where $n > 1$, is to be decomposed into a sum of polynomials, not necessarily all different. Each of these polynomials is to be of the form $a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + a_3 x^3 + \cdots + a_n x^n$ where each $a_i$ is one of the numbers $1, 2, 3, \ldots, n$ and no two $a_i$ are equal.
> Find all the values of $n$ for which this decomposition is possible.
