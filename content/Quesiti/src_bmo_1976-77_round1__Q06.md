# British Mathematical Olympiad 1976-77 — Round 1 — Quesito 6

*Decompose polynomial with exponents from {1,...,n} into factors; two exponents equal*

> The polynomial $26(a_1 x^{a_2} + a_2 x^{a_3} + \cdots + a_{n-1} x^{a_n})$, where $n > 1$, is to be decomposed into a product of (not necessarily all) different polynomials. Each of these polynomials is to be of the form $a_1 x^{a_1} + a_2 x^{a_2} + \cdots + a_n x^{a_n}$ where each $a_k$ is one of the numbers $1, 2, 3, \ldots, n$ and no two $a_k$ are equal.
> Find all such decompositions.
