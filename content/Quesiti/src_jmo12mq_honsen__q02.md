*Flipping coins on a circle by parity operation*

> Let $n$ be a natural number with $n \ge 3$. There are $n$ coins, each distinguishable as heads or tails, arranged around a circle. Consider the "operation" of simultaneously flipping all coins that satisfy the following condition. Condition: among the three coins consisting of that coin together with the two coins on either side of it, the number of coins showing heads is odd. Initially, exactly one of the coins is showing heads. Starting from this state, the above operation is repeated several times. Answer the following questions. (1) Show that if $n$ is odd, it is impossible for all coins to become tails (face down). (2) Determine for which $n$ it is possible, after performing the operation some number of times, for all coins to be facing down. Moreover, in those cases, express in terms of $n$ after how many operations all coins first become face down.
