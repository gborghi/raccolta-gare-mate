*Circumradius of triangle ADY in terms of a,b,c*

> There is a triangle $ABC$ with $AB > AC$. Let $D$ be the intersection of the bisector of $\angle BAC$ with side $BC$. Furthermore, let $E$ and $F$ be the intersections of the perpendicular bisector of segment $AD$ with sides $AB$ and $AC$ respectively, and take a point $X$ on side $BC$ such that $BX : XC = BE : CF$. Also, let $Y$ be the intersection of the circumcircle of triangle $ABC$ with line $AX$ other than $A$, and set $BC=a$, $CA=b$, $AB=c$. Express the radius of the circumcircle of triangle $ADY$ in terms of $a$, $b$, $c$. Here, $ZW$ denotes the length of segment $ZW$.
