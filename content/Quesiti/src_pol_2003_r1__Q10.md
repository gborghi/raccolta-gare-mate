*Cards 1..52; a shuffle is a permutation pi with pi(i)<pi(i+1) except at one index m. Prove or disprove any order reachable from any other in at most 5 shuffles.*

> On the table there are $52$ cards numbered with the numbers $1,2,\ldots,52$. A permutation $\pi$ of the set $\{1,2,\ldots,52\}$ is called a shuffle if there is a number $1\le m\le 51$ such that $\pi(i)<\pi(i+1)$ for $i=1,2,\ldots,m-1,m+1,\ldots,51$. Prove or disprove that starting with cards in an arbitrary order we can arrange them in any other order in at most $5$ shuffles.
