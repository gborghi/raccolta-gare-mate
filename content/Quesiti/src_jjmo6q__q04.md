*Count prices payable with 1000, 100, 10, 1 yen coins minimizing change*

> Taro has one 1000-yen bill, one 100-yen coin, one 10-yen coin, and one 1-yen coin, and he uses all four of them to buy an item. How many possible prices can the item have?
> 
> The condition is that Taro's payment has no amount in common (as coins/bills) with the change he receives; among all payment methods satisfying this condition, he chooses one that minimises the number of coins/bills he holds after the transaction (i.e., minimises the total number of his remaining bills/coins after receiving change). Furthermore, change is always given with the minimum number of coins/bills, and it is also possible that the change is 0 yen.
