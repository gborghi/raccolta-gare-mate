# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2015 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 3

*Ants on n x n board visiting every cell, minimal black cells*

> Let $n,k$ be positive integers with $n \ge k$. There is an $n \times n$ grid of cells, and each cell is colored either white or black. There are $k$ ants. Repeatedly, each ant moves to an adjacent cell. On a white cell an ant can only move straight ahead, while on a black cell an ant may choose freely which adjacent cell to move to next. Suppose that by coloring some $m$ cells black and the rest white, and by suitably choosing the initial placement of the ants and their initial directions of movement, it can be arranged that every cell of the grid is visited by at least one ant. Find the smallest possible value of $m$ for which such an arrangement is possible.
