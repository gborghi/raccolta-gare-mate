*An increasing function from N to R that is additive on products of naturals must be a logarithm.*

> An increasing function $f:\mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{R}$ satisfies
> $$f(kl)=f(k)+f(l)\quad\text{for all }k,l\in\mathbb{N}.$$
> Show that there is a real number $p>1$ such that $f(n)=\log_p n$ for all $n$.
