*Incircle tangent points and midpoints define lines; prove concurrence of AX, BY, CZ*

> Let $\omega$ be the incircle of $\triangle ABC$, and let $A'$, $B'$, $C'$ be the points where $\omega$ is tangent to sides $BC$, $CA$, $AB$ respectively. Let $B_1$ and $C_1$ be points on sides $CA$ and $AB$ (or their extensions) such that lines $B'C_1$, $C'A_1$, $A'B_1$ satisfy certain parallelism conditions. More precisely: let $6$ points $B_1$, $C_1$, $A_1$ be defined on sides $BC$, $CA$, $AB$ respectively so that all of lines $B_1C_1$, $C_1A_1$, $A_1B_1$ are parallel to $BC$, $CA$, $AB$ respectively. Line $B'C'$ meets line $AB$ at $X$; line $C'A'$ meets line $BC$ at $Y$; line $A'B'$ meets line $CA$ at $Z$. Prove that the three lines $AX$, $BY$, $CZ$ are concurrent.

Linea $B'C'$ incontra la linea $AB$ a $X$; linea $C'A'$ incontra la linea $BC$ a $Y$; linea $A'B'$ incontra la linea $CA$ a $Z$. Prove che le tre linee $AX$, $BY$ e $CZ$ sono simultanee.
