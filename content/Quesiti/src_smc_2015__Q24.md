*Largest N cards (integers 1-25) that can be placed in a row so every adjacent pair shares a prime factor*

> Peter has 25 cards, each printed with a different integer from $1$ to $25$. He wishes to place $N$ cards in a single row so that the numbers on every pair of adjacent cards share a prime factor in common. What is the largest value of $N$ for which this is possible?
>
> - **(A)** $16$
> - **(B)** $18$
> - **(C)** $20$
> - **(D)** $22$
> - **(E)** $24$
