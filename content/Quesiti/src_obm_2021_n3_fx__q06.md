*Bicentric n-gon: circle tangent to circumcircles of semicircle-intersection triangles*

> Let $n \ge 5$ be an integer. The polygon $P = A_1 A_2 \cdots A_n$ is *bicentric*, that is, it has both an inscribed circle and a circumscribed circle. Define $A_{i+n} = A_i$ for all $i$ (all indices are taken modulo $n$). Suppose that for each $i$, $1 \le i \le n$, the semicircles on segments $A_{i-1}A_i$ and $A_i A_{i+1}$ (as diameters, drawn on the same side) meet at the point $B_i$. Let $\omega_i$ be the circumcircle of triangle $B_i A_i A_{i+1}$. Prove that there exists a circle tangent simultaneously to all $n$ circles $\omega_i$, $1 \le i \le n$.
