*Sequence a_n = (n+2)^2 - (n+3); find index of first non-prime term*

> The natural numbers greater than 2, each decreased by 2 more than itself (i.e., $a_n = (n+2)^2 - (n+3)$ for $n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$), form the sequence $5, 11, 19, 29, 41, 55, \ldots$ The first element of this sequence that is *not* prime is the:
> 
> (A) 4th \quad (B) 10th \quad (C) 6th \quad (D) 9th \quad (E) 7th
