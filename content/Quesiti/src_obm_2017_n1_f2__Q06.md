# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2017 — Nível 1 — Fase 2 — Quesito 6

*Words over {a,b}: m-equivalence of two words of length l via subword deletion*

> We consider here words over the alphabet $\{a, b\}$: sequences of $a$'s and $b$'s of finite length. We write $v \preceq w$ if $v$ is a *subword* of $w$, that is, $v$ can be obtained from $w$ by deleting some letters (for example, $aba \preceq abba$). We say that two words $x$ and $y$ are *$m$-equivalent* if $u \preceq x$ as well as $u \preceq y$, or neither $u \preceq x$ nor $u \preceq y$, for every word $u$ with $|u| \le m$ (i.e., whether $u$ is a subword of $x$ is the same as whether $u$ is a subword of $y$, for all words $u$ of length at most $m$).
> 
> Let $l$ and $m$ be positive integers.
> 
> a) Prove that if $2m \le l$, then there exist two distinct words $x$ and $y$ of length $l$ that are $m$-equivalent.
> 
> b) Prove that if $2m > l$, then two distinct words $x$ and $y$ of length $l$ cannot be $m$-equivalent.
