*Place 4 black and 4 white pieces on 8x8 chessboard, one per row/column, count arrangements*

> Consider an $8 \times 8$ chessboard (with alternating black and white squares in the usual coloring). Place $4$ black pieces and $4$ white pieces on the board, one piece per row and one piece per column (so exactly $8$ cells are occupied), subject to the following condition: in each row and each column there is exactly $1$ piece, which is placed on a black cell if it is a black piece and on a white cell if it is a white piece.
> 
> Arrangements that coincide after rotation or reflection are still counted as distinct. How many such arrangements are there?
