*X is reflection of B across line r (perpendicular bisector setup); X' Y' Z' are reflections of vertices of triangle XYZ across the opposite sides; compute the area of triangle X'Y'Z'*

> In the drawing, the line $r$ is perpendicular to the segment $AB$ and passes through its midpoint $M$. We say that $X$ is the symmetric (reflection) of $B$ with respect to the line $r$ when $r$ is the perpendicular bisector of segment $BX$ (analogously for the symmetric of a point with respect to segment $PQ$).
> 
> Let the triangle $X'Y'Z'$ be such that $X'$ is the symmetric of $X$ with respect to the side $YZ$, $Y'$ is the symmetric of $Y$ with respect to the side $XZ$, and $Z'$ is the symmetric of $Z$ with respect to the side $XY$.
> 
> Compute the area of triangle $X'Y'Z'$.
