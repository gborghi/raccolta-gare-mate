*Smallest positive N where product formula with nearest perfect squares holds; find sum of squares of digits*

> For a positive integer $n$, let $\langle n \rangle$ denote the perfect square integer closest to $n$. For example, $\langle 74 \rangle = 81$, $\langle 18 \rangle = 16$. If $N$ is the smallest positive integer such that $$\langle 91 \rangle \cdot \langle 120 \rangle \cdot \langle 143 \rangle \cdot \langle 180 \rangle \cdot N = 91 \cdot 120 \cdot 143 \cdot 180 \cdot N,$$ find the sum of the squares of the digits of $N$.
