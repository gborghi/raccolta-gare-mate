*Sequence a_1=3, a_{n+1}=a_n^2+a_n-1; prove infinitely many prime divisors*

> Define a sequence $(a_n)$ by $a_1 = 3$ and $a_{n+1} = a_n^2 + a_n - 1$ for $n \geq 1$.
> 
> a) Prove that there are infinitely many prime numbers which divide at least one term of the sequence.
> 
> b) Prove that there are infinitely many prime numbers which divide no term of the sequence.
