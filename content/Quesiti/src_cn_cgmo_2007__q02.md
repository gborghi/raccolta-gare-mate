*With D, E, F on the sides of acute triangle ABC and each of AD, BE, CF passing through the circumcenter, if any two of six side-segment ratios are integers, then ABC is isosceles.*

> Let $ABC$ be an acute triangle. Points $D$, $E$ and $F$ lie on segments $BC$, $CA$ and $AB$ respectively, and each of the three segments $AD$, $BE$ and $CF$ contains the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$. Prove that if any two of the ratios $\frac{BD}{DC}, \frac{CE}{EA}, \frac{AF}{FB}, \frac{BF}{FA}, \frac{CD}{DB}, \frac{AE}{EC}$ are integers, then triangle $ABC$ is isosceles.
