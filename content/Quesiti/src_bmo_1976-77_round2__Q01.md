*Bound quadratic on interval given values at n points*

> $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$ are given points of the interval $\left[-\tfrac{1}{2}, 1\right]$ such that
> $$0 < x_1 < x_2 < \cdots < x_{k-1} \leq \tfrac{1}{2} \leq x_k \quad (1 < k < n).$$
> The quadratic function $f$, of the form
> $$f(x) = x^2 + bx + c$$
> where $a$, $b$, $c$ are real constants, satisfies the condition
> $$|f(x_i)| \leq 1 \quad (0 \leq i \leq k).$$
> Prove that
> $$|f(x)| \leq \tfrac{3}{2}$$
> for all $x$ in $\left[-\tfrac{1}{2}, 1\right]$. Show by means of an example that this proposition becomes false if $\dfrac{3}{2}$ is replaced by any smaller number.
