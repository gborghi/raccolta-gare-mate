*Point on segment between two lines through O; minimize area then perimeter*

> Consider two lines $D_1$ and $D_2$ intersecting at $O$, and a point $M$ belonging to neither line. Consider two variable points $A$ on $D_1$ and $B$ on $D_2$ such that $M$ belongs to the segment $[A, B]$. (Questions 1 and 2 are independent.)
> 
> (1) Show that there exists a position of points $A$ and $B$ for which the area of triangle $OAB$ is minimal. Construct the points $A$ and $B$ thus determined.
> 
> (2) Show that there exists a position of points $A$ and $B$ for which the perimeter of triangle $OAB$ is minimal, and that in this case the perimeters of triangles $OAM$ and $OBM$ are equal, and moreover the following relation holds:
> $$\frac{AM}{\tan\dfrac{\widehat{OAM}}{2}} = \frac{BM}{\tan\dfrac{\widehat{OBM}}{2}}.$$
> Construct the points $A$ and $B$ thus determined.
