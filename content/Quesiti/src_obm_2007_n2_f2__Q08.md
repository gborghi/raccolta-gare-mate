# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2007 — Nível 2 — Fase 2 — Quesito 8

*Count divisors of 23^5-23 and prove n^3-n is always a multiple of 30.*

> (Part B, Problem 3.) a) Let $N=23^5-23=23\cdot(23^4-1)=23\cdot(23^2+1)(23^2-1)=23\cdot(23^2+1)(23+1)(23-1)=23\cdot530\cdot24\cdot22=2^5\cdot3\cdot5\cdot11\cdot23\cdot53$. Determine the number of positive divisors of $N$ (which is $6\times2\times2\times2\times2=192$). b) Let $N=n^3-n=n(n-1)(n+1)$. Show that $N$ is always a multiple of $30$. (Among the three consecutive integers $n-1,n,n+1$ one is even and one is a multiple of $3$; if none is a multiple of $5$ then $n\equiv2$ or $3\pmod5$, and $n^2+1$ is a multiple of $5$, so $n^3-n$ is a multiple of $5$. Hence $N$ is a multiple of $2\cdot3\cdot5=30$.) [Source is the solutions sheet; statement reconstructed from the solution.
