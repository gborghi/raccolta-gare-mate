*Permutation of points on half-disk boundary minimising sum of squared distances*

> Let $X = \{(x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 : x \ge 0,\, x^2 + y^2 = 1\} \cup \{(x,0) : -1 \le x \le 1\}$ be the boundary of the closed half-disk of radius $1$.
> 
> a) Let $n > 1$ be an integer and $P_1, P_2, \ldots, P_n \in X$. Prove that there exists a permutation $\sigma : \{1, 2, \ldots, n\} \to \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ such that
> $$\sum_{i=1}^{n} |P_{\sigma(i+1)} - P_{\sigma(i)}|^2 \le 8,$$
> where we define $\sigma(n+1) = \sigma(1)$.
> 
> b) Determine all subsets $\{P_1, P_2, \ldots, P_n\} \subset X$ such that for any permutation $\sigma : \{1, 2, \ldots, n\} \to \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$,
> $$\sum_{i=1}^{n} |P_{\sigma(i+1)} - P_{\sigma(i)}|^2 \ge 8$$
> (where $\sigma(n+1) = \sigma(1)$).
