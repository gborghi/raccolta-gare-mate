*Maximum size of a set of permutations with no triple all-between property*

> Let $n$ be an integer greater than or equal to 1. For a permutation $p = (p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n)$ of $(1, 2, \ldots, n)$, we say that $j$ lies between $i$ and $k$ if $i < j < k$ or $k < j < i$. (For example, in the permutation $(1, 3, 2, 4)$, $3$ lies between $1$ and $4$, and $3$ does not lie between $1$ and $2$.) Let $S = \{p^{(1)}, p^{(2)}, \ldots, p^{(m)}\}$ be a set of $m$ distinct permutations of $(1, 2, \ldots, n)$ such that among every three distinct numbers in $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$, one of these numbers does not lie between the other two in every permutation $p \in S$. Determine the maximum value of $m$.
