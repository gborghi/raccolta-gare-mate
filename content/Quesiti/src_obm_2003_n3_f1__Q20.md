# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2003 — Nível 3 — Fase 1 — Quesito 20

*Find digit sum of N expressible as 9 consecutive integers and as 11 consecutive integers*

> There exist positive integers $n_1$ and $n_2$ such that
> $$N = (n_1 - 4) + (n_1 - 3) + (n_1 - 2) + (n_1 - 1) + n_1 + (n_1 + 1) + (n_1 + 2) + (n_1 + 3) + (n_1 + 4)$$
> (a sum of $9$ consecutive integers centered at $n_1$) and also
> $$N = (n_2 - 5) + (n_2 - 4) + \cdots + (n_2 + 5)$$
> (a sum of $11$ consecutive integers centered at $n_2$). The smallest such positive $N$ is $N = 495$. The sum of the digits of $N$ is:
> 
> A) $9$ \quad B) $18$ \quad C) $22$ \quad D) $27$ \quad E) $36$
