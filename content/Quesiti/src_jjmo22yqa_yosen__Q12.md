*Find maximum N such that 2N positive integers a_i, x_i satisfy four divisibility and concatenation conditions*

> Let $N$ be a positive integer. Find the maximum value of $N$ such that there exist $2N$ positive integers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_N, x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_N$ satisfying all of the following conditions:
> 
> - Each $x_i$ is a divisor of $9 \cdot 10^{100}$.
> - $x_i \ne x_{i+1}$ for every integer $i$ with $1 \le i \le N - 1$.
> - For every integer $i$ with $1 \le i \le N - 1$, $a_{i+1}$ is obtained by appending $x_i$ to the end of $a_i$.
> - For every integer $i$ with $1 \le i \le N$, $a_i$ is a multiple of $x_i$.
> 
> (For positive integers $s$ and $t$, appending $t$ to the end of $s$ means writing the decimal representation of $s$ followed immediately by the decimal representation of $t$. For example, appending $22$ to $2024$ gives $202422$.)
