*Permutation of positive integers: show f maps some AP to an AP*

> Let $f : \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}$ be a bijection from the set of positive integers $\mathbb{N}$ to itself.
> (i) Show that there is an arithmetic progression of positive integers $a, a+d, a+2d, \ldots$ ($d > 0$) such that $f(a), f(a+d), f(a+2d), \ldots$ is also an arithmetic progression.
> (ii) Is there such a bijection $f$ that maps every arithmetic progression to an arithmetic progression? How many such functions $f$ are there?
