*Circumscribed circle midpoints, inner center, concyclicity proof*

> (30 marks) As shown in Fig. 1, $M$, $N$ are the midpoints of arcs $\widehat{BC}$, $\widehat{AC}$ respectively, which are on the circumscribed circle of an acute triangle $\triangle ABC$ ($\angle A < \angle B$). Through point $C$ draw $PC \perp MN$, intercepting the circle $\Gamma$ at point $P$. $I$ is the inner center of $\triangle ABC$. Extend line $PI$ to intercept $\Gamma$ at point $T$.
> 
> (1) Prove that $MP > MT = NP > NT$.
> 
> (2) For an arbitrary point $Q$ ($Q \neq A, T, B$) on arc $\widehat{AB}$ (not containing $C$), denote the inner centers of $\triangle AQC$, $\triangle QCB$ by $I_1$, $I_2$ respectively. Prove that $Q$, $I_1$, $I_2$, $T$ are concyclic.
