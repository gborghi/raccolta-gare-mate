*6000 students' grades; after a replacement the mean rises by 1/10; bound a count difference*

> Six thousand students took an exam and all obtained a grade that is an integer between $0$ and $8$ ($0$ and $8$ included). Vincent decides to replace all the grades equal to $1$, $2$ and $3$ by $0$ and all the grades equal to $5$, $6$ or $7$ by $8$. The other grades are unchanged. After this change, the mean of the scores increased by $\frac{1}{10}$.
> 
> Prove that there exist two integers $a$ and $b$ such that the difference between the number of students having obtained the grade $a$ and the number of students having obtained the grade $b$, before the modifications carried out by Vincent, is at least $100$.
