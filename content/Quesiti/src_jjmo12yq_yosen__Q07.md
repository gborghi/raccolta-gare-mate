# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2014 — Yosen (Preliminary) — Quesito 7

*Count placements of six 1x2 boards with two covered cells per row/column*

> There is a $6 \times 6$ grid. Six rectangular boards of dimensions $1 \times 2$ are placed, each covering exactly $2$ cells, so that they do not overlap, in such a way that in every row and in every column exactly $2$ cells are covered by boards. In how many ways can this be done? Here, the six boards are indistinguishable, and placements that coincide under a rotation or reflection of the $6 \times 6$ grid are counted as different.
