*Return to the initial problem: product of sums of two squares, induction that every prime dividing n^2+1 is a sum of two squares, infinitude, and rationality of L(n,k)*

> Return to the initial problem.
> 
> 1. Let $n$ and $m$ be two natural integers sum of two squares, $n = a^2 + b^2$, $m = c^2 + d^2$. By introducing the complex numbers $a + ib$ and $c + id$ and by considering $n = |a + ib|^2$ and $m = |c + id|^2$, show that the product $mn$ is an integer sum of two squares and give explicitly a decomposition of it as a function of $a$, $b$, $c$ and $d$.
> 
> 2. We propose to demonstrate, for every integer $n$ strictly positive, the proposition $\mathcal{P}(n)$ following:
>    $\mathcal{P}(n)$: "every prime number that divides $n^2 + 1$ is a sum of two squares".
>    For this, one proceeds by recurrence on $n$.
>    a. Show that $\mathcal{P}(1)$, $\mathcal{P}(2)$ and $\mathcal{P}(3)$ are true.
>    b. Let $n$ be an integer strictly greater than $1$. One supposes the proposition $\mathcal{P}(j)$ true for every integer $j$ such that $1 \le j \le n-1$ and one considers a prime number $p$ that divides $n^2 + 1$.
>       i. Show that $p$ is different from $n$.
>       ii. Suppose $p < n$. Show that $p$ divides $(n-p)^2 + 1$.
>       iii. Suppose $p > n$ and $p < n^2 + 1$. Show that the other prime divisors of $n^2 + 1$ are strictly less than $n$. Deduce, by discussing according to the parity of $n$, that $p$ is congruent to $1$ modulo $4$.
>       iv. Show that $p$ is a sum of two squares.
>    c. Conclude.
> 
> 3.
>    a. For $s$ an integer greater than or equal to $2$, one denotes $p_s$ the smallest prime divisor of the number $(s!)^2 + 1$. Show that $p_s > s$ and that $p_s$ is a sum of two squares.
>    b. Deduce that there exists an infinity of prime numbers sum of two squares.
> 
> 4.
>    a. Show that there exists an infinity of couples of integers $(n,k)$ with $1 \le k < n$ such that $L(n,k)$ is rational.
>    b. Determine an integer $n$ such that there exist several values of $k$ for which $L(n,k)$ is rational.
