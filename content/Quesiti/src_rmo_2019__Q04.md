*Extend a 3xk array using 1..3k so row sums and row sums of squares are all equal.*

> Consider the following $3 \times 2$ array formed by using the numbers $1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6$:
> $$\begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} \\ a_{31} & a_{32} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 6 \\ 2 & 5 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}.$$
> Observe that all row sums are equal, but the sum of the squares is not the same for each row. Extend the above array to a $3 \times k$ array $(a_{ij})_{3 \times k}$ for a suitable $k$, adding more columns, using the numbers $7, 8, 9, \ldots, 3k$ such that
> $$\sum_{j=1}^{k} a_{1j} = \sum_{j=1}^{k} a_{2j} = \sum_{j=1}^{k} a_{3j} \quad \text{and} \quad \sum_{j=1}^{k} (a_{1j})^2 = \sum_{j=1}^{k} (a_{2j})^2 = \sum_{j=1}^{k} (a_{3j})^2.$$
