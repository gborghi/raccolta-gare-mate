*If a cubic has three distinct real roots, a related cubic does too*

> Prove that if the polynomial $x^3 + ax^2 + bx + c$ has three distinct real roots, then so does the polynomial
> $$x^3 + ax^2 + \frac{1}{4}(a^2 + b)x + \frac{1}{8}(ab - c).$$
