*Prove a triple-product inequality for real numbers x,y,z >= 1.*

> Let $x$, $y$, $z$ be real numbers greater than or equal to $1$. Prove that
> $$(x^2 - 2x + 2)(y^2 - 2y + 2)(z^2 - 2z + 2) \le (xyz)^2 - 2xyz + 2.$$
> (Posed by Xiong Bin)
