*Count 3-element subsets of {1,...,n} with intersection and empty conditions*

> Let $X = \{A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_k\}$ be a collection of 3-element subsets of $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ such that:
> (i) $A_i$ and $A_j$ have non-empty intersection for every $i, j$,
> (ii) the intersection of all the elements of $X$ is the empty set.
> Show that $n \le 100$. How many such $X$ are there when $n = 100$?
