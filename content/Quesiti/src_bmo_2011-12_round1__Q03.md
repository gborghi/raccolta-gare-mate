*Intersection of two circles through P tangent to S is fixed*

> Consider a circle $S$. The point $P$ lies outside $S$ and a line is drawn through $P$, cutting $S$ at distinct points $X$ and $Y$. Circles $S_1$ and $S_2$ are drawn through $P$ which are tangent to $S$ at $X$ and $Y$ respectively. Show that the intersection (other than $P$) of $S_1$ and $S_2$ is independent of the positions of $P$, $X$ and $Y$.
