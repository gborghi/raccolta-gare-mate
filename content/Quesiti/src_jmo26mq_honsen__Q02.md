*Cyclic quadrilateral: angle bisectors, midpoints, parallel line*

> A cyclic quadrilateral $ABCD$ is inscribed in a circle, satisfying $AB : AD = CD : CB$. Let $X$ be the intersection of diagonals $AC$ and $BD$, and let $Y$ be the intersection of lines $AB$ and $CD$. Let $E$, $F$, $G$, $H$ be the midpoints of $AB$, $BC$, $CD$, $DA$ respectively. Let $S$ be the intersection of the angle bisector of $\angle AXB$ with segment $EG$, and let $T$ be the intersection of the angle bisector of $\angle AYD$ with segment $FH$. Prove that line $ST$ is parallel to diagonal $BD$.
> 
> (Here, $UV$ denotes the length of segment $UV$.)
