# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2015 — Nível 3 — Fase 2 — Quesito 1

*Esmeralda solves bx=a instead of ax=b; her root is the correct root minus 60; find m+n from correct root m+sqrt(n)*

> (Part A) Professor Piraldo gave Esmeralda an equation of the form $ax = b$, where $a$ and $b$ are real. Esmeralda makes a mistake and solves the equation $bx = a$, obtaining a solution that is equal to the correct solution minus $60$. If the correct solution is of the form $m + \sqrt{n}$ with $m$ and $n$ integers, what is the value of $m + n$?
