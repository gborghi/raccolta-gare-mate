*Chess-knight game on m×n board; determine winner*

> Arnaldo and Bernaldo play the following game on an $m \times n$ board, $m, n \ge 2$. Arnaldo starts by choosing a square and placing a chess knight on it; then Arnaldo and Bernaldo alternately move the knight, starting with Bernaldo, with the restriction that the knight may not land on a square that has already been visited. The player who cannot move the knight loses. Determine, in terms of $m$ and $n$, which player has a winning strategy, regardless of the other player's moves, and show how he should play to win.
> 
> *Remark:* Each move of a chess knight consists of two squares in the vertical or horizontal direction followed by one square in the perpendicular direction.
