# British Mathematical Olympiad 2024-25 — Round 1 — Quesito 2

*Card trick with n cards: find n for which initial+final sum is always 0*

> A magician performs a trick with $n$ cards that are numbered $1$ to $n$. The magician performs the trick by putting the cards in an order of her choosing. Then she challenges a member of the audience to write an integer on a board. The magician turns over the cards one by one in their pre-arranged order. Each time the magician turns over a card, the audience member multiplies the number on the board by $-1$, adds it to the number on the card, writes the result on the board, and erases the old number. The magician guarantees that, no matter which initial integer is chosen, the initial and final numbers will sum to $0$.
> 
> Determine for which natural numbers $n$ the magician can perform the trick. You must both prove that the trick is possible for the numbers you claim, and prove that it is not possible for any other numbers.
