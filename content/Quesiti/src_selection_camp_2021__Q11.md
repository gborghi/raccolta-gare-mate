# Japan IMO Selection Camp 2021 — Selection Camp — Quesito 11

*Two externally tangent circles yield collinear points A, X, Y*

> Let $\Gamma$ be the circumcircle of an acute triangle $ABC$ and let $I$ be its incenter. Let $\omega_B$ and $\omega_C$ be two circles that are externally tangent to each other. The circle $\omega_B$ is tangent to side $AC$, and the circle $\omega_C$ is tangent to side $AB$. Moreover $\omega_B$ is tangent to line $AB$ at a point $M$, and $\omega_C$ is tangent to line $AC$ at a point $N$. Lines $PM$ and $QN$ meet at the point $X$, and $\omega_B$ and $\omega_C$ meet at the point $Y$. Prove that $A$, $X$, $Y$ lie on a single line.
