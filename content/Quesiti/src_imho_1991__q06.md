*Construct bounded sequence with |x_i - x_j||i-j|^a ≥ 1*

> An infinite sequence $x_0, x_1, x_2, \ldots$ of real numbers is said to be *bounded* if there is a constant $C$ such that $|x_i| \le C$ for every $i \ge 0$.
> 
> Given any real number $a > 1$, construct a bounded infinite sequence $x_0, x_1, x_2, \ldots$ such that
> $$|x_i - x_j| \cdot |i - j|^a \ge 1$$
> for every pair of distinct nonnegative integers $i$, $j$.
