*Triangle ABC with X,Y,Z on sides at ratio 2; ratio of area XYZ to triangle of medians.*

> Let $ABC$ be a triangle and $X$, $Y$, $Z$ points on the sides $BC$, $CA$, $AB$ such that $\frac{CX}{XB}=\frac{AY}{YC}=\frac{BZ}{ZA}=2$. What is the ratio between the area of triangle $XYZ$ and the area of the triangle whose sides are congruent to the medians of $ABC$? (Note: the medians of a triangle are the segments joining the vertices to the midpoints of the opposite sides.)
>
> - **(A)** $\frac{2}{3}$
> - **(B)** $\frac{1}{2}$
> - **(C)** $\frac{4}{9}$
> - **(D)** $\frac{1}{3}$
> - **(E)** $\frac{1}{4}$
