# Indian National Mathematical Olympiad 1991 — Quesito 5

*Triangle with incenter I, points X,Y on AB,AC: collinearity and angle*

> In a triangle $ABC$ with incenter $I$, points $X$, $Y$ are taken on the segments $AB$, $AC$ respectively such that $BX \cdot AB = IB^2$ and $CY \cdot AC = IC^2$. Given that the points $X$, $I$, $Y$ are collinear, find the possible values of $\angle A$.
