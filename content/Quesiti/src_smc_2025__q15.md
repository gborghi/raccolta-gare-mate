*Integer n with 1 < n < 99; P = |1 - n|, Q = |99 - n|, R = |P - Q|; for how many n is R prime?*

> The integer $n$ is such that $1 < n < 99$. $P$ is the difference between 1 and $n$, $Q$ is the difference between 99 and $n$, and $R$ is the difference between $P$ and $Q$. For how many values of $n$ is $R$ a prime number?
>
> - **(A)** 0
> - **(B)** 2
> - **(C)** 4
> - **(D)** 8
> - **(E)** 99
