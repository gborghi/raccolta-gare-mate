# British Mathematical Olympiad 1996-97 — Round 1 — Quesito 1

*Reverse digits of N equals 4N+3; find all four-digit N*

> $N$ is a four-digit integer, not ending in zero, and $B(N)$ is the four-digit integer obtained by reversing the digits of $N$. For example, $B(3275) = 5723$.
> 
> Determine all such integers $N$ for which $B(N) = 4N + 3$.
