*In cyclic quadrilateral ABCD with diagonals meeting at P and rays AD, BC meeting at Q, the bisector of angle BQA meets AC at R and the bisector of angle APD meets AD at S; prove RS parallel to CD.*

> Consider a cyclic quadrilateral $ABCD$. The diagonals $AC$ and $BD$ meet at $P$, and the rays $AD$ and $BC$ meet at $Q$. The internal angle bisector of angle $\angle BQA$ meets $AC$ at $R$ and the internal angle bisector of angle $\angle APD$ meets $AD$ at $S$. Prove that $RS$ is parallel to $CD$.
