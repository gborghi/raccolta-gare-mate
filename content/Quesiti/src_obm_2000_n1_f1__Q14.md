*Emir Abdel Azir sons: multiples logic problem*

> Emir Abdel Aziz was famous for having many sons. He had more than $39$ sons, including twins. In fact, the historian Ahmed Aab affirms in two of his books that all his sons who were twins were duplets, except $39$; they were triplets, except $39$; all were quadruplets, except $39$. The number of sons of the Emir is:
> 
> (A) $111$ \quad (B) $48$ \quad (C) $51$ \quad (D) $78$ \quad (E) $75$
