*Lattice points on segments of a periodic integer polynomial orbit are constant*

> Let $P(x,y)$ and $Q(x,y)$ be two-variable polynomials with integer coefficients, and let $a_0, b_0$ be integers. Define sequences $a_n, b_n$ by $$a_{n+1}=P(a_n,b_n), \qquad b_{n+1}=Q(a_n,b_n) \qquad (n=0,1,2,\ldots).$$ Suppose that $(a_1,b_1)\neq(a_0,b_0)$ but there exists a positive integer $k$ with $(a_k,b_k)=(a_0,b_0)$. Prove that the number of points in the plane lying on the segment whose endpoints are $(a_n,b_n)$ and $(a_{n+1},b_{n+1})$ and having both $x$-coordinate and $y$-coordinate integers does not depend on $n$.
