*Only finitely many subsets S of {1,...,n} with |S|≥√n+1 and all products xy being perfect powers*

> Prove that there are only finitely many positive integers $n$ for which there exists a subset $S \subseteq \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ satisfying the following two conditions:
> \begin{itemize}
> \item The number of elements of $S$ is at least $[\sqrt{n}] + 1$.
> \item For any two elements $x, y \in S$, the product $xy$ is a perfect power, i.e., an integer that can be expressed as $a^b$ for some positive integer $a$ and integer $b \ge 2$.
> \end{itemize}
> Here, for a real number $r$, $[r]$ denotes the greatest integer not exceeding $r$.
