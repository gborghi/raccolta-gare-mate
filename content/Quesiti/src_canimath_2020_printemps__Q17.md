*Smallest n to place black cells in an n x n grid, k per row and column, no two sharing a side or corner*

> Let $k > 1$ be a positive integer. Determine the smallest integer $n$ for which one can colour certain cells of an $n \times n$ board black in such a way that no two black cells share a side or a corner, and each row and each column contains exactly $k$ black cells.
