*Polygonal line in 50×50 square with distance constraint; prove length > 1248*

> A polygonal line is a continuous line $A_1A_2A_3\ldots A_{n+1}$, where, for $i = 1$ to $n$, $A_iA_{i+1}$ is a straight line segment.
> 
> In a square of side 50, a polygonal line $L$ is constructed in such a way that the distance of any point inside the square from $L$ (i.e. from the nearest point on $L$) is less than 1. Prove that the length of $L$ is greater than 1248.
