*Real sequence with sum-of-squares identity; power sums as perfect squares*

> Let $(x_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ be a sequence of real numbers such that, for every natural number $n$:
> $$x_0^2 + x_1^2 + \cdots + x_n^2 = (x_0 + x_1 + \cdots + x_n)^2.$$
> 
> **1.** Show that for every non-zero natural number $n$, there exists a natural number $m$ such that:
> $$x_0 + x_1 + \cdots + x_n = \frac{m(m+1)}{2}.$$
> 
> **2.** Let $n$ and $p$ be two non-zero natural numbers, and set $S_{n,p} = 1^p + 2^p + \cdots + n^p$.
> 
> Determine the non-zero natural numbers $p$ such that, for every non-zero natural number $n$, $S_{n,p}$ is a perfect square.
