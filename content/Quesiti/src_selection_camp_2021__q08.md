*People grouped into n circles with a common-circle condition; count totals*

> Let $n$ be an integer with $n \ge 2$. There are $n$ circles (groups), and exactly $n$ people gather at each circle, so that in total $n^2$ people surround the circles. The people belonging to any one circle are mutually distinct, and any two people who both belong to some common circle belong to exactly one circle in common. Determine, over all such configurations, how many people there can be.
