*Point P off the plane with equal products; circumcircles meet plane in triangle similar to T*

> In three-dimensional space there is a plane $\pi$. On $\pi$ there is a non-isosceles triangle $ABC$, and let $I$ be its incenter. Prove that there exists a triangle $\mathcal{T}$ such that, for every point $P$ not lying on $\pi$ satisfying
> $$AP \cdot BC = BP \cdot CA = CP \cdot AB,$$
> the following holds:
> 
> Let $I_A$, $I_B$, $I_C$ be the incenters of triangles $BCP$, $CAP$, $ABP$, respectively. If the circumcircle of triangle $II_BI_C$, the circumcircle of triangle $II_CI_A$, and the circumcircle of triangle $II_AI_B$ meet $\pi$ at points $X$, $Y$, $Z$ respectively (each different from $I$), then triangle $XYZ$ is similar to $\mathcal{T}$.
> 
> Here, $UV$ denotes the length of segment $UV$.
