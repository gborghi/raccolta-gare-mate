*Functional inequality implying f(x)=0 for x<=0*

> Let $f : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a real-valued function defined on the set of real numbers that satisfies
> $$f(x+y) \le yf(x) + f(f(x))$$
> for all real numbers $x$ and $y$. Prove that $f(x) = 0$ for all $x \le 0$.
