*Quadratic with digit coefficients has rational root, so three-digit number is composite*

> Let $a$, $b$, $c \in \{0, 1, 2, \ldots, 9\}$. The quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ has a rational root. Prove that the three-digit number $\overline{abc}$ is not a prime number.
