*Euler totient phi: prove that for every m there exists n with phi(n) = m!*

> Let $n$ be a positive integer. We define $\varphi(n) = \left(1 - \dfrac{1}{p_1}\right) \cdot \left(1 - \dfrac{1}{p_2}\right) \cdot \ldots \cdot \left(1 - \dfrac{1}{p_k}\right) \cdot n$, where $p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_k$ are the distinct prime factors of $n$. Prove that for every $m \ge 1$, there exists $n$ such that $\varphi(n) = m!$.
> 
> Note: $m! = 1 \cdot 2 \cdot \ldots \cdot m$.
