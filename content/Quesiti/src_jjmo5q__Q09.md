*19×19 grid: min n so any 9 removed cells leave ≤n good rectangles*

> There is a $19 \times 19$ grid of unit squares. Call a rectangle whose all four sides lie along grid lines a ''good rectangle.'' Find the smallest positive integer $n$ satisfying the following condition:
> 
> \textbf{Condition:} No matter which $9$ unit squares are removed from the grid, the remaining region can always be partitioned into at most $n$ good rectangles.
