*Date today under Julian calendar given Gregorian reform of October 1582*

> In Portugal, October $4$, $1582$ was the last day of the Julian calendar, which was replaced by the calendar currently adopted, the Gregorian calendar. The following day was defined as October $15$, $1582$, that is, the days October $5$ to $14$, $1582$ did not exist. The only difference between the calendars is that, in the Julian calendar, all years multiple of $4$ were leap years; in the Gregorian calendar, the years that are multiples of $100$, but not of $400$, are not leap years. Thus, $1900$ would be a leap year in the Julian calendar, but not in the Gregorian calendar. What day would today, June $3$, $2014$, be if we had not changed the calendar?
> (A) May 20, 2014 (B) May 21, 2014 (C) May 22, 2014 (D) June 16, 2014 (E) June 17, 2014
