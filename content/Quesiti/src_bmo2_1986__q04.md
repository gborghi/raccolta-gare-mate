*Divisibility of product of binomial coefficients and gcd determination*

> Show that if $m, n$ are positive integers with $m \le n$, then the product $\binom{n}{1} \binom{n}{2} \cdots \binom{n}{m}$ is divisible by $n$. Find with proof the smallest positive integer $k$ such that the product $k \binom{n}{1} \binom{n}{2} \cdots \binom{n}{m}$ is divisible by $n^2$ for all integers $m, n$ with $2 \le m \le n$. [$k$ is to be independent of $m, n$.] For this $k$ and given $m$, determine, with proof, the greatest common divisor of the integers $\frac{k}{n^2} \binom{n}{1} \binom{n}{2} \cdots \binom{n}{m}$, $2 \le m \le n$.
