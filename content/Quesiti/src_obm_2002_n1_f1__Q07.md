*Area of a square hole formed by rearranging four congruent rectangles*

> A square of area $1$ was divided into $4$ congruent rectangles, as indicated in the drawing on the left. Then, the four rectangles were rearranged so as to form a square, with a square hole in the center, as the drawing on the right indicates.
> 
> The area of the hole is equal to:
> 
> (A) $\dfrac{1}{2}$ (B) $\dfrac{9}{16}$ (C) $\dfrac{16}{25}$ (D) $\dfrac{3}{4}$ (E) $1$
