# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2005 — Nível 2 — Fase 2 — Quesito 9

*Domino sequences with houses 1-3: count arrangements, explain impossibility for 1-4*

> Each piece of a domino game has two numbered houses. Consider the $6$ pieces formed using only the numbers $1$, $2$, and $3$:
> 
> (a) In how many ways is it possible to arrange all $6$ such pieces in a sequence so that the right house of each piece equals the left house of the next piece? Two examples are shown below.
> 
> (b) Explain why it is not possible to do the same with all $10$ pieces formed using only the numbers $1$, $2$, $3$, and $4$.
