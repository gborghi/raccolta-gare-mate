*Partition function satisfies convexity inequality p(n+1)-2p(n)+p(n-1)>=0*

> For $m$ a positive integer denote by $p(m)$ the number of ways of expressing $m$ as the sum of one or more positive integers. Thus $p(4) = 5$, because there are 5 different ways, namely $1+1+1+1$, $1+1+2$, $1+3$, $2+2$, $4$. Prove that for $n > 1$, $$p(n+1) - 2p(n) + p(n-1) \ge 0.$$
