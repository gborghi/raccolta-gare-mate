# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2026 — Yosen (Preliminary) — Quesito 4

*Maximum area of rectangle ABCD cut into sub-rectangles with distinct integer sides at most 9*

> There is a rectangle $ABCD$ whose side lengths are positive integers. A **cut line** is defined as any of the following straight lines:
> - a line passing through a point on side $AB$ (excluding endpoints) and parallel to $BC$,
> - a line passing through a point on side $BC$ (excluding endpoints) and parallel to $AB$.
> 
> We choose some cut lines and use them to completely cut the rectangle $ABCD$ into smaller rectangles. It turned out that each smaller rectangle has all side lengths positive integers at most $9$. Moreover, no two distinct smaller rectangles have the same pair of side lengths. Find the maximum possible area of rectangle $ABCD$.
