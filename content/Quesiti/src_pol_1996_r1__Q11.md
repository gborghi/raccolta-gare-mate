*65-contestant ski jump in random order; prove probability of exactly one leader change exceeds 1/16*

> In a skiing jump competition $65$ contestants jump in a previosly established order, each of them exactly once. We assume that their results are all different and that all possible final rankings are equaly likely. In each moment of the competition we call a person with the best score (at that moment) a leader. Let $p$ be the probability that during the whole competition there was exactly one change of a leader. Prove that $p > 1/16$.
