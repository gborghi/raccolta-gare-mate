*Möbius maps on (-1,1) via tanh; find minimum contraction factor*

> (a) Let $g : \mathbb{R} \to (-1, 1)$ be the bijection given by
> $$g(x) = \tanh(x) = \frac{e^{2x} - 1}{e^{2x} + 1}.$$
> Given $r \in [0, 1)$, prove that there exists $\lambda(r) \in [0, 1)$ with the following property: for every rational function
> $$f(x) = \frac{ax + b}{cx + d}$$
> with $a, b, c, d$ real numbers and $|d| > |c|$ such that $f((-1,1)) \subset (-r, r)$, we have $|(g^{-1} \circ f \circ g)(x)| \le \lambda(r)$ for all $x \in \mathbb{R}$.
> 
> (b) Determine the smallest $\lambda = \lambda(r)$ possible in part (a).
