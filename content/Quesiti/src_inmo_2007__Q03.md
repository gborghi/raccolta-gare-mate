*Integer roots iff floor expression is a perfect square*

> Let $m$ and $n$ be positive integers such that the equation $x^2 - mx + n = 0$ has real roots $\alpha$ and $\beta$. Prove that $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are integers if and only if $[m\alpha] + [m\beta]$ is the square of an integer. (Here $[x]$ denotes the largest integer not exceeding $x$.)
