*Nonneg 3x9 matrix with property (O) on first-three-column submatrix*

> (50 marks) Suppose that a matrix of nonneg entries
> $$P = \begin{pmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12} & x_{13} & x_{14} & x_{15} & x_{16} & x_{17} & x_{18} & x_{19} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} & x_{23} & x_{24} & x_{25} & x_{26} & x_{27} & x_{28} & x_{29} \\ x_{31} & x_{32} & x_{33} & x_{34} & x_{35} & x_{36} & x_{37} & x_{38} & x_{39} \end{pmatrix}$$
> has the following properties:
> 
> (1) Numbers in a row are different from each other;
> 
> (2) The sum of the numbers in a column from the first six columns is $1$;
> 
> (3) $x_{i4} = x_{i5} = x_{i6} = 0$ for $i = 1, 2, 3$;
> 
> (4) $x_{i1} + x_{i2} + x_{i3} + x_{i7} + x_{i8} + x_{i9} \geq 1$.
> 
> Matrix $S$ is constituted by the first three columns of $P$:
> $$S = \begin{pmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12} & x_{13} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} & x_{23} \\ x_{31} & x_{32} & x_{33} \end{pmatrix}.$$
> $S$ has the following property:
> 
> (O) For any column $\begin{pmatrix} x_{1k} \\ x_{2k} \\ x_{3k} \end{pmatrix}$ ($k = 1, 2, \ldots, 9$) in $P$, there exists $i \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ such that
> $$x_{ik} \leq u_i = \min(x_{i1}, x_{i2}, x_{i3}).$$
> 
> Prove that $\widetilde{S}$, the matrix obtained by rearranging any two rows of $S$, also has property (O).
