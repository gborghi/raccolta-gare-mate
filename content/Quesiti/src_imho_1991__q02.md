*Arithmetic residues mod n implies n is 1, 2, 4, prime power, or double*

> Let $n > 0$ be an integer and let $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_k$ be all the natural numbers less than and relatively prime to $n$. If
> $$a_2 - a_1 = a_3 - a_2 = \cdots = a_k - a_{k-1} > 0,$$
> prove that $n$ must be either $1$, $2$, $4$, a power of an odd prime, or twice a power of an odd prime.
