*Sequence x_n with x_1=-1, x_2=2; prove nS_n + F_n = 1 for all n*

> The real numbers $x_1, x_2, x_3, \ldots$ are defined by
> $$x_1 = -1, \quad x_2 = 2 \quad \text{and} \quad x_{n+1} = 2x_n - x_{n-1} \text{ for all } n \geq 1.$$
> $S_n$ is the mean and $F_n$ is the $n$-th term of the sequence $y_1, y_2, \ldots$, where $y_n = \dfrac{x_{n-1} + x_n}{2}$.
> 
> Prove that $nS_n + F_n = 1$ for all $n$.
