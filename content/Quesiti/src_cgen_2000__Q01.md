*Distribute b white and n black balls into two urns to maximize the probability of drawing a white ball*

> We have $b$ white balls and $n$ black balls -- at least one of each -- which we distribute between two urns so that neither of them is empty; we denote by $s$ the number of balls in the first urn, and by $r$ the number of those balls that are white. The event considered is the drawing of one ball at random from one of the two urns, the urn itself being chosen at random; the aim of the exercise is to determine the distributions that make the probability $p$ of drawing a white ball maximal.
> 
> 1. Express $p$ as a function of $b$, $n$, $r$ and $s$.
> 2. In this question, the value of $s$ is fixed; how should $r$ be chosen in order to increase $p$?
> 3. Solve the exercise.
> 4. What generalizations do you propose by increasing the number of colours and of urns?
