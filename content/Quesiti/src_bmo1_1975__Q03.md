*Pigeonhole: disc with 7 points, two within distance 1*

> Use the pigeonhole principle (i.e. if more than $n$ objects are put into $n$ pigeonholes then at least one pigeonhole must contain more than one object) to answer the following question.
> 
> A disc $S$ is defined as the set of all points $P$ in a plane such that $|OP| < 1$, where $|OP|$ is the distance of $P$ from $O$, a given point in the plane, called the centre of the disc.
> 
> Prove that if the disc $S$ contains 7 points such that the distance from any of the 7 points to any other is greater than or equal to 1, then one of the 7 points is $O$.
