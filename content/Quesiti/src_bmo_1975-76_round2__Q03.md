*Quadratic form has only trivial integer solution; cubic has infinitely many*

> Prove that the equation $x^2 - 3y^2 - 3z^2 + 7t^2 = 0$ has no solutions in integers other than $x = y = z = t = 0$.
> 
> Prove that $x^3 - 3y^3 - 3z^3 + 7t^3 = 0$ has infinitely many solutions in positive integers $x_0, y_0, z_0, t_0$ in no two of which the ratio $x:y:z:t$ is the same.
