*Binary representations with exactly three 1s; existence of k*

> For any positive integer $k$, let $f(k)$ be the number of elements in the set $\{k+1, k+2, \ldots, 2k\}$ whose base $2$ representation has precisely three $1$s.
> (a) Prove that, for each positive integer $m$, there exists at least one positive integer $k$ such that $f(k) = m$.
> (b) Determine all positive integers $m$ for which there exists exactly one $k$ with $f(k) = m$.
