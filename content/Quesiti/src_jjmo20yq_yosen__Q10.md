# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2022 — Yosen (Preliminary) — Quesito 10

*Ring of 103 squares; A moves piece by c or d, B by a or b; count (a,b,c,d)*

> Let $a, b, c, d$ be integers satisfying $0 < a < b < c < d < 163$. Consider $103$ squares arranged in a ring (circle), with one square labeled $S$ and the square one step counterclockwise from $S$ labeled $G$. A token starts on $S$. Players $A$ and $B$ alternate turns, starting with $A$:\n- On $A$'s turn: move the token clockwise by $c$ or $d$ squares.\n- On $B$'s turn: move the token clockwise by $a$ or $b$ squares.\nPlayer $B$'s goal is to have the token land on $G$ immediately after one of $B$'s moves. Find the number of 4-tuples $(a, b, c, d)$ for which player $B$ can always achieve the goal in finitely many moves, regardless of player $A$'s choices.
