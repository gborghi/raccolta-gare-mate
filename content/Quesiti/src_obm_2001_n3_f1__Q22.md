*Acute triangle ABC; max of sum of distances from an interior/boundary point to the three sides*

> For each point belonging to the interior and to the sides of an acute triangle $ABC$, consider the sum of its distances to the three sides of the triangle. The maximum value of this sum is equal to:
> 
> **A)** the arithmetic mean of the 3 altitudes of the triangle. $\quad$ **B)** the longest side of the triangle. $\quad$ **C)** the greatest altitude of the triangle. $\quad$ **D)** three times the radius of the circle inscribed in the triangle. $\quad$ **E)** the diameter of the circle circumscribed about the triangle.
