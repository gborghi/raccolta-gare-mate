*Prove for each N≥1 there is n≥1 with N not dividing f(n)*

> For each integer $n \geq 1$, let $f(n)$ be the number of lists of different positive integers starting with $n$ and ending with $n$, in which each term except the last divides its successor. Prove that for each integer $N \geq 1$ there is an integer $n \geq 1$ such that $N$ does not divide $f(n)$.
> 
> $(So\ f(1) = 1,\ f(2) = 1\ and\ f(6) = 3.)$
