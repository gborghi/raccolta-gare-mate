*Smallest possible center value of a 3x3 multiplicative magic square*

> A multiplicative magic square is an $n \times n$ square with distinct positive integers whose products of the numbers in the same row, column or diagonal are equal. For example, we have the following multiplicative magic square:
> $\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline 128 & 1 & 32 \\ \hline 4 & 16 & 64 \\ \hline 8 & 256 & 2 \\ \hline \end{array}$
> What is the smallest possible value of the number at the center of a $3 \times 3$ multiplicative magic square?
> (A) $1$ (B) $2$ (C) $4$ (D) $6$ (E) $8$
