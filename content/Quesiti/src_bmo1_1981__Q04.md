*Bound on tetrahedra cut in quadrilateral cross-sections by a plane*

> $n$ points are given such that no plane passes through four of them. $S$ is the set of tetrahedra whose vertices are $4$ of the $n$ points. A plane does not pass through any of the $n$ points.
> 
> Prove that it cannot cut more than $\dfrac{n^2(n-2)}{4}$ of the tetrahedra of $S$ in quadrilateral cross-sections.
