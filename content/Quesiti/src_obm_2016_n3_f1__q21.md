*Minimum number of merge operations to split piles 1,2,...,9,11 into equal piles*

> There are piles of stones containing $1,2,3,\dots,9$ and $11$ stones (no pile of $10$), with total $1+2+3+\dots+9+11=56$. For all piles to have the same amount $k$, $k$ must be a divisor of $56$. Analyzing the divisors, the possibilities are $14,28$ or $56$ stones per pile. Each merge of piles is one operation; to make piles of $28$ requires more operations, and to obtain $4$ piles of $14$ requires $6$ operations, realized explicitly as $(11+3),(9+5),(8+6),(7+4+2+1)$. What is the minimum number of operations? [Answer key value corresponds to option D.]
