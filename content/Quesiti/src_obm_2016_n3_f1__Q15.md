*Ordering probabilities p1, p2, p3 of obtaining large sums with 1, 2 and 3 dice*

> Let $p_1=\frac{2}{6}=\frac13$ be the probability of getting a result equal to $5$ or $6$ with one die. For two dice ($6^2=36$ outcomes), the favorable outcomes are sums equal to $10,11,12$: $2+1+2+1=6$ favorable, so $p_2=\frac{6}{36}=\frac16$. For three dice ($6^3=216$ outcomes), counting sums $15,16,17,18$ gives $20$ favorable outcomes, so $p_3=\frac{20}{216}=\frac{5}{54}$. Hence $p_3<p_2<p_1$. Order $p_1$, $p_2$, $p_3$. [Answer key value: $p_3<p_2<p_1$.]
