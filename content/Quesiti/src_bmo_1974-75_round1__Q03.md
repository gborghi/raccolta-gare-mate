# British Mathematical Olympiad 1974-75 — Round 1 — Quesito 3

*Pigeonhole: disc of radius 3 with 7 points; some two within distance 1*

> Use the pigeonhole principle (i.e. if more than $n$ objects are put into $n$ pigeonholes then at least one pigeonhole must contain more than one object) to prove the following:
> 
> A disc $S$ is defined as the set of all points $P$ in a plane such that $|OP| \le 3$, where $|OP|$ is the distance of $P$ from $O$, a given point in the plane and the centre of the disc.
> 
> Prove that if the disc $S$ contains $7$ points such that the distance from any of the $7$ points to any other is greater than or equal to $1$, then one of the $7$ points is $O$.
