# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2014 — Nível 3 — Fase 1 — Quesito 1

*Smallest power-product integer with exactly 2014 positive divisors*

> To find the quantity of positive divisors of a positive integer $n$, it suffices to take its factorization into primes and compute the product of the exponents of the primes increased by $1$. For example, $2800 = 2^4 \cdot 5^2 \cdot 7$ has $(4+1)(2+1)(1+1) = 30$ positive divisors. What is the smallest positive integer with $2014$ positive divisors?
> (A) $2^2 3^{19} 5^{53}$ (B) $2^{53} 3^{19} 5^2$ (C) $2^{52} 3^{18} 5$ (D) $2^{38} 3^{53}$ (E) $2^{37} 3^{52}$
