*Count integers n in [10^98, 10^100) such that the erasing operation leaves exactly 3 numbers*

> Let $n$ be an integer with $10^{98} \le n < 10^{100}$. Write the integers from $1$ to $n$ on a blackboard, then repeatedly perform the following operation until at most $3$ numbers remain on the blackboard:
> 
> *Simultaneously erase all numbers on the blackboard that are at a perfect-square position from the smallest (i.e., erase the 1st smallest, the 4th smallest, the 9th smallest, etc.).*
> 
> Find the number of values of $n$ for which exactly $3$ numbers remain on the blackboard when the operation terminates.
