# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2016 — Nível 2 — Fase 1 — Quesito 15

*Extra meetings of the hands of Esmeralda's clock vs a normal clock*

> The point where the hands of a clock meet rotates faster than the hour hand. For Esmeralda's clock, where the hour hand makes a complete turn faster, the encounters occur at instants corresponding to hour-hand displacements of sizes $\tfrac{l}{13},\tfrac{2l}{13},\dots,\tfrac{12l}{13}$. For a normal clock the next encounter occurs when $12k-k=l$, i.e. $k=\tfrac{l}{11}$, giving encounters at $\tfrac{l}{11},\tfrac{2l}{11},\dots,\tfrac{10l}{11}$. Thus in $12$ hours there are $2$ more encounters in Esmeralda's clock than in a normal one, and over a full day $y=x+4$. Determine the relation between the numbers of encounters.
