*For an interior point P of an acute triangle T with successive reflections in its sides, prove the six triple-reflection images are concyclic iff P is the orthocentre.*

> Three lines $t_1, t_2, t_3$ form an acute angled triangle $\mathcal{T}$ in the plane. Point $P$ lies in the interior of $\mathcal{T}$. Let $r_i$ denote the transformation of the plane such that the image $r_i(X)$ of any point $X$ in the plane is the reflection of $X$ in $t_i$, for each $i \in \{1, 2, 3\}$. Denote by $P_{ijk}$ the point $r_k(r_j(r_i(P)))$ for every permutation $(i, j, k)$ of $(1, 2, 3)$. Prove that $P_{123}, P_{132}, P_{213}, P_{231}, P_{312}, P_{321}$ are concyclic if and only if $P$ coincides with the orthocentre of $\mathcal{T}$.
