*For every natural n and reals a_1,...,a_n, prove there exists a natural k such that the absolute difference of the first-k sum and the rest is at most the maximum absolute value.*

> Prove that for every natural number $n$ and a sequence of real numbers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ there exists a natural number $k$ that satisfies
> $$\left| \sum_{i=1}^{k} a_i - \sum_{i=k+1}^{n} a_i \right| \le \max_{1 \le i \le n} |a_i|.$$
