*Ratio of inradii equals ratio of exradii in triangle*

> Let $M$ be a point on the side $AB$ of $\triangle ABC$. Let $r_1$, $r_2$, $r$ be the radii of the inscribed circles of triangles $AMC$, $BMC$ and $ABC$. Let $q_1$, $q_2$, $q$ be the radii of the escribed circles of the same triangles that lie in the angle $ACB$. Prove that
> $$\frac{r_1}{q_1} \cdot \frac{r_2}{q_2} = \frac{r}{q}.$$
