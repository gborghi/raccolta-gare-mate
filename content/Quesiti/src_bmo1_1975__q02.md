*Primes partitioned into two sets, products divisibility*

> The first $n$ prime numbers $2, 3, 5, \ldots, p_n$ are partitioned into two disjoint sets $A$ and $B$. The primes in $A$ are $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_m$ and the primes in $B$ are $b_1, b_2, \ldots, b_k$ where $m + k = n$.
> 
> The two products $\prod_{1}^{m} a_i^{\,\alpha_i}$ and $\prod_{1}^{k} b_j^{\,\beta_j}$ are formed where the $\alpha_i$ and $\beta_j$ are any positive integers.
> 
> If $d$ divides the difference between these two products prove that either $d = 1$ or $d > p_n$.
