- [[src_smc_2016__Q01|Quesito 1]] — How many times does the digit 9 appear in 987654321 × 9?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q02|Quesito 2]] — On Monday prices at Isla's shop are 20% more than normal; on Friday 10% less than normal. James bought a book on Monday for £5.50. What is the price on Friday?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q03|Quesito 3]] — A circle of radius 1 rolls without slipping around the inside of a square with sides of length 5. What distance does the centre travel?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q04|Quesito 4]] — Alex draws a scalene triangle with one angle 80°. Which of the listed angles could be another angle of this triangle?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q05|Quesito 5]] — Digits 2,3,4,5,6 placed one per cell in a 3×3 grid to form two three-digit square numbers. Which digit is in the centre?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q06|Quesito 6]] — Square ABCD with BC=3 and right-angled triangle ABE with BE=4. Find the area of the shaded region ABCF.
- [[src_smc_2016__Q07|Quesito 7]] — Which of 2016^(−1), 2016^(−1/2), 2016^0, 2016^(1/2), 2016^1 has the smallest value?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q08|Quesito 8]] — Points divide each side of a square into n equal parts; joined to form small squares and triangles. How many small squares when n=7?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q09|Quesito 9]] — A square has vertices at (0,0),(1,0),(1,1),(0,1). How many of the five listed graphs pass through exactly two vertices of the square?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q10|Quesito 10]] — Digits 1–9 placed one per cell in a 3×3 grid; row products are 12, 112, 216. Find the product of the digits in the shaded cells (second column, first and third rows).
- [[src_smc_2016__Q11|Quesito 11]] — In a row of six cells, each interior cell equals the mean of its two neighbours. The first cell is 10 and the last is 25. Which number goes in the square marked X (the third cell)?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q12|Quesito 12]] — What is the smallest perfect square that has 2016 as a factor?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q13|Quesito 13]] — Five square tiles (side length 1) placed side by side; a quarter circle on each tile forms a continuous curve. What is the total length of the curve?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q14|Quesito 14]] — Which positive integer n is a counterexample to: 'if n is not prime then n − 2 is not prime'?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q15|Quesito 15]] — Three rectangles meet three straight lines at points P, Q, R with exterior angles p°, q°, r°. Find p + q + r.
- [[src_smc_2016__Q16|Quesito 16]] — For which value of k does √2016 + √56 equal 14^k?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q17|Quesito 17]] — Aaron chooses a three-digit code from digits 1–9, all different, in increasing order. How many codes can he choose?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q18|Quesito 18]] — The circumference of a circle of radius r is divided into four equal arcs; alternate pairs of arcs are joined to form a shaded region. What is the area of the shaded region?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q19|Quesito 19]] — S is a set of five different positive integers, largest m. No quadrilateral with non-zero area has all side-lengths distinct elements of S. Find smallest possible m.
- [[src_smc_2016__Q20|Quesito 20]] — A tile from Marrakech has line of symmetry XY of length 8 cm; VZ is parallel to the sides of the square tile. Find the area of the central grey octagon.
- [[src_smc_2016__Q21|Quesito 21]] — Ten equal discs lie between two concentric circles; each disc touches its two neighbours and both circles; inner circle radius 1. Find the radius of the outer circle.
- [[src_smc_2016__Q22|Quesito 22]] — Ben, Cam and Dan each make one statement about who is telling the truth. Exactly one of the three is lying. Which of the three is lying?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q23|Quesito 23]] — Cuboid with sides 22, 2, 10 is contained within a sphere of smallest possible radius. What is the side-length of the smallest cube that contains this sphere?
- [[src_smc_2016__Q24|Quesito 24]] — In square PQRS, arc QS is a quarter circle with centre Q. Line TU is tangent to the arc, with T on QR and U on SR extended. Find TR : UR.
- [[src_smc_2016__Q25|Quesito 25]] — Let n be the smallest integer such that 7n has 2016 digits. What is the units digit of n?
