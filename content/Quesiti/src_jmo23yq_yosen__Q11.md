*Orient unit lattice segments in 3D cube so each lattice square is congruent; count*

> Let $n$ be a positive integer. In $xyz$-space, consider the region $0 \le x, y, z \le n$, and call a segment of length $1$ joining two lattice points a lattice edge. Suppose every lattice edge is given an orientation (for a lattice edge $AB$, either $A \to B$ or $B \to A$). Each of the $4$ sides of a unit square is a lattice edge; a unit square whose $4$ edges' orientations go around it (form a directed cycle) is called a lattice square. Two lattice squares are regarded as congruent when, by rotation and translation, the orientations of all their edges can be made to coincide. In how many ways can the lattice edges be oriented so that all lattice squares are mutually congruent?
