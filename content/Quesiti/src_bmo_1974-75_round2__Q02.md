*Positive integers not in a given infinite set are sums of consecutive positive integers*

> Prove that every positive integer which is not a member of the infinite set
> $$3,\ 2^2 \cdot 3,\ 2^4 \cdot 3,\ \ldots,\ 2^{2k} \cdot 3,\ \ldots$$
> is equal to the sum of two or more consecutive positive integers.
