*Tetrahedra in sphere with mutually orthogonal lateral edges; fixed plane and formula*

> Let $S$ be a fixed point on a sphere $\Sigma$ with centre $\Omega$. We consider tetrahedra $SABC$ inscribed in the sphere $\Sigma$ whose lateral edges from $S$ are pairwise orthogonal.
> 
> **1.** Show that the planes $(ABC)$ all pass through a fixed point.
> 
> **2.** For such a tetrahedron $SABC$: the point $S$ and the centre $\Omega$ of the sphere $\Sigma$ project orthogonally onto the plane $(ABC)$ at points $H$ and $O$ respectively. Let $R$ denote the radius of the circumscribed circle of triangle $ABC$.
> 
> Prove that:
> $$R^2 = OH^2 + 2SI^2,$$
> where $I$ denotes a point appearing in the configuration. *(Warning: the last term $2SI^2$ may involve a point $I$ not explicitly named in the readable portion of the image; could be $2SH^2$ or $2\,\Omega I^2$. See warnings.)*
