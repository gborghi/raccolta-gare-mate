*Paper-folding trisection of an angle; prove lines AA' and AB' divide angle alpha into three equal parts.*

> On a sheet of paper a line $r$ passes through the corner $A$ of the sheet and forms an angle $\alpha$ with the horizontal border, as in figure 1. To divide this angle $\alpha$ into three equal parts, we carry out the following constructions:
> a) initially, we mark two points $B$ and $C$ on the vertical border so that $AB = BC$; through the point $B$ we draw the line $s$ parallel to the border (figure 2);
> b) next, we fold the paper, adjusting it so that the point $C$ coincides with a point $C'$ on the line $r$ and the point $A$ coincides with a point $A'$ on the line $s$ (figure 3); we call $B'$ the point with which $B$ coincides.
> 
> Show that the lines $AA'$ and $AB'$ divide the angle $\alpha$ into three equal parts.
