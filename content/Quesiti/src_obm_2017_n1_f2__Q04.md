*Strictly positive sequence with controlled differences converging to 0 satisfies a_n ≥ d/n*

> Let $(a_n)_{n \ge 1}$ be a sequence of strictly positive terms with $\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 0$, such that for some constant $c > 0$ and for all $n \ge 1$,
> $$|a_{n+1} - a_n| \le c \cdot a_n^2.$$
> Prove that there exists $d > 0$ such that $a_n \ge \dfrac{d}{n}$ for all $n \ge 1$.
