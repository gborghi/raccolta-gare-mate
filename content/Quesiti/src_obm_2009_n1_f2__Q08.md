# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2009 — Nível 1 — Fase 2 — Quesito 8

*Three-digit A times two-digit B; reversing B's digits gives a result 2034 larger; find A when B's digits are consecutive / non-consecutive*

> Esmeralda was going to multiply a number $A$ of three digits by another number $B$ of two digits, but at the moment of multiplying she inverted the order of the digits of $B$ and obtained a result $2034$ units larger.
> 
> a) What was the number $A$, if the digits of $B$ were consecutive?
> 
> b) What would the number $A$ be, if the digits of $B$ were not consecutive?
