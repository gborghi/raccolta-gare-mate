*Sum of square roots minus arithmetic mean; monotonicity and limit*

> For every non-zero natural number $n$, we associate to the integer $n$ the application $f_n$ of the real variable $x$, defined for $x \ge n$ by:
> $$f_n(x) = \sqrt{x-n} + \sqrt{x-n+1} + \cdots + \sqrt{x-1} + \sqrt{x} + \sqrt{x+1} + \cdots + \sqrt{x+n} - (2n+1)\sqrt{x}.$$
> 
> **1.** In this question, the integer $n$ is fixed.
> 
> Show that $f_n$ is increasing and that $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to +\infty} f_n(x) = 0$.
> 
> **2.** Determine the limit of the sequence with general term $f_n(n)$.
