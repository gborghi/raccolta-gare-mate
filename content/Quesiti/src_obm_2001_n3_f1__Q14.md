*Five animals (dogs tell truth, wolves lie); given statements, count the wolves*

> Five animals $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$ and $E$ are either dogs or wolves. Dogs always tell the truth and wolves always lie. $A$ says that $B$ is a dog. $B$ says that $C$ is a wolf. $C$ says that $D$ is a wolf. $D$ says that $B$ and $E$ are animals of different species. $E$ says that $A$ is a dog. How many wolves are there among the five animals?
> 
> **A)** 1 $\quad$ **B)** 2 $\quad$ **C)** 3 $\quad$ **D)** 4 $\quad$ **E)** 5
