*Rectangular parallelepiped, tetrahedra with right-triangle faces, and isosceles tetrahedra*

> 1. Let $ABCDEFGH$ be a rectangular parallelepiped (cuboid). Show that one can choose four of its vertices in such a way that the four faces of the resulting tetrahedron are right triangles.
> 
> 2. Conversely, show that every tetrahedron whose four faces are right triangles can be obtained by choosing four vertices of a rectangular parallelepiped.
> 
> 3. Among the tetrahedra that also have at least two isosceles faces, find those whose face lengths (as a function of the side length $a$ of the smallest edge) minimise the largest edge.
