*With omega = sum of exponents, decide if ratio omega(n+k)/omega(n) can be made both < alpha and > beta*

> For any integer $n$ with $n>1$, let $n=p_1^{\alpha_1}p_2^{\alpha_2}\cdots p_s^{\alpha_s}$ be its standard factorization; write $\omega(n)=\alpha_1+\alpha_2+\cdots+\alpha_s$. Prove or disprove the following statement: Given any positive integer $k$ and any positive real numbers $\alpha$ and $\beta$, there exists a positive integer $n$ with $n>1$ such that $\dfrac{\omega(n+k)}{\omega(n)}<\alpha$ and $\dfrac{\omega(n+k)}{\omega(n)}>\beta$.
