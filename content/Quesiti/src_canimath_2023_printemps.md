# Coupe Animath Printemps 2023

Sorgente: apri PDF

- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q01|Quesito 1]] — Compute the value of 4^8/8^4.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q02|Quesito 2]] — In triangle ABC with angle CAB = 20 degrees, D the midpoint of [AB], and angle CDB = 40 degrees; find angle ABC.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q03|Quesito 3]] — In a 5x5 grid of 25 white cells, find the smallest number of cells to color red so that every 2x3 and 3x2 rectangle contains at least one red cell.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q04|Quesito 4]] — In a pentagon ABCDE with all sides equal, with angles BCD and CDE right angles and A not inside quadrilateral BCDE, P the intersection of lines (AC) and (BD); prove AP = PD.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q05|Quesito 5]] — Theo's grades are integers from 1 to 5 with average at most 3; he replaces every grade equal to 1 with a 3; show the new average remains at most 4.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q06|Quesito 6]] — A pair (x,y) with 1<=y<x is 'joli' if x and y have exactly x-y common positive divisors; with s(n) the number of joli pairs with 1<=y<x<=n, decide whether s(n)=2022 or s(n)=2023 is achievable.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q07|Quesito 7]] — In a 10x10 grid with rows numbered 1 (top) to 10 (bottom) and columns 1 (left) to 10 (right), Martin colors cells so that each colored cell c has at most one other colored cell with both column number and row number greater than or equal to those of c; find the maximum number of colored cells.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q08|Quesito 8]] — Count the integers n such that n/3 and 3n are both integers between 1 and 1000 inclusive.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q09|Quesito 9]] — In triangle ABC with angle CAB = 20 degrees, D the midpoint of [AB], and angle CDB = 40 degrees; find angle ABC.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q10|Quesito 10]] — Using the numbers 1 to 22 exactly once each as numerators and denominators, Antoine writes 11 fractions; find the maximum possible number of fractions having integer values.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q11|Quesito 11]] — Given 100 distinct integers 1 <= a_1 < a_2 < ... < a_100 <= 400 with d_i = a_{i+1} - a_i, show that at least 15 of d_1,...,d_99 are equal to one another.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q12|Quesito 12]] — In a rhombus ABCD with E the intersection of the diagonals, F the midpoint of [BE], G the midpoint of [AD], I the intersection of (FG) and (AC), and K the reflection of A across I; find EK/EA.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q13|Quesito 13]] — In a 10x10 grid with rows numbered 1 (top) to 10 (bottom) and columns 1 (left) to 10 (right), Martin colors cells so that each colored cell c has at most one other colored cell with both column number and row number greater than or equal to those of c; find the maximum number of colored cells.
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q14|Quesito 14]] — For which positive integers n can Aline write 2n real numbers, not all equal, such that for every choice of n of them the sum of the chosen n equals the product of the other n?
- [[src_canimath_2023_printemps__Q15|Quesito 15]] — For a fixed integer n>=2, a positive integer S is 'special' if for every k and every ordered decomposition of n into k positive parts n_1<=...<=n_k there exist integers 0<=a_1<a_2<...<a_k with sum a_i n_i = S; show n^2-2n is not special and find all special numbers.
