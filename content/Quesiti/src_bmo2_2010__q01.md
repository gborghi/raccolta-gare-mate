*Each of 2010^2010 camp children has at most three friends; decide whether the children can always be listed so that any pair of friends has at most 2010 children between them.*

> There are $2010^{2010}$ children at a mathematics camp. Each has at most three friends at the camp, and if $A$ is friends with $B$, then $B$ is friends with $A$. The camp leader would like to line the children up so that there are at most $2010$ children between any pair of friends. Is it always possible to do this?
