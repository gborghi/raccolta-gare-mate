*Rafael chooses cards from an arithmetic set summing to 100*

> Rafael has $10$ cards. On each one of them is written one of the numbers $3, 8, 13, 18, 23, 28, 33, 38, 43, 48$, and all of these numbers appear (each exactly once). Rafael wants to choose some cards in such a way that the sum of the numbers written on the chosen cards is exactly $100$. How many cards must he choose?
> 
> (A) $2$ (B) $3$ (C) $4$ (D) $5$ (E) It is not possible to obtain $100$ with these cards.
