*Largest x with x+y=10 satisfying a parity-counting equation*

> In a set with $n$ elements the number of two-element subsets is $\frac{n(n-1)}{2}$. Let $x$ and $y$ be the quantities of even and odd numbers in Janaina's list, with $x+y=10$. The stated condition gives $\frac{x(x-1)}{2}+\frac{y(y-1)}{2}=4xy$. Substituting $y=10-x$ yields $x^2-9x+10=0$, with solutions $x=1$ or $x=9$. Since $(x,y)=(9,1)$ satisfies the condition, the maximum value of $x$ is $9$. What is the maximum value of $x$? (Note: original multiple-choice options are not reproduced in the source gabarito.)
