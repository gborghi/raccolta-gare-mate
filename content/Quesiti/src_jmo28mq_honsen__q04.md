*no labeling of infinite grid with 1,2,3 meets all three conditions*

> Let $n$ be a positive odd integer. Consider an infinite grid of cells extending without bound in all directions. Show that it is impossible to write one of the numbers $1, 2, 3$ in each cell so that all of the following conditions are satisfied:
> \begin{enumerate}
> \item[(a)] No two cells sharing an edge contain the same number.
> \item[(b)] In no block of three consecutive cells in a row or column (that is, a $3 \times 1$ or $1 \times 3$ block of cells) do the numbers read $1, 2, 3$ in order, whether read from top, bottom, left, or right.
> \item[(c)] The sum of the numbers written in an $n \times n$ block of cells is the same regardless of the position of the block.
> \end{enumerate}
