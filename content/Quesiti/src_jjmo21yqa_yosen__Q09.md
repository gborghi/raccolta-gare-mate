*Two-coloring triangles in 7x7 grid with constraints*

> Consider a $7 \times 7$ grid of cells. For two adjacent cells, the edge they share is called a "good edge". When each cell is divided into four parts by its two diagonals, the whole figure is divided into $196$ congruent right isosceles triangles; each such triangle is called a "small triangle". In how many ways can each of these $196$ small triangles be colored either red or blue so that both of the following conditions hold?
> \begin{itemize}
> \item For every cell, among the four small triangles contained in it, the number colored red is exactly $1$ or $3$.
> \item For every good edge, the two small triangles having it as a side are colored the same color.
> \end{itemize}
