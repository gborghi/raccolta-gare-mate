*Octagon with blue integers on sides; can vertex products be 1,2,...,8 in some order?*

> On each side of an octagon, Anna writes in blue a strictly positive integer (the 8 integers she writes are not necessarily distinct). Then, on each vertex of the octagon, she writes in red the product of the two blue integers written at the ends of the two sides meeting at that vertex. Can Anna choose the blue integers so that the red integers written on the vertices are exactly $1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8$ (not necessarily in that order)?
