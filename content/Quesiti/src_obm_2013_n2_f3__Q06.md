# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2013 — Nível 2 — Fase 3 — Quesito 6

*n semirays from each of A and B; minimum distinct circles through intersection points*

> Consider a positive integer $n$ and two points $A$ and $B$ in a plane. Starting from point $A$, $n$ semirays are drawn, and starting from point $B$, $n$ semirays are drawn, in such a way that all of them lie in the half-plane defined by line $AB$, and that all angles formed by the $2n$ semirays with segment $AB$ are acute. Define circles passing through $A$, $B$ and through each intersection point between a semiray from $A$ and a semiray from $B$. What is the minimum number of **distinct** circles that can be defined by this construction?
