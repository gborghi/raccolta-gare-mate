*Polynomial evaluated at 5th roots of unity; pentagon vertex distance product*

> **1.** Consider the map $P : \mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{C}$ defined by:
> $$P(z) = z^5 + a_4 z^4 + a_3 z^3 + a_2 z^2 + a_1 z,$$
> where $a_1, a_2, a_3, a_4$ are four given complex numbers. Set $w_l = e^{2il\pi/5}$ where $l$ is an integer with $0 \le l \le 4$.
> 
> Show that $P(w_0) + P(w_1) + P(w_2) + P(w_3) + P(w_4) = 5$.
> 
> **2.** Let $A_1, A_2, A_3, A_4, A_5$ be five points of the plane. A regular pentagon is inscribed in the circle with centre $A_1$ and given radius $R$.
> 
> Prove that there exists a vertex $S$ of the pentagon such that:
> $$SA_1 \cdot SA_2 \cdot SA_3 \cdot SA_4 \cdot SA_5 \ge R^5.$$
