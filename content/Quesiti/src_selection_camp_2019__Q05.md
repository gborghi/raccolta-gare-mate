*Good numbers 2^n+2^{floor(n/2)}: sums and non-sums of distinct good numbers*

> Call an integer a \textit{good number} if it can be written, using a nonnegative integer $n$, in the form $2^n + 2^{\lfloor n/2 \rfloor}$. (Here $\lfloor r \rfloor$ denotes the greatest integer not exceeding the real number $r$.)
> 
> (1) Prove that there exist infinitely many good numbers that can be expressed as a sum of two or more distinct good numbers.
> 
> (2) Prove that there exist infinitely many good numbers that cannot be expressed as a sum of two or more distinct good numbers.
