*Quadratics with P(1)=P(2)=Q(3)=0 and P^2+Q^2=R^2; find R*

> Let $P,Q,R$ be quadratic polynomials with integer coefficients satisfying the following conditions. Find all possible $R(x)$.
> \begin{itemize}
> \item $P(1) = P(2) = Q(3) = 0$.
> \item For every real number $x$, $P(x)^2 + Q(x)^2 = R(x)^2$ holds.
> \item There is no integer $\ge 2$ that divides all of the coefficients of $P$, $Q$, $R$.
> \item The leading (quadratic) coefficients of $P$ and $Q$ are nonzero, and the leading (quadratic) coefficient of $R$ is positive.
> \end{itemize}
