*On an 8x8 board two players alternately place 1x1 pawns on squares; the player who cannot move loses; determine which player has a winning strategy independent of the opponent's play*

> Two players play on a chessboard ($8 \times 8$) made up of squares the size of the pieces. The two players play alternately, and on each play a player places a $1 \times 1$ pawn on an empty square of the board. The player who cannot place a pawn on the board loses. Can a player win independently of how the adversary plays — that is, does one of the players have a winning strategy?
