*Cyclic pentagon, diagonal intersection points; prove concyclicity*

> A pentagon $A_1A_2A_3A_4A_5$ is inscribed in a circle. For each integer $i$ with $1 \le i \le 5$, let $B_i$ be the intersection point of line $A_{i+1}A_{i-1}$ and line $A_{i-1}A_{i-2}$, where indices are taken modulo $5$ so that $A_n = A_{n+5}$. From the pentagon $B_1B_2B_3B_4B_5$ one obtains five further intersection points $I_1, I_2, I_3, I_4, I_5$ of its sides/diagonals. Prove that $I_1, I_2, I_3, I_4, I_5$ lie on a common circle. (Indices satisfy $A_n = A_{n+5}$.)
