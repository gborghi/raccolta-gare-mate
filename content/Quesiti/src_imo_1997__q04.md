*none for n=1997, exist for infinitely many n*

> An n × n matrix whose entries come from the set S = {1, 2, . . . , 2n −1} is
> called a silver matrix if, for each i = 1, 2, . . . , n, the ith row and the ith column
> together contain all elements of S. Show that
> (a)
> there is no silver matrix for n = 1997;
> (b)
> silver matrices exist for infinitely many values of n.
