*Positive integer conditions on a_i imply polynomial M(x+1)^k - product has no positive real roots*

> Let $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n, k, M$ be positive integers satisfying
> $$\frac{1}{a_1} + \frac{1}{a_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{a_n} = k, \quad a_1 a_2 \cdots a_n = M.$$
> Assume $M > 1$. Prove that the equation in $x$
> $$M(x+1)^k - (x + a_1)(x + a_2) \cdots (x + a_n) = 0$$
> has no positive real solutions.
