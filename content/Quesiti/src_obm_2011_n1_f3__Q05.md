*On a 3x3 board fill 1 to 9 so that of the 8 sums (3 rows, 3 columns, 2 diagonals) exactly 3 are multiples of 3; and decide whether none can be a multiple of 3.*

> On a $3 \times 3$ board we write the numbers from $1$ to $9$, one in each cell. In this way we form $8$ sums, of the three numbers in each row, in each column and in each of the two diagonals, and we count how many of these sums are multiples of $3$. For example, in the board on the side a tabuleiro is shown in which there are exactly $3$ sums that are multiples of $3$ (the three sums that are multiples of $3$ being indicated).
> 
> a) Complete a board so that exactly $3$ of the $8$ sums are multiples of $3$.
> 
> b) Is it possible that none of the $8$ sums is a multiple of $3$? Remember that you must justify your answer.
