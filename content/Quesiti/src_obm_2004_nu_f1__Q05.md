# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2004 — Nível Universitário — Fase 1 — Quesito 5

*Matrix with a_{ij}=m(i-1)+j; find lambda for limit of phi(n)/n^lambda*

> Consider an $n \times n$ matrix $A$ defined by $a_{ij} = m(i-1)+j$ for all $1 \le i,j \le n$. Let $\varphi(n)$ be the sum of the determinants of all $k \times k$ submatrices of $A$ (over all choices of $k$ rows and $k$ columns of $A$).
> 
> (a) Determine a real number $\lambda$ such that $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\varphi(n)}{n^{\lambda}}$ exists and is nonzero.
> 
> (b) Determine the value of the limit found in part (a).
