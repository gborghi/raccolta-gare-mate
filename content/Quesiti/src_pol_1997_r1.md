- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q01|Quesito 1]] — Solve a system of two equations involving signed absolute values of x and y.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q02|Quesito 2]] — In a parallelogram with an interior point giving an angle equality, prove another angle equality.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q03|Quesito 3]] — Prove an inequality for real numbers a,b at least 1 and c at least 0, with integer n at least 1.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q04|Quesito 4]] — Prove that an integer n at least 2 is composite iff certain positive integers a,b,x,y exist.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q05|Quesito 5]] — Angle bisectors of a triangle meet the opposite sides and the circumcircle; prove a sum of ratios is at least 1.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q06|Quesito 6]] — Determine P(0) for a degree-n polynomial taking value 1/k at powers of 2.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q07|Quesito 7]] — Find the supremum of volumes of tetrahedra contained in a ball, with one edge a diameter.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q08|Quesito 8]] — Counting subsets by sum-remainder mod 7 and by product-remainder mod 7; find the ratio a_n/b_n.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q09|Quesito 9]] — Find all functions on [1,infinity) into [1,infinity) satisfying a recurrence and a boundedness condition.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q10|Quesito 10]] — In an acute triangle with two interior points satisfying angle equalities, prove a right-angle condition is equivalent to Q being an orthocenter.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q11|Quesito 11]] — For a non-constant integer polynomial with at least three distinct integer roots, prove P(x)+5^m has at most one integer root.
- [[src_pol_1997_r1__Q12|Quesito 12]] — For n people dining three at a time with each pair meeting exactly once, prove n is congruent to 1 or 3 mod 6.
