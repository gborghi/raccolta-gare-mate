*Arnaldo and Beatriz communicate via smoke signals; bound N ≤ 4096 on dictionary size*

> Arnaldo and Beatriz communicate during a camping trip using smoke signals, sometimes using a small fire and sometimes a large one. During the time available before morning coffee, Arnaldo manages to send a sequence of 24 smoke puffs. Beatriz and Arnaldo then create a dictionary in order to communicate. The dictionary lists $N$ sequences of 24 puffs of smoke (where $G$ indicates a large smoke puff and $P$ indicates a small smoke puff, for example the sequence $GPGPGPGPGPGPGPGPGPGPGPGPGP$), and the dictionary indicates the meaning of each sequence. To avoid misinterpretations, Arnaldo and Beatriz agree to include in the dictionary only sequences that differ in at least 8 of the 24 positions. More precisely, two sequences are considered close if they differ in at most $8$ of the $24$ positions. Arnaldo and Beatriz agree to not include close sequences in the dictionary.
> 
> Prove that $N \le 4096$.
