# Japan Junior Mathematical Olympiad 2025 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 5

*At least m² ordered triples (i,s,t) with s≤t across cyclic sheets*

> Let $m$ be a positive integer and $n$ be an integer with $n \ge 2$. There are $n$ sheets of paper labeled $1, 2, \ldots, n$; on each sheet, $m$ distinct real numbers are written. (The same real number may appear on different sheets.) Prove that there are at least $m^2$ triples $(i, s, t)$, where $i$ is an integer and $s$, $t$ are real numbers, satisfying all of the following conditions:
> 
> \begin{itemize}
> \item $1 \le i \le n$.
> \item $s$ is written on sheet $i$, and $t$ is written on sheet $i + 1$.
> \item $s \le t$.
> \end{itemize}
> 
> Here, sheet $n + 1$ refers to sheet $1$.
