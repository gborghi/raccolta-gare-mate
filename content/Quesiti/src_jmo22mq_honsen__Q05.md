*Grid game: find all k for which A wins in finitely many moves*

> Let $k$ be a positive integer. Players $A$ and $B$ play a game. Initially, one piece is placed at the origin $(0,0)$ of the $xy$-plane. With $A$ going first, the two players alternate taking turns according to the following rules:
> 
> **Player $A$'s move:** Choose one lattice point that does not yet have a stamp, and place a stamp on it. (Lattice points with stamps may not be chosen again.)
> 
> **Player $B$'s move:** Move the piece from its current position $(x,y)$ to $(x+1,y)$ or $(x,y+1)$, and repeat this between $1$ and $k$ times total in the turn. However, the piece may not be moved to a lattice point that already has a stamp.
> 
> If $B$ is unable to move the piece (all neighboring lattice points reachable by $B$ are stamped or $B$ has no valid move), then $A$ wins. Find all positive integers $k$ for which $A$ can guarantee a win in a finite number of turns.
