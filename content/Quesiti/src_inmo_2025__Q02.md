*Alice erases numbers on a board to leave one number remaining*

> Let $n \ge 2$ be a positive integer. The integers $1, 2, \ldots, n$ are written on the board. In a move, Alice can pick two integers written on the board $a \ne b$ such that $a + b$ is an even number, erase both $a$ and $b$ from the board and write $\frac{a+b}{2}$ on the board instead. Find all $n$ for which Alice can make a sequence of moves so that she ends up with only one number remaining on the board.
> 
> **Note.** When $n = 3$, Alice changes $\{1, 2, 3\}$ to $\{2, 3\}$ and after that Alice cannot make any further moves.
