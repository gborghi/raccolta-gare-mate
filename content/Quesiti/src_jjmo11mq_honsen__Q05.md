*Min total surveillance to suppress any rebellion of n*

> Let $n, N$ be positive integers with $2n \le N$. A certain country has $N$ citizens, and to suppress rebellions it uses the following method:
> 
> - Each citizen surveils a certain number of the other citizens.
> - When some of the citizens stage a rebellion, each citizen who did not rebel can arrest at most one of the citizens whom they surveil among those who rebelled.
> - If all the citizens who rebelled are arrested, then that rebellion is suppressed.
> 
> For all citizens, the number of citizens each one surveils was examined and these numbers were summed, giving $K$. Moreover, for any rebellion staged by at most $n$ citizens, there always exists a way to suppress it. Find the smallest possible value that $K$ can take. Note that even if citizen $A$ surveils citizen $B$, it is not necessarily the case that citizen $B$ surveils citizen $A$.
