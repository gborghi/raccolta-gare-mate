*If polynomials P,Q,R of degree less than m satisfy the given identity, prove each is zero.*

> Prove that if $m$ is a natural number and $P,Q,R$ polynomials of degrees less than $m$ satisfying
> $$x^{2m}P(x,y)+y^{2m}Q(x,y)=(x+y)^{2m}R(x,y),$$
> then each of the polynomials is zero.
