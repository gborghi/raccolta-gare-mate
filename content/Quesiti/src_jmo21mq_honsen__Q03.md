*Grid game: B can always terminate the game regardless of A's moves*

> Let $N$ be a positive integer. Consider a row of $N$ squares. Players A and B play the following game:
> 
> (1) First, A writes one non-negative integer in each of the $N$ squares.
> 
> (2) For any integer $i$ with $1 \le i \le N-1$, whenever the integer written in the $i$-th square from the left is greater than or equal to the integer written in the $(i+1)$-th square from the left (i.e., the squares are in non-increasing order from the left), the game ends. Otherwise, the following steps are repeated:
> 
> (a) A designates one non-negative integer.
> 
> (b) B chooses one of the $N$ squares and replaces the integer written in that square with the integer designated by A in step (a).
> 
> Prove that B can always force the game to end, regardless of A's strategy.
