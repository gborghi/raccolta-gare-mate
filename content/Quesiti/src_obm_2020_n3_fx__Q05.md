# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2020 — Nível 3 — Fase x — Quesito 5

*Parity questions to identify truth-tellers in a group of n people*

> Let $n$ and $k$ be positive integers with $k \le n$. In a group of $n$ people, each person either always tells the truth or always lies. Arnaldo may ask questions of any of these people of the following type: given a subset $A$ of size $k$ of the group, "what is the parity of the number of truth-tellers in $A$?" The answer can only be "even" (par) or "odd" (ímpar).
> 
> (a) (4 points) For which values of $n$ and $k$ is it possible to determine, for each person, whether they always tell the truth or always lie?
> 
> (b) (6 points) What is the minimum number of questions necessary to make this determination, when it is possible?
