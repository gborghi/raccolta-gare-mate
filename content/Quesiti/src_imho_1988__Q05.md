*Right triangle, incenters line meets sides, area inequality*

> $ABC$ is a triangle right-angled at $A$, and $D$ is the foot of the altitude from $A$. The straight line joining the incenters of the triangles $ABD$, $ACD$ intersects the sides $AB$, $AC$ at the points $K$, $L$ respectively. $S$ and $T$ denote the areas of the triangles $ABC$ and $AKL$ respectively. Show that $S \ge 2T$.
