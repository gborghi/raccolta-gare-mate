*Positive integers with harmonic relation, GCD yields perfect squares*

> Suppose $x, y, z$ are positive integers satisfying
> $$\frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{y} + \frac{1}{z}$$
> and let $k$ be the greatest common divisor of $x, y, z$.
> 
> Prove that $kxy$ is a perfect square.
> 
> Prove also that $k(y - x)$ is a perfect square.
