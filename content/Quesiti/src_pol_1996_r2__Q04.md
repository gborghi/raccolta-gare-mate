*Digit sequence: repeated consecutive pair among first 99 terms*

> Let $a_1, a_2, \ldots$ be a sequence of digits from the set $\{0, 1, \ldots, 9\}$ such that if $a_i = n$, $n \in \mathbb{N}$, $n \ge 10$, then $a_{i+1} \ge 2$, and if $a_i = 3$ then $a_{i+1} \ne 4$. Prove that there exist indices $i, j \in \{1, \ldots, 99\}$, $i \ne j$, such that $a_i = a_j$ and $a_{i+1} = a_{j+1}$.
