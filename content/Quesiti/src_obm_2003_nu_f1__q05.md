*Polynomial (1/n)((X+a)^n - X^n - a) has integer coefficients iff n is prime*

> Let $a$ and $n$ be integers with $n > 1$ and $\gcd(a, n) = 1$. Prove that the polynomial $\dfrac{1}{n}\bigl((X + a)^n - X^n - a\bigr)$ has all integer coefficients if and only if $n$ is prime.
