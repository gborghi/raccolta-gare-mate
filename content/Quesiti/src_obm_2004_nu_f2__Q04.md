# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2004 — Nível Universitário — Fase 2 — Quesito 4

*Construct integer polynomial vanishing at P_j but not divisible by p at Q*

> Let $\mathbb{Z}^k=\{(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_k):x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_k\in\mathbb{Z}\}$. Let $p$ be a prime, $k$ a positive integer, and $P_1,P_2,\ldots,P_n, Q\in\mathbb{Z}^k$ such that for all $j$, $1\le j\le k$,
> $$\frac{P_j-Q}{p}\in\mathbb{Z}^k.$$
> 
> Prove that there exists a polynomial $f(x_1,\ldots,x_k)$ with integer coefficients such that $f(P_j)=0$ for all $j$, $1\le j\le k$, and $\dfrac{f(Q)}{p}\notin\mathbb{Z}$.
