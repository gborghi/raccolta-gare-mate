*Prove two statements about fractions (a_j+a_{j+1})/gcd for prime/composite 2n-1*

> Prove the following statements:
> 
> (1) If $2n - 1$ is a prime, then for any group of distinct positive integers $a_1, a_2, \cdots, a_n$ (where $a_{n+1} = a_1$), there exists $j \in \{1, 2, \cdots, n\}$ such that $\dfrac{a_j + a_{j+1}}{(a_j, a_{j+1})} \ge 2n - 1$.
> 
> (2) If $2n - 1$ is a composite number, then there exists a group of distinct positive integers $a_1, a_2, \cdots, a_n$ such that $\dfrac{a_j + a_{j+1}}{(a_j, a_{j+1})} < 2n - 1$ for any $j \in \{1, 2, \cdots, n\}$.
> 
> Here $(x, y)$ denotes the greatest common divisor of positive integers $x$ and $y$.
