*Sum of k times permutations with k fixed points equals n!*

> Let pn(k) be the number of permutations of the set {1, . . . , n}, n ≥1, which
> have exactly k fixed points. Prove that
> n
> X
> k=0
> k · pn(k) = n!.
> (Remark: A permutation f of a set S is a one-to-one mapping of S onto itself.
> An element i in S is called a fixed point of the permutation f if f(i) = i.)
