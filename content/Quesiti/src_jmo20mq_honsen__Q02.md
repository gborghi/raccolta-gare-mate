# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2010 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 2

*Existence of n with n^n - m divisible by 2^k*

> Let $k$ be a positive integer and let $m$ be an odd number. Prove that there exists a positive integer $n$ such that $n^n - m$ is divisible by $2^k$.
