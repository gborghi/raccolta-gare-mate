# Coupe Animath Printemps 2014 — Quesito 3

*Two tangent circles, common tangent, four sub-questions on geometry*

> Two circles, one with centre $O$ and radius $R$, the other with centre $O'$ and radius $R'$ with $R < R'$, are externally tangent at point $B$. Let $T$ be a common tangent line passing through $B$. Draw another common tangent $[MM']$ (where $M$ belongs to the first circle and $M'$ to the second). This tangent meets $(T)$ at $A$. The lines $(OA)$ and $(O'A)$ cut $BM$ and $BM'$ at $D$ and $D'$ respectively. The lines $(OO')$ and $(DD')$ meet at $E$.
> 
> (1) Show that $(DD')$ is parallel to $(MM')$.
> 
> (2) Show that $OAO'$ is a right angle at $A$.
> 
> (3) Calculate the length $EO$ in terms of $R$ and $R'$.
> 
> (4) Calculate the length $DD'$ in terms of $R$ and $R'$.
