*Equidistant-points configuration bound k<1/2+sqrt(2n)*

> Let n and k be positive integers and let S be a set of n points in the plane such
> that
> (i)
> No three points of S are collinear, and
> (ii)
> For any point P of S there are at least k points of S equidistant from P.
> Prove that:
> k < 1
> 2 +
> √
> 2n.
> 
> 30th International Mathematical Olympiad
> Braunschweig, Germany
> Day II
