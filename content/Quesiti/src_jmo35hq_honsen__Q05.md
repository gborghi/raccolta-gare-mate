# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2025 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 5

*Interior points via angle bisectors; reflections yield similar triangles*

> Let $ABC$ be a non-isosceles acute triangle. There are three distinct points $A_1$, $B_1$, $C_1$ in the interior of triangle $ABC$ satisfying
> $$AB_1 : CB_1 = AB : CB \quad \text{and} \quad AC_1 : BC_1 = AC : BC.$$
> Let $A_2$ be the reflection of $A_1$ over line $BC$, let $B_2$ be the reflection of $B_1$ over line $AC$, and let $C_2$ be the reflection of $C_1$ over line $AB$. The following conditions all hold:
> \begin{itemize}
> \item The four points $A$, $A_2$, $B$, $C_2$ are concyclic.
> \item The four points $A$, $A_2$, $B_2$, $C$ are concyclic.
> \item The four points $B$, $B_2$, $C$, $C_2$ are concyclic.
> \item None of the points $A_2$, $B_2$, $C_2$ lies on the circumcircle of triangle $ABC$.
> \end{itemize}
> Prove that triangle $A_1B_1C_1$ is similar to triangle $A_2B_2C_2$. (Here, $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$.)
