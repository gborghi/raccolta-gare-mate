*Existence of X in SL(2,Z) such that X^t AX equals one of four matrices*

> Define $SL(2,\mathbb{Z})$ as the set of $2 \times 2$ matrices with integer entries and determinant $1$. Let $A \in SL(2,\mathbb{Z})$ be a matrix such that there exists $n > 0$ an integer with $A^n = I$. Prove that there exists $X \in SL(2,\mathbb{Z})$ such that $X^T A X$ equals one of the following matrices:
> $$\pm\begin{pmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{pmatrix},\quad \pm\begin{pmatrix}0 & -1\\1 & 0\end{pmatrix},\quad \pm\begin{pmatrix}0 & -1\\1 & -1\end{pmatrix},\quad \pm\begin{pmatrix}0 & -1\\1 & 1\end{pmatrix}.$$
