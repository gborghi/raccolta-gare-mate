*f(x)=ax+b|x|, g(x)=ax-b|x|; if f(f(x))=x for all x then prove g(g(x))=x for all x.*

> Let $a, b$ be real numbers. Consider the functions $f(x) = ax + b|x|$ and $g(x) = ax - b|x|$. Prove that if $f(f(x)) = x$ for every $x \in \mathbb{R}$, then $g(g(x)) = x$ for every $x \in \mathbb{R}$.
