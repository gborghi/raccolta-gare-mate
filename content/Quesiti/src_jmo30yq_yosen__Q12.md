*Max length of good sequence of distinct positive integers at most 30 with divisibility pattern*

> For a positive integer $k$, a sequence of $k$ distinct positive integers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_k$ is called a *good sequence* if it satisfies both of the following conditions:
> \begin{itemize}
>   \item All terms are at most $30$.
>   \item For each $i = 1, 2, \ldots, k-1$: if $i$ is odd, then $a_{i+1}$ is a multiple of $a_i$; if $i$ is even, then $a_{i+1}$ is a divisor of $a_i$.
> \end{itemize}
> Find the maximum possible length of a good sequence.
