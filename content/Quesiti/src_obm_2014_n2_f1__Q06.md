*Find which number has exactly 2014 positive divisors*

> To find the number of positive divisors of a positive integer $n$, it suffices to take its prime factorization and compute the product of the exponents of the primes each increased by 1. For example, $2880 = 2^5 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 5 \cdot 7$ has $(5+1)(2+1)(1+1)(1+1) = 5 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 = 36$ divisors. What is the least positive integer with exactly 2014 positive divisors?
> 
> (A) $2^{2^3 \cdot 19} \cdot 5^3$ $\quad$ (B) $2^{53} 3^{18} 5^2$ $\quad$ (C) $2^{52} 3^{18} 5$ $\quad$ (D) $2^{18} 5^3$ $\quad$ (E) $2^{27} 5^2$
