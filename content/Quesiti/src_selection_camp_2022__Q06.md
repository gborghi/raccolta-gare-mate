*Three lines AM, PY, QX concur in a circle configuration*

> Let $\Gamma$ be a circle with diameter $MN$, and let $A$ be a point in the interior of $\Gamma$ (not on $\Gamma$). Let the circle centered at $N$ passing through $A$ meet $\Gamma$ at points $B$ and $C$. Let $P$ and $Q$ be distinct points on segment $BC$ (excluding the endpoints) such that $\angle BAP = \angle QAC$. Among the intersections of line $NP$ with $\Gamma$, let $X$ be the one other than $N$; among the intersections of line $NQ$ with $\Gamma$, let $Y$ be the one other than $N$. Prove that the three lines $AM$, $PY$, and $QX$ are concurrent.
