*Partition of integers 1 to 99 into groups so no two in a group sum to a multiple of 3*

> The integers from $1$ to $99$ are divided into $n$ groups, obeying the following rules:
> 
> I- each number belongs to exactly one group;
> 
> II- each group has at least two distinct elements;
> 
> III- if two numbers belong to the same group, then their sum is not a number divisible by $3$.
> 
> Knowing this:
> 
> a) Explain why the number $n$ of groups cannot be $50$.
> 
> b) What is the smallest possible value of $n$?
