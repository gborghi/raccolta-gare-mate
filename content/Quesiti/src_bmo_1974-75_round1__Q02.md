# British Mathematical Olympiad 1974-75 — Round 1 — Quesito 2

*Primes partition into sets A and B; divisibility of product difference*

> The $n$ prime numbers $2, 3, 5, \ldots, p_n$ are partitioned into two disjoint sets $A$ and $B$. The primes in $A$ are $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_h$ and the primes in $B$ are $b_1, b_2, \ldots, b_k$.
> 
> The two products $\displaystyle\prod_{i=1}^{h} a_i$ and $\displaystyle\prod_{j=1}^{k} b_j$ are formed where the $a_i$ and $b_j$ are any positive integers.
> 
> If $4$ divides the difference between these two products prove that either $d = 1$ or $d = p_n$.
