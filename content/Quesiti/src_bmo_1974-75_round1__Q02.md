*Divisor of difference of prime-set products is 1 or at least p_n*

> The first $n$ prime numbers, $2, 3, 5, \ldots, p_n$ are partitioned into two disjoint sets $A$ and $B$. The primes in $A$ are $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \ldots, \alpha_h$ and the primes in $B$ are $\beta_1, \beta_2, \ldots, \beta_k$, where $h + k = n$.
> 
> The two products $\displaystyle\prod_{1}^{h} \alpha_i^{a_i}$ and $\displaystyle\prod_{1}^{k} \beta_i^{b_i}$ are formed where the $a_i$ and $\beta_i$ are any positive integers.
> 
> If $d$ divides the difference between these products show that either $d = 1$ or $d \ge p_n$.
