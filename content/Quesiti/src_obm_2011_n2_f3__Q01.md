*Magic-sum 3x3 grid with multiples of 3*

> In a $3 \times 3$ table we write the numbers $1$ to $9$, one in each cell. We then compute the sum of the numbers in each row, in each column, and in each diagonal, obtaining $8$ sums in total (the three rows, three columns, and two diagonals). For example, in the table below all $8$ numbers are multiples of $3$.
> 
> $$\begin{array}{|c|c|c|}\hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 7 & 8 & 9 \\ \hline\end{array}$$
> 
> Is it possible that none of the $8$ sums is a multiple of $3$? Remember to justify your answer.
