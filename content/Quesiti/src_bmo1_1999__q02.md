*Ratio of tangents constant as P moves on circle with diameter AB*

> A circle has diameter $AB$ and $X$ is a fixed point of $AB$ lying between $A$ and $B$. A point $P$, distinct from $A$ and $B$, lies on the circumference of the circle. Prove that
> $$\frac{\tan \angle APX}{\tan \angle PAX}$$
> remains constant as $P$ moves around the circle.
