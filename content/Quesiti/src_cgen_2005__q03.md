*Iterating the incenter (then later the orthocenter) of a triangle from three non-collinear points; prove convergence of the sequence of points and study what changes when using the orthocenter.*

> We consider in the plane three points $A_0$, $B$, $C$ that are non-collinear.
> 
> **1.** We denote by $A_1$ the center of the circle inscribed in the triangle $A_0BC$ (that is, the point of intersection of the interior bisectors of the triangle $A_0BC$). We pursue the process by considering $A_2$, center of the circle inscribed in the triangle $A_1BC$, etc. Thus, for every natural integer $i$, $A_{i+1}$ is the center of the circle inscribed in the triangle $A_iBC$. Show that there exists a point $A$, limit of the sequence $(A_n)$, that is to say that $AA_n$ tends towards $0$ when $n$ tends to its position.
> 
> **2.** What becomes of the previous result if, at each step, for $i=0,1,2,\ldots$, we take $A_{i+1}$ to be the orthocenter of the triangle $A_iBC$ instead of the center of the inscribed circle?
