*Sum of weights of all continued fraction expansions of p/q equals q*

> Let $p$ and $q$ be integers with $q \ge 2$, and suppose $p$ and $q$ are mutually coprime. We say that the sequence of integers $(r; a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n)$ is a **continued fraction expansion** of $\dfrac{p}{q}$ if all of $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ have absolute value at least $2$, and
> $$\frac{p}{q} = r + \cfrac{1}{a_1 + \cfrac{1}{a_2 + \cfrac{1}{\ddots + \cfrac{1}{a_n}}}}.$$
> For example,
> $$\frac{-10}{7} = -1 + \cfrac{1}{-3 + \cfrac{1}{2 + \cfrac{1}{-2}}}$$
> so $(-1,\,-3,\,2,\,-2)$ is a continued fraction expansion of $\dfrac{-10}{7}$.
> 
> The **weight** of a continued fraction expansion $(r; a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n)$ is defined to be
> $$(|a_1| - 1) \times (|a_2| - 1) \times \cdots \times (|a_n| - 1).$$
> For example, the weight of the continued fraction expansion $(-1;\,-3,\,2,\,-2)$ of $\dfrac{-10}{7}$ is $2$.
> 
> Prove that the sum of the weights of all continued fraction expansions of $\dfrac{p}{q}$ equals $q$.
