*Find the volume of a tetrahedron given two opposite edges, their distance and angle.*

> Suppose in the tetrahedron $ABCD$, $AB = 1$, $CD = \sqrt{3}$, the distance and angle between the lines $AB$ and $CD$ are $2$ and $\frac{\pi}{3}$ respectively. Then the volume of the tetrahedron equals ( ).
> 
> (A) $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ \quad (B) $\frac{1}{2}$ \quad (C) $\frac{1}{3}$ \quad (D) $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$
