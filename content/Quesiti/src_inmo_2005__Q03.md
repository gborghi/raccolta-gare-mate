*Three equations share root; prove root negative and third has non-real roots*

> Let $p, q, r$ be real numbers, not all equal, such that some two of the equations
> $$px^2 + 2qx + r = 0, \quad qx^2 + 2rx + p = 0, \quad rx^2 + 2px + q = 0$$
> have a common root, say $\alpha$. Prove that
> (a) $\alpha$ is real and negative; and
> (b) the third equation has non-real roots.
