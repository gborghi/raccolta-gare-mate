*Disjoint quadrilaterals with 3-same-1-other colored vertices*

> Among the $100$ vertices of a regular $100$-gon, $41$ are colored black and the remaining $59$ are colored white. Prove that one can choose $24$ convex quadrilaterals $Q_1, Q_2, \ldots, Q_{24}$, each having four of these $100$ vertices as its vertices, satisfying the following two conditions:
> \begin{itemize}
> \item No two of $Q_1, \ldots, Q_{24}$ have any common point (neither in their interiors nor on their boundaries).
> \item For every integer $i$ with $1 \le i \le 24$, among the four vertices of $Q_i$ exactly three are of the same color and the remaining one is of the other color.
> \end{itemize}
