*Edges of a cube numbered 1 through 12; prove at least eight index-increasing triples of consecutive edges exist, and exhibit a numbering with exactly eight.*

> The edges of a cube are numbered 1 through 12.
> 
> (a) Prove that for any such numbering there exist at least eight triples of integers $(i, j, k)$ with $1 \le i < j < k \le 12$ such that the edges assigned numbers $i$, $j$, $k$ are consecutive segments of a polygonal line.
> 
> (b) Give an example of a numbering for which there are exactly eight such triples.
