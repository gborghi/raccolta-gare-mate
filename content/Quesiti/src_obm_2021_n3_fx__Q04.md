*Find smallest real belonging to every framed (bounded, closed under (a-b)^2) set*

> A set $X$ of real numbers, finite or infinite, is called *bounded* when there exists a real $M$ such that $|x| \le M$ for all $x \in X$. For example, $A = [-3, n]$ and $B = \{2^k : k \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\} = \{1, 2, 4, 8, \ldots\}$ are bounded (taking $M \ge n$ and $M \ge 1$, respectively). On the other hand, $C = \mathbb{Z}$ and $D = \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$ are not bounded.
> 
> A set $A$ of real numbers is called *framed* when it is bounded and, for all distinct $a, b \in A$, we have $(a - b)^2 \in A$.
> 
> What is the smallest real number that belongs to every framed set?
