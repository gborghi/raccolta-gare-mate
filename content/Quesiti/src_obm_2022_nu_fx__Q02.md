*Show group G of integer matrices is generated by A and B*

> Consider the set $G$ of $2 \times 2$ matrices given by
> $$G = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \,\middle|\, a,b,c,d \in \mathbb{Z},\; ad - bc = 1,\; c \text{ is a multiple of } 3 \right\}$$
> and the matrices
> $$A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \qquad B = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 \end{pmatrix}.$$
> Show that any matrix in $G$ can be written as a product $M_1 M_2 \cdots M_r$, with $M_i \in \{A, A^{-1}, B, B^{-1}\}$, for all $i \le r$.
