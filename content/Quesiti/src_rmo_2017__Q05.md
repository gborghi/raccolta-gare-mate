*Circle tangent to AB, internally to two circles; prove XY is a diameter*

> Let $\Omega$ be a circle with a chord $AB$ which is not a diameter. Let $\Gamma_1$ be a circle on one side of $AB$ such that it is tangent to $AB$ at $C$ and internally tangent to $\Omega$ at $D$. Similarly, let $\Gamma_2$ be a circle on the other side of $AB$ such that it is tangent to $AB$ at $E$ and internally tangent to $\Omega$ at $F$. Suppose the line $DC$ intersects $\Omega$ at a point $X \ne D$ and the line $FE$ intersects $\Omega$ at a point $Y \ne F$. Prove that $XY$ is a diameter of $\Omega$.
