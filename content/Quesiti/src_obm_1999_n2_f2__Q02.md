# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 1999 — Nível 2 — Fase 2 — Quesito 2

*Three integers on a board; each move erases two and writes their sum minus one; can starting from 2,2,2 or 3,3,3 yield 17,75,91?*

> On a blackboard three integers are written. Then a sequence of moves is performed in which, at each step, one erases two of them and writes in their place a number equal to the sum of the two erased ones diminished by one unit. After several moves the numbers $17$, $75$ and $91$ are obtained on the blackboard. Is it possible that the numbers written at the start were:
> 
> a) $2, 2, 2$ ?
> 
> b) $3, 3, 3$ ?
