*Identify which pair is NOT 'primanos' (in a 3-term all-prime AP)*

> Two integer numbers are called $\textit{primanos}$ when they belong to an arithmetic progression of three prime terms. For example, the numbers $41$ and $59$ are primanos, because they belong to the arithmetic progression $(41, 47, 53, 59)$ which contains only prime numbers. Among the alternatives below, the two numbers that are $\textbf{not}$ primanos are:
> (A) $7$ and $11$ (B) $13$ and $53$ (C) $41$ and $131$ (D) $31$ and $43$ (E) $23$ and $41$
