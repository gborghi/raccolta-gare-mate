*Largest r such that n reals contain r with no two at distance 1 (or alpha)*

> Let $n$ be a strictly positive integer.
> 
> 1) Determine, in terms of $n$, the largest integer $r$ possessing the following property: for all reals $a_1, \ldots, a_n$, there exist $r$ reals $a_{i_1} < a_{i_2} < \cdots < a_{i_r}$ among them such that for all integers $k, \ell$ verifying $1 \le k, \ell \le r$, one has $|a_{i_k} - a_{i_\ell}| \ne 1$.
> 
> 2) Suppose $\alpha$ is an irrational number. Determine, in terms of $n$, the largest integer $r$ possessing the following property: for all reals $a_1, \ldots, a_n$, there exist $r$ reals $a_{i_1} < a_{i_2} < \cdots < a_{i_r}$ among them such that for all integers $k, \ell$ verifying $1 \le k, \ell \le r$, one has $|a_{i_k} - a_{i_\ell}| \ne 1$ and $|a_{i_k} - a_{i_\ell}| \ne \alpha$.
> 
> An irrational number is a real number that cannot be written in the form $\frac{p}{q}$, where $p$ and $q$ are integers and $q \ne 0$.
