*13x17 grid numbered first by rows then re-numbered by columns; count cells where the two numbers coincide.*

> In a rectangular grid of 13 rows and 17 columns we place numbers in each cell in the following way: first, we number the cells of the first row in increasing order, from left to right, with the numbers 1, 2, ..., 17; then we number the cells of the second row, also from left to right, in increasing order, with the numbers 18, 19, ..., 34, and we proceed analogously with the other rows, so that the numbers of the first column are, from top to bottom, 1, 18, 35, etc. After numbering all the cells, we redo the numbering, now by columns: we number the cells of the first column, from top to bottom, in increasing order, with the numbers 1, 2, ..., 13; then we number the cells of the second column, also from top to bottom, in increasing order, with the numbers 14, 15, ..., 26, and we proceed analogously with the other columns. In this way, each cell ends up with two numbers. In how many cells do the two numbers coincide?
> 
> (A) 2 \quad (B) 3 \quad (C) 4 \quad (D) 5 \quad (E) 6
