# Indian National Mathematical Olympiad 1998 — Quesito 5

*Real quadratic with complex roots implies triangle inequality*

> Suppose $a, b, c$ are real numbers such that the quadratic equation
> $$x^2 - (a + b + c)x + (ab + bc + ca) = 0$$
> has roots of the form $\alpha \pm i\beta$, where $\alpha > 0$ and $\beta \neq 0$ are real numbers. Show that:
> (a) The numbers $a, b, c$ are all positive.
> (b) The numbers $\sqrt{a}$, $\sqrt{b}$, $\sqrt{c}$ are the sides of a triangle.
