# IMO 1997 (38th IMO, Mar del Plata) — Quesito 6

*Bound f(2^n), partitions of n into powers of 2*

> For each positive integer n , let f(n) denote the number of ways of representing
> n as a sum of powers of 2 with nonnegative integer exponents. Representations
> which differ only in the ordering of their summands are considered to be the
> same. For instance, f(4) = 4, because the number 4 can be represented in the
> following four ways:
> 4; 2 + 2; 2 + 1 + 1; 1 + 1 + 1 + 1.
> Prove that, for any integer n ≥3,
> 2n2/4 < f(2n) < 2n2/2.
