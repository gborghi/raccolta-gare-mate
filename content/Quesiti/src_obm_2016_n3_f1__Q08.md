*Finding angle x from configuration with regular polygons (square, pentagon, octagon) and isosceles triangles*

> The interior angle of a regular polygon with $n$ sides is $\frac{180^\circ(n-2)}{n}$. In the configuration one has $\angle ABL=90^\circ$, $\angle ABI=108^\circ$ and $\angle ABC=135^\circ$ (angles of a square, a regular pentagon and a regular octagon), hence $\angle CBI=27^\circ$ and $\angle LBC=360^\circ-90^\circ-135^\circ=135^\circ$. Since $LB=BC=BI$, triangles $LBC$ and $CBI$ are isosceles with bases $LC$ and $CI$, giving $x=\angle LCB+\angle BCI=\frac{180^\circ-135^\circ}{2}+\frac{180^\circ-27^\circ}{2}=99^\circ$. Find $x$. [Answer key value: $x=99^\circ$.]
