*Expected waiting time for traveler choosing between two trains*

> Every day at a station, there is just one train arriving between 8:10 am and 10:00 am. The arrival times and their probabilities for the two trains (Train A and Train B) are shown in the following table:
> 
> | Arrival time | Train A | Train B |
> |---|---|---|
> | 8:10 | $\dfrac{1}{2}$ | |
> | 8:30 | $\dfrac{1}{4}$ | |
> | 9:10 | $\dfrac{1}{4}$ | |
> | 9:30 | | $\dfrac{1}{2}$ |
> | 10:10 | | $\dfrac{1}{2}$ |
> 
> Suppose that the events of the two trains are independent of each other. Now, a traveler comes into the station at 8:20. Then the mathematical expectation of his waiting time is \_\_\_\_ (round to the nearest minute).
