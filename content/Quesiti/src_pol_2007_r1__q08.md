*For a prime p, show there is a permutation of 1..p-1 whose partial products are all distinct modulo p.*

> Let $p$ be a prime number. Show that there exists a permutation $(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_{p-1})$ of the numbers $1,2,\ldots,p-1$ such that the numbers
> $$x_1,\, x_1x_2,\, x_1x_2x_3,\ldots,\, x_1x_2\cdots x_{p-1}$$
> are distinct modulo $p$.
