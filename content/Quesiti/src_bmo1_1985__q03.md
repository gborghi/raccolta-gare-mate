*Prove identity involving binomial coefficients product*

> $n$ and $m$ are non-negative integers. Prove that
> $$2 \binom{n+1}{1} + 3 \binom{n+2}{2} + \cdots + (r+1)\binom{n+r}{r} + \cdots + (m+1)\binom{n+m}{m} = \binom{n+m+2}{m},$$
> where $\binom{p}{r}$ is the binomial coefficient $p(p-1)(p-2)\cdots(p-r+1)/r!$.
