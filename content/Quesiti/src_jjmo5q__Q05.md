*5×5 grid numbered 1–16; find minimum m for a column-sum condition*

> There is a $5 \times 5$ grid. The numbers $1, 2, \ldots, 16$ are written into the top-left $4 \times 4$ portion of cells (one number per cell) in some order. For each of the four rows (rows $1$–$4$), the running cumulative sum of the four entries in that row, read left to right, is recorded in the rightmost cell of that row (marked $\clubsuit$). Similarly, for each of the four columns, the cumulative sum from row $1$ to row $4$ is recorded in the bottom cell of that column. The bottom-right cell is left blank. The following condition must be satisfied:
> 
> \textbf{Condition ($\clubsuit$):} At the moment when the running sums for a given row are being recorded, if the cumulative total reaches $m$ or more in the rightmost entry, then at least $2$ of the cells in that row's running sums are $m$ or more. Furthermore, in row $5$ (the bottom row), at least $2$ of the four column sums are $m$ or more.
> 
> Find the smallest integer $m$ satisfying this condition.
