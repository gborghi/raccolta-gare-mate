*Compare volumes of two solids of revolution related to parabola and line*

> A region is enclosed by the curves $x^2 = 4y$, $x - 2y - 4 = 0$, $x = 4$ and $y = -4$. $V_1$ is the volume of the solid obtained by rotating the above region round the $y$-axis. Another region consists of points $(x, y)$ satisfying $x^2 \ge 4y$, $|x| \le 4$, $y \ge -4$. $V_2$ is the volume of the solid obtained by rotating this region round the $y$-axis. Then \quad (\quad).
> 
> (A) $V_1 = \dfrac{1}{2}V_2$ \qquad (B) $V_1 = \dfrac{2}{3}V_2$ \qquad (C) $V_1 = V_2$ \qquad (D) $V_1 = 2V_2$
