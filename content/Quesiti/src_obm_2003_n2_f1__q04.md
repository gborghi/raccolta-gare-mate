*Calculator keys A (2x+1) and B (3x-1) from 5; largest two-digit number*

> A certain calculator has a display that shows a non-negative integer $x$, and two keys A and B. Key A replaces the display number by $2x + 1$. When key B is pressed, the display number is replaced by $3x - 1$.
> 
> If the display currently shows the number 5 and you press the keys A and B (in any order and any number of times), the largest two-digit number you can obtain is:
> 
> (A) 85 \quad (B) 87 \quad (C) 92 \quad (D) 95 \quad (E) 96
