*Two spheres each externally tangent to five vertex-spheres of octahedron; common point?*

> $A_1 A_2 A_3 A_4 A_5 A_6$ is a regular octahedron whose sides are each of length $2a$. For each $i = 1, 2, \ldots, 5$, $K_i$ is a sphere with centre $A_i$ and radius $r_i$. Each of these spheres $K_1, K_2, \ldots, K_5$ are all touched externally by each of two spheres $P_1$ and $P_2$ of radius $a$. Determine with proof and without tables whether $P_1$ and $P_2$ have or have not a common point.
