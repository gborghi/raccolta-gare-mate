# Coupe Animath Printemps 2020 — Quesito 12

*With min x_i >= max y_i, prove P <= G <= 2P for P=max(x_i-y_i), G=max x_i - min y_i*

> Let $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ and $y_1, \ldots, y_n$ be two lists of reals such that $\min_{1 \le i \le n} x_i \ge \max_{1 \le i \le n} y_i$. Set $P = \max_{1 \le i \le n}(x_i - y_i)$ and $G = \max_{1 \le i \le n} x_i - \min_{1 \le i \le n} y_i$. Show that $P \le G \le 2P$.
> 
> *Given reals $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$, the number $\min_{1 \le i \le n} a_i$ denotes the smallest real among the numbers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$. The number $\max_{1 \le i \le n} a_i$ denotes the largest real among the numbers $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$.*
