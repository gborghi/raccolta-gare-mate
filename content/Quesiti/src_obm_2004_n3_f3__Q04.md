*10x10 table with digits 0–9 each 10 times; largest n guaranteeing a row/column with ≥n distinct digits*

> Consider all ways of placing the digits $0, 1, 2, \ldots, 9$ in the cells of a $10 \times 10$ table such that each digit appears exactly ten times.
> 
> Find the largest integer $n$ with the property that, in every such placement, some row or some column contains at least $n$ distinct digits.
