*No integer alternating product of A^a_i B^b_i equals identity for n>1*

> Consider the matrices
> $$A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \quad \text{and} \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix}.$$
> Prove that, for $n > 1$, there do not exist integers $a_1, a_2, a_3, \ldots, a_n$ and $b_1, b_2, \ldots, b_{n-1}, b_n$ with $a_2, a_3, \ldots, a_n$ and $b_1, b_2, \ldots, b_{n-1}$ all nonzero, such that
> $$A^{a_1} \cdot B^{b_1} \cdot A^{a_2} \cdot B^{b_2} \cdots A^{a_n} \cdot B^{b_n} = I,$$
> where $I$ is the $2 \times 2$ identity matrix.
