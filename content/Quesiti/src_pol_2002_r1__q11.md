*With an angle condition on triangle ABC, a circle tangent to AC at A through D on BC meets AB at P; prove BP/AC = BD/DC.*

> In the triangle $ABC$ it holds that $\angle B > \angle C$. The point $D$ lies on the side $BC$ and satisfies the equality $\angle DAC = (1/2)(\angle B - \angle C)$. The circle tangent to the line $AC$ in the point $A$ and containing the point $D$ intersects the line $AB$ in the point $P \ne A$. Prove that
> $$\frac{BP}{AC} = \frac{BD}{DC}.$$
