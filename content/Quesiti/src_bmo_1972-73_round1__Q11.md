# British Mathematical Olympiad 1972-73 — Round 1 — Quesito 11

*Two cylinders; one rolls on the other; differential equation for angle; obtain theta-dot*

> (i) Two uniform rough right circular cylinders $A$ and $B$, with the same length, have radii $a$ and $b$ and masses $m_a$ and $m_b$ respectively.
> 
> $A$ rests with a generator in contact with a rough horizontal table. Initially in unstable equilibrium, with its axis vertically above $A$'s, $B$ rolls on $A$ on the table. In the subsequent motion the plane containing the axes makes an angle $\theta$ with the vertical.
> 
> Draw diagrams showing angles, forces etc., for the period when there is no slipping. Write down equations which will give on elimination a differential equation for $\theta$, stating the principles used. You are not asked to do it.
> 
> (ii) Such a differential equation is, with $k = 10^{10}$:
> $$\ddot{\theta}(4 + 2\cos\theta - 2\cos^2\theta) + \dot{\theta}^2 \sin\theta\,(2\cos\theta - 1) + k^2 \sin\theta = \frac{3(1 + k)\sin\theta}{a + b}.$$
> 
> Obtain $\dot{\theta}$ in terms of $\theta$.
> 
> [Moment of inertia of a uniform cylinder about its axis is $\frac{1}{2}\,(\text{mass})(\text{radius})^2$.]
