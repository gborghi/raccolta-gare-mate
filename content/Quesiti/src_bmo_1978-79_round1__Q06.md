*Infinite sequence 1001,100010001,...; prove no prime divides all terms*

> Consider the infinite sequence of integers
> $$1001, \quad 100010001, \quad 1000100010001, \quad \ldots$$
> where each integer after the first is obtained by adjoining $0001$ to the digits of the previous integer.
> 
> Prove that there is no prime number that divides all the integers in the sequence.
