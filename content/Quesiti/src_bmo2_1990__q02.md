*With fractional part f(x)=x-[x], find a positive x with f(x)+f(1/x)=1; ask whether rational solutions exist.*

> The \textit{integer part} $[x]$ of a number $x$ is the greatest integer which is not greater than $x$. The \textit{fractional part} $f(x)$ is defined by $f(x) = x - [x]$. Find a positive number $x$ such that $$f(x) + f\left(\frac{1}{x}\right) = 1.$$ Are there any \textit{rational} solutions?
