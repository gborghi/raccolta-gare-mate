*Sequence where each digit except last has opposite parity to successor*

> Given any integer $n > 1$ prove that there exists an infinity of positive integers $m$ such that the last $m$ decimal digits of $5^m$ form a sequence in which each digit except the last is of opposite parity to its successor; i.e. if one is odd then the next is even and vice versa.
