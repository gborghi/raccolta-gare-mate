*Prove inequality for four different positive reals or find counterexample*

> $a, b, c, d$ are different positive real numbers. Prove that if at least one of the numbers $c$ and $d$ lies between the numbers $a$ and $b$, or at least one of the numbers $a$ and $b$ lies between the numbers $c$ and $d$, then
> $$(*) \quad \sqrt{(a+b)(c+d)} \ge \sqrt{ab} + \sqrt{cd}.$$
> Otherwise show that the four numbers can be chosen so that $(*)$ is false.
