# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2016 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 5

*Partition m cities into two groups so each city has at most 1 highway*

> Let $m, n$ be positive integers with $m \ge 2$ and $n < \dfrac{3}{2}(m-1)$. A country has $m$ cities and $n$ roads, where each road connects two distinct cities (there may be multiple roads between the same pair of cities).
> 
> The cities are to be divided into two groups $\alpha$ and $\beta$, and every road connecting a city in group $\alpha$ to a city in group $\beta$ is designated a highway. Prove that there exists such a partition satisfying both of the following conditions:
> 
> - Each group contains at least one city.
> - For every city, the number of highways incident to that city is at most $1$.
