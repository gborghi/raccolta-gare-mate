# IMO 1989 (30th IMO, Braunschweig) — Quesito 6

*More permutations of 1..2n with property P than without*

> A permutation (x1, x2, . . . , xm) of the set {1, 2, . . . , 2n}, where n is a positive
> integer, is said to have property P if |xi −xi+1| = n for at least one i in
> {1, 2, . . . , 2n −1}. Show that, for each n, there are more permutations with
> property P than without.
