# Japan IMO Selection Camp 2024 — Selection Camp — Quesito 12

*Acute triangle, orthocenter, circumcircles, reflection; prove four points concyclic*

> Let $ABC$ be an acute triangle with $AB < AC$, and let $H$ be its orthocenter. Let $D$ be a point on side $BC$ (excluding its endpoints) with $AB = AD$, and let $P$ be a point on segment $BD$ (excluding its endpoints). Line $BH$ meets the circumcircle of triangle $ABC$ again at a point $E$ (with $E \neq B$). Let $Q$ be the second intersection (other than $D$) of the circumcircle of triangle $ABD$ and the circumcircle of triangle $DHP$, and let $Q'$ be the reflection of $Q$ across line $AB$. Prove that the four points $B$, $E$, $P$, $Q'$ lie on a common circle (are concyclic). Here $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$.
