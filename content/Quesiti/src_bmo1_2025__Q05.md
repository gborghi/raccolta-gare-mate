*Prove at least one of (n+1)^p-1 or (n+1)^(p-1)-1 divisible by p*

> Let $p$ be a prime number, and let $n$ be the smallest positive integer, strictly greater than $1$, for which $n^p - 1$ is divisible by $p$.
> 
> Prove that at least one of $(n+1)^p - 1$ and $(n+1)^{p-1} - 1$ is divisible by $p$.
