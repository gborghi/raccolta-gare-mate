# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2020 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 4

*Two players, special points on circle; show fraction of connected pairs is large*

> Let $n \ge 2$ be an integer. Suppose there are $3n$ distinct points on a circle, called special points. Players $A$ and $B$ alternately perform the following operation $n$ times each.
> 
> First, Player $A$ chooses 2 special points that are not yet connected by a segment and connects them with a segment. Then, Player $B$ chooses 1 special point that has not yet had a piece placed on it and places a piece on it.
> 
> Regardless of how Player $B$ plays, prove that when all $n$ operations are finished, Player $A$ can always make it so that the number of segments connecting a special point with a piece on it to a special point without a piece on it is at least $\dfrac{n}{6}$.
