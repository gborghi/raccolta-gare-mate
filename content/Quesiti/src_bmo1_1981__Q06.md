*Sum of a_i^k / P_i over distinct nonzero integers is always an integer*

> $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n$ are distinct non-zero integers.
> $$P_i = \prod_{j \ne i}(a_i - a_j)$$
> is the product of the $(n-1)$ factors $(a_i - a_1)$, $(a_i - a_2)$, $\ldots$, $(a_i - a_{n-1})$, the zero factor $(a_i - a_i)$ being excluded.
> 
> Prove that if $k$ is a non-negative integer,
> $$\sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{a_i^k}{P_i}$$
> is an integer.
