*Given integer m>=2, find the smallest n>=m so that every 2-coloring of {m,...,n} yields a monochromatic a,b,c with ab=c.*

> Let be given an integer $m \ge 2$. Find the smallest integer $n \ge m$ with the property that, for every partition of the set $\{m, m+1, \ldots, n\}$ into two subsets, one of the subsets contains three numbers $a, b, c$ (not necessarily distinct) with $ab = c$.
