*Alternating sum of products of consecutive integers divisible by m!*

> $m$ and $n$ are positive integers. $S_n$ is the sum of $m$ terms of
> $$[(n+1)(n+2)] - [(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)] + [(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)(n+4)] - \cdots$$
> where the terms alternate in sign and each, after the first, is the product of consecutive integers with the number of integers one more than before.
> 
> Prove that $S_n$ is divisible by $m!$ but not necessarily by $(m+1)!$.
