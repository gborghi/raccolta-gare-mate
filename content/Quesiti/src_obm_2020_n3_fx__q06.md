*Iterates of f(x)=2x^2+x-1: real fixed points and real zeros*

> Let $f(x) = 2x^2 + x - 1$. Define $f^{n+1}(x) = f(f^n(x))$ for all real $x$ and $n \ge 0$ (that is, $f^n$ is the $n$-th iterate of $f$).
> 
> (a) (2 points) Determine the number of distinct real solutions of the equation $f^1(x) = x$.
> 
> (b) (8 points) Determine, for each non-negative integer $n$, the number of distinct real solutions of the equation $f^n(x) = 0$.
