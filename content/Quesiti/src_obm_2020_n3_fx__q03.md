*Mixtilinear circles in a triangle: common tangents concurrent and isogonal conjugates*

> Let $r_A$, $r_B$, $r_C$ be the mixtilinear inradii of triangle $XYZ$ centered at $P$ in the interior of the triangle. The circle $\omega_A$ (center $X$) is internally tangent to $r_B r_C$; the circle $\omega_B$ (center $Y$) is internally tangent to $r_C r_A$; and the circle $\omega_C$ (center $Z$) is internally tangent to $r_A r_B$. The point $P$ lies in the interior of triangle $XYZ$ such that the lines $r_A$, $r_B$, $r_C$ are the common internal tangents to the corresponding pairs of circles. Let $s_A$ be the line internally tangent to $\omega_B$ and $\omega_C$ that does not contain $r_A$; let $s_B$ be the line internally tangent to $\omega_A$ and $\omega_C$ that does not contain $r_B$; let $s_C$ be the line internally tangent to $\omega_A$ and $\omega_B$ that does not contain $r_C$. Prove that $s_A$, $s_B$, and $s_C$ meet at a common point $Q$, and that $P$ and $Q$ are isogonal conjugates in triangle $XYZ$ — that is, the lines $XP$ and $XQ$ are symmetric with respect to the angle bisector of $\angle YXZ$, and similarly for vertices $Y$ and $Z$.
