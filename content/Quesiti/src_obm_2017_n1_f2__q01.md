*Prove a nonconstant polynomial with nonneg coefficients whose power is positive is itself positive*

> We say that a polynomial $f$ is *positive* if it can be written as the product of two nonconstant polynomials with all real coefficients greater than or equal to $0$. Let $f(x)$ be a nonconstant polynomial with nonnegative coefficients such that $f^n$ is positive for some positive integer $n$. Prove that $f(x)$ is positive.
> 
> **Remark.** The problem statement is incorrect. The hypothesis that the leading coefficient of $f$ is non-zero (i.e., $f$ is non-zero) was missing. Alternatively, the conclusion that $f(x)$ is positive is false as stated, since $f(x) = cx$ with $c$ a positive integer is a counterexample. Students who give an answer correcting the problem by adding a hypothesis such as $f(1) = cx$ with $c$ a positive integer, as well as those who correct the problem by adding such a hypothesis as mentioned above and then solve it, will receive full marks together with an apology from the jury.
