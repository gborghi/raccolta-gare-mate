# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2007 — Nível 3 — Fase 2 — Quesito 9

*Minimum number of roads among 20 cities to guarantee the road network is connected*

> Choose $20$ cities of a country. If one linked every two of them by a road one would use $\binom{20}{2} = 190$ roads. Find the least number of roads that must be built so that, starting from any city, one can reach any other city by car (that is, so the road network is connected). Show that $190$ roads need not be enough, and prove that with $191$ roads it is always possible to travel by car between any two cities. (Hint: if the cities could be split into two groups $A$ and $B$ with $a$ and $b$ cities respectively, $a + b = 21$, with no road joining the two groups, then the number of roads would be at most $\binom{a}{2} + \binom{b}{2}$; derive a contradiction.)
