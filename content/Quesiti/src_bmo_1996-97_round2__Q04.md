# British Mathematical Olympiad 1996-97 — Round 2 — Quesito 4

*Set S of reciprocals; find maximal arithmetic progressions in S of length 5*

> The set $S = \left\{ \frac{1}{1+t} : t = 1, 2, 3, \ldots \right\}$ of reciprocals of various positive integers contains arithmetic progressions of various lengths. For instance, $\{1/20, 1/8, 3/20\}$ is such a progression (with common difference $1/40$). Moreover, this is a maximal arithmetic progression in $S$ (i.e., it cannot be extended by including another member of $S$ in arithmetic progression at either end).
> 
> Find, with proof:
> (i) a maximal arithmetic progression in $S$ of length $5$;
> (ii) Is there a maximal progression in $S$ of length greater than $5$ in $1996$--$1997$?
