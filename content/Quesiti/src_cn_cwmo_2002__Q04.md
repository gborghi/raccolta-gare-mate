*Prove two disjoint index sets give equal unions of subsets*

> Assume that $n$ is a positive integer, and $A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_{n+1}$ are $n+1$ nonempty subsets of the set $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$. Prove that there are two disjoint and nonempty subsets $\{i_1, i_2, \ldots, i_k\}$ and $\{j_1, j_2, \ldots, j_m\}$ such that
> $$A_{i_1} \cup A_{i_2} \cup \cdots \cup A_{i_k} = A_{j_1} \cup A_{j_2} \cup \cdots \cup A_{j_m}.$$
> (posed by Pan Chengbiao)
