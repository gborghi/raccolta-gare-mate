*Elements of two-element subsets each appearing exactly twice*

> $P_1, P_2, \ldots, P_n$ are $n$ distinct subsets of $\{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ each having two elements. $P_i$ and $P_j$ ($i \neq j$) have an element in common if and only if $\{i, j\}$ is one of the subsets $P_k$. Prove that each of $1, 2, \ldots, n$ belongs to exactly two of the $P_k$.
