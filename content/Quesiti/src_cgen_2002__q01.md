*Premiere partie: characterizations of a pseudo-rectangle triangle and complex-plane loci.*

> Let $ABC$ be a triangle. Denote by $P$ the orthogonal projection of the point $A$ onto the line $(BC)$, and by $D$ the reflection of the point $C$ across the line $(AP)$.
> 
> This triangle is said to be *pseudo-rectangle* at $A$ if $|\widehat{B} - \widehat{C}| = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$. We specify that it is *pseudo-rectangle at $A$, obtuse at $B$* in the case where $\widehat{B} - \widehat{C} = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$.
> 
> **1.** Show that the triangle $ABC$ is pseudo-rectangle at $A$ if and only if the triangle $ABD$ is right-angled at $A$.
> 
> **2.** Show that $PA^2 = PB \cdot PC$ if and only if the triangle $ABC$ is right-angled at $A$ or pseudo-rectangle at $A$.
> 
> **3.** Show that the triangle $ABC$ is pseudo-rectangle at $A$ if and only if its orthocentre is the reflection of the point $A$ across the line $(BC)$.
> 
> **4.** Let $R$ be the radius of the circumscribed circle of the triangle $ABC$. Show that $PB + PC = 2R$ if and only if $ABC$ is right-angled at $A$ or pseudo-rectangle at $A$.
> 
> **5.** Show that the triangle $ABC$ is pseudo-rectangle at $A$ if and only if the line $(AP)$ is tangent to the circle circumscribed to the triangle $ABC$.
> 
> **6.** In the complex plane associated with the direct orthonormal frame $(O;\vec{\imath},\vec{\jmath})$, let $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$ be the affixes of the non-collinear points $A$, $B$, $C$.
>   a. Give a necessary and sufficient condition on $\dfrac{(\alpha-\beta)(\alpha-\gamma)}{(\beta-\gamma)^2}$ for the triangle $ABC$ to be pseudo-rectangle at $A$.
>   b. Suppose $\beta = -\gamma = e^{i\frac{\pi}{4}}$. Determine the set $(E_1)$ of points $A$ of the plane such that the triangle $ABC$ is pseudo-rectangle at $A$.
>   c. Suppose $\beta = -\gamma = 1$. Determine the set $(E_2)$ of points $A$ of the plane such that the triangle $ABC$ is pseudo-rectangle at $A$.
>   d. By which simple geometric transformation does one pass from $(E_2)$ to $(E_1)$?
