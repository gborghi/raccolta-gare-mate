*Prove orthocentre equidistant from circumcentre and foot of altitude in isosceles triangle*

> Let $ABC$ be an acute-angled triangle with $AB = AC$ and $\angle BAC = \alpha$. Denote the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ by $D$. Let $O$ be the circumcentre and let $H$ be the foot of the altitude from $A$ to $BC$. Let $P$ be the orthocentre of $\triangle ABC$ (i.e., the intersection of the perpendiculars from the vertices $A$, $B$, $C$ to the opposite sides). Prove that $PO = PH$.
> 
> (Note: The circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ is the circle which passes through the vertices $A$, $B$ and $C$. The orthocentre is the point of intersection of the perpendiculars from the vertices to the opposite sides.)
