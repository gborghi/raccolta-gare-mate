*Find minimum-degree integer-polynomial f(x) with (f(x))^3 - 2 = g(x)h(x), g,h degree ≥ 1*

> A polynomial $f(x)$ with integer coefficients has degree at least $1$. There also exist integer-coefficient polynomials $g(x)$ and $h(x)$, each of degree at least $1$, such that
> $$\bigl(f(x)\bigr)^3 - 2 = g(x)\,h(x).$$
> Among all such polynomials $f(x)$, find the one of smallest degree.
