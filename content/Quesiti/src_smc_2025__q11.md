*Right-angled triangle PQR with angle PQR = 90°, PQ = 20, PR = 25; M on PR; N on QR; find MN where triangle PQM area is half PQR.*

> The diagram shows a triangle $PQR$ with $\angle PQR = 90^\circ$, $PQ = 20$ and $PR = 25$. Point $M$ lies on $PR$ such that $QM$ is a right-angled triangle whose area is half that of triangle $PQR$. What is the length of $MN$?
>
> - **(A)** $4\sqrt{2}$
> - **(B)** $\frac{15}{\sqrt{2}}$
> - **(C)** $8\sqrt{2}$
> - **(D)** $\frac{17}{\sqrt{2}}$
> - **(E)** $10\sqrt{2}$
