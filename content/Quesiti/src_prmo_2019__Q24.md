*Largest prime factor of coloring count for rectangle grid*

> A $1 \times n$ rectangle ($n \ge 1$) is divided into $n$ unit ($1 \times 1$) squares. Each square of this rectangle is coloured red, blue or green. Let $f(n)$ be the number of colourings of the rectangle in which there are an even number of red squares. What is the largest prime factor of $f(9)/f(3)$? (The number of red squares can be zero.)
