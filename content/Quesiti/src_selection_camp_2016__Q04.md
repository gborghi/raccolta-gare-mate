# Japan IMO Selection Camp 2016 — Selection Camp — Quesito 4

*Product of fractions minus 1 divisible by an odd prime*

> Let $m, n$ be positive integers satisfying $m > n$. For $k = 1, 2, \ldots, n+1$, define $x_k = \dfrac{m+k}{n+k}$. Suppose that $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}$ are all integers. Prove that $x_1 x_2 \cdots x_{n+1} - 1$ is divisible by some odd prime.
