*Third part: coordinates of the orthocentre of a triangle with given coordinates; orthocentric set obtained from a line plus an external point; and properties of an orthocentric set contained in the union of the two coordinate axes.*

> Troisieme partie.
> 
> 1. Let $a$, $b$, $c$ be three real numbers such that $a(b-c)\neq 0$, and $A$, $B$, $C$ the points of respective coordinates $(0,a)$, $(b,0)$, $(c,0)$. Compute the coordinates of the orthocentre $D$ of triangle $ABC$.
> 
> 2. Let $X$ be the set obtained by taking the union of a line $\Delta$ and a point $M$ not belonging to $\Delta$. Determine $\mathcal{H}(X)$. Show that $\mathcal{H}(X)\cup X$ is an orthocentric set.
> 
> 3. Let $X$ be an orthocentric set contained in the union of the axes $(O,\vec{u})$ and $(O,\vec{v})$ and containing at least three points of $(O,\vec{u})$ distinct from $O$.
>    a. Show that $X$ contains at least three points of $(O,\vec{u})$ with nonzero abscissae of the same sign.
>    b. Show that $X$ contains at least three points of $(O,\vec{u})$ with strictly positive abscissae.
> 
> 4.
>    a. Determine the finite orthocentric sets, containing at most five points and contained in the union of the axes $(O,\vec{u})$ and $(O,\vec{v})$.
>    b. Let $X$ be an orthocentric set contained in the union of the axes $(O,\vec{u})$ and $(O,\vec{v})$ and containing at least six points. Show that there exist two sequences $(x_n)$ and $(x'_n)$ of nonzero reals such that, for every integer $n$, the points of coordinates $(x_n,0)$ and $(x'_n,0)$ belong to $X$, and such that:
> $$\lim_{n\to+\infty} x_n=+\infty,\qquad \lim_{n\to+\infty} x'_n=0.$$
> Can an orthocentric set contained in the union of the axes $(O,\vec{u})$ and $(O,\vec{v})$ and containing at least six points be finite?
