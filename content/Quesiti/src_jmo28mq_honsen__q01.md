*gcd operation on blackboard, final number not a square*

> On a blackboard, the integers from $1$ to $100$ are written, each exactly once. We repeatedly perform the following operation: choose two integers $a, b$ written on the blackboard, erase them, and write the greatest common divisor of $a^2 b^2 + 3$ and $a^2 + b^2 + 2$. Show that when only one integer remains on the blackboard, that integer is not a perfect square.
