# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2001 — Nível 2 — Fase 1 — Quesito 3

*Count composite numbers in the set {1, 101, 1001, ..., 1000000000001}*

> In the set $\{1,\ 101,\ 1001,\ \ldots,\ 1\,000\,000\,000\,001\}$ each element is a number formed by the digit $1$ at the extremities and digits $0$ between them. Some of these elements are prime numbers and others are composite. About the quantity of composite numbers we can state that:
> 
> (A) it is equal to $1$ \quad (B) it is equal to $4$ \quad (C) it is less than $3$ \quad (D) it is greater than $4$ and less than $11$ \quad (E) it is $3$
