*If n is sum of squares of two positives in consecutive triple, so is n^2*

> Let $S = \{n \mid n-1,\, n,\, n+1 \text{ all can be expressed as the sum of the squares of two positive integers}\}$. Prove that, if $n \in S$, then $n^2 \in S$.
