*Stones in a row of cups with two moves; prove a terminal configuration is reached and is unique*

> We have a long row of cups and $n$ stones in the central cup (cup $0$). The following moves are permitted:
> 
> Move of type $A$: If there is at least one stone in cup $i$ and at least one stone in cup $i+1$, we may make a stone that is in cup $i+1$ jump to cup $i-1$, eliminating a stone from cup $i$.
> 
> Move of type $B$: If there are at least two stones in cup $i$, we may make one of them jump to cup $i+2$ and another to cup $i-1$.
> 
> Prove the following fact: performing the moves of type $A$ or $B$ for a sufficiently long time, we always reach a configuration in which it is no longer possible to make any of these moves. Moreover, prove that this final configuration does not depend on the choice of moves made during the process.
