*Find PQ where Q is the second intersection of circumcircles of triangles PAC and PBD*

> Five points $A, B, C, D, P$ lie in a plane, with $A, B, C, D$ collinear in that order. We have $AB = BC = CD = 6$, $PB = 5$, $PC = 7$. Let $Q$ be the intersection of the circumcircle of $\triangle PAC$ and the circumcircle of $\triangle PBD$ other than $P$. Find the length $PQ$.
> 
> (Here $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$.)
