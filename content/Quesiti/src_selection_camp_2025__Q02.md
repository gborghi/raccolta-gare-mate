# Japan IMO Selection Camp 2025 — Selection Camp — Quesito 2

*Triangle, incircle tangency construction; prove two lines parallel*

> Let $ABC$ be a triangle with incircle $\omega$. Points $D$ and $E$ lie on sides $AB$ and $AC$ respectively (not at the endpoints), and $\omega$ is also tangent to line $DE$, touching it at a point $P$. Let $M$ be the midpoint of arc $BC$ of the circumcircle, and let $Q$ be the second intersection of line $MP$ with $\omega$. Let $B'$ be the second intersection of line $DM$ with $\omega$ and $C'$ the second intersection of line $EM$ with $\omega$, with $B' \ne C'$, and suppose $A$ and $Q$ lie on the same side of line $BC$. Let $O$ be the incenter of triangle $DEM$, and let $Q'$ be the point on $\omega$ with $B'Q' : C'Q' = BQ : CQ$. Prove that line $MO$ is parallel to line $QQ'$. (Here $XY$ denotes the length of segment $XY$.)
