*Two quadratics equal at two integers, prove difference of leading linear coefficients is even*

> Let $P_1(x) = x^2 + a_1 x + b_1$ and $P_2(x) = x^2 + a_2 x + b_2$ be two quadratic polynomials with integer coefficients. Suppose $a_1 \neq a_2$ and there exist integers $m \neq n$ such that $P_1(m) = P_2(m)$ and $P_1(n) = P_2(n)$. Prove that $a_1 - a_2$ is even.
