*Ten equal discs lie between two concentric circles; each disc touches its two neighbours and both circles; inner circle radius 1. Find the radius of the outer circle.*

> The diagram shows ten equal discs that lie between two concentric circles — an inner circle and an outer circle. Each disc touches two neighbouring discs and both circles. The inner circle has radius 1. What is the radius of the outer circle?
>
> - **(A)** $2\tan 36^\circ$
> - **(B)** $\dfrac{\sin 36^\circ}{1 - \sin 36^\circ}$
> - **(C)** $\dfrac{1 + \sin 18^\circ}{1 - \sin 18^\circ}$
> - **(D)** $\dfrac{1}{\cos 18^\circ}$
> - **(E)** $\dfrac{1}{5}$
