# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2018 — Yosen (Preliminary) — Quesito 10

*Practice-match outcomes giving exactly 2 possible champions*

> $2^3 = 8$ players take part in a single-elimination chess tournament, in which the champion is decided as follows. First, all players are lined up in a row. Then the following operation is repeated $3$ times: the players in the row are grouped into pairs, two at a time from one end, and the two players in each pair play a match; the winner stays in the row and the loser drops out. (There are no draws.) The player remaining in the row at the end is the champion. Before the tournament a full round-robin of practice matches was held, in which no draws occurred; that is, for every pair of players a practice match was played and a winner determined. Now assume the results of the tournament matches agree with the practice-match results. Depending on the initial lineup of the players, the number of players who could possibly become champion turned out to be exactly $2$. How many possible combinations of the practice-match results are there (for which exactly $2$ players can possibly become champion)?
