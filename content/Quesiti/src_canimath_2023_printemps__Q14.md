*For which positive integers n can Aline write 2n real numbers, not all equal, such that for every choice of n of them the sum of the chosen n equals the product of the other n?*

> Let $n$ be a strictly positive integer. Aline wants to write $2n$ real numbers on the board in such a way that:
> 
> $\triangleright$ the $2n$ numbers written are not all equal;
> 
> $\triangleright$ if Aline circles $n$ of the numbers written on the board, then, whatever Aline's choice is, the sum of the $n$ circled numbers is equal to the product of the $n$ numbers that are not circled.
> 
> For which values of $n$ can Aline realize her wish?
