*Infinitely many primes whose island-bridge graph is disconnected*

> For each prime number $p$, there is a kingdom $P$ consisting of islands $1, 2, \ldots, p$. For two distinct islands $n$ and $m$ of kingdom $P$, a bridge connects them if and only if $(n^2 - m + 1)(m^2 - n + 1)$ is divisible by $p$. Prove that there exist infinitely many primes $p$ for which there is a pair of two distinct islands of kingdom $P$ that cannot be reached from one another by travelling across the bridges.
