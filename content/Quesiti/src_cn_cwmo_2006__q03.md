*Geometry: angle bisector and tan value in circumcircle configuration*

> In $\triangle ABC$, $\angle ABC = 60^\circ$. The tangent at point $P$ to the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ meets line $CB$ extended at $A$. Let $\angle PCB = \alpha$. Points $D$ and $E$ lie on the line segment $PA$ and the circumcircle respectively, such that $\angle DBE = 90^\circ$ and $PD = PE$. Let $F$ be the intersection of $BE$ and $PC$. It is given that lines $AF$, $BP$ and $CD$ are concurrent.
> (1) Prove that $BF$ is the bisector of $\angle PBC$.
> (2) Find the value of $\tan\alpha$.
