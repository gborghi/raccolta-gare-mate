# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2009 — Honsen (Final) — Quesito 2

*Blackboard integers with even sum: prove circle-cross labeling equates partial sums*

> Let $N$ be a positive integer. On a blackboard, finitely many integers are written satisfying the following three conditions:
> \begin{itemize}
> \item Every number written is at least $1$ and at most $N$.
> \item For every integer $m$ with $1 \le m \le N$, at least one copy of $m$ is written.
> \item The sum of all the numbers written is even.
> \end{itemize}
> Prove that one can label each written number with either $\bigcirc$ or $\times$ so that the sum of the $\bigcirc$-labeled numbers equals the sum of the $\times$-labeled numbers.
