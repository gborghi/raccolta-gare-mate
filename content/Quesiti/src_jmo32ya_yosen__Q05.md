# Japan Mathematical Olympiad 2022 — Yosen (Preliminary) — Quesito 5

*Count pairs (m,n) satisfying an unbounded common-divisor condition*

> Find the number of pairs $(m, n)$ of positive integers with $1 \le m, n \le 2022$ such that: for every positive integer $N$, there exist a non-negative integer $k$ and an integer $d > N$ such that $d$ divides both $m - k^2$ and $n + 2k$.
