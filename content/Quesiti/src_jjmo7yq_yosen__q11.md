*Count selections of balls labeled 1–9 satisfying a box-difference condition*

> There are $9$ balls, labeled with the integers $1$ through $9$ (one integer per ball). Choose some of these balls. The rule is: place the chosen balls in a red box and the remaining balls in a blue box, such that for any two balls in the same box, the absolute difference of their labels is at least $3$. Find the number of ways to choose balls satisfying this condition. (Choosing no balls counts as $1$ way.)
