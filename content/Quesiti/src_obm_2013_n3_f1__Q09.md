*Number of ways to choose three pairwise-skew edges of a cube*

> We say that two lines or line segments are \emph{skew} (reversas) when there is no plane that contains both lines or segments. In how many ways can we choose three edges of a cube so that any two of these edges are skew?
> 
> (A) $6$ \quad (B) $8$ \quad (C) $12$ \quad (D) $24$ \quad (E) $36$
