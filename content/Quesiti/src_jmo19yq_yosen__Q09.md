*Pair interpreters into rooms sharing a common language*

> Ten interpreters are invited to an international mathematics conference. Each interpreter can speak exactly two of the following five languages: Greek, Slovenian, Vietnamese, Spanish, German. Moreover, no two interpreters have the same combination of languages. These interpreters are to be lodged two per room in five rooms, in such a way that the two interpreters in each room can speak a common language. In how many ways can this be done? Here, two room assignments that give exactly the same groupings of people are counted as the same (merely swapping which room a pair occupies does not create a different assignment).
