*Triangle with 2R ≤ r_a; prove inequalities on sides and exradii*

> Let $R$ denote the circumradius of a triangle $ABC$; $a$, $b$, $c$ its sides $BC$, $CA$, $AB$; and $r_a$, $r_b$, $r_c$ its exradii opposite $A$, $B$, $C$, respectively. If $2R \le r_a$, prove that
> (i) $a > b$ and $a > c$;
> (ii) $2R > r_b$ and $2R > r_c$.
