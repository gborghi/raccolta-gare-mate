*Three lines in space; PQ perpendicular to n has constant length*

> $l$, $m$, $n$ are three lines in space. Neither $l$ nor $m$ is perpendicular to $n$. Points $P$ and $Q$ vary on $l$ and $m$ respectively in such a way that $PQ$ is perpendicular to $n$, and the plane through $PQ$ perpendicular to $n$ meets $n$ at a point. Prove that $PQ$ is of constant length.
