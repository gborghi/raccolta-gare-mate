# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2004 — Nível 1 — Fase 3 — Quesito 3

*Prove a specific large number is composite*

> We say that a natural number is \emph{composite} if it can be written as the product of two natural numbers both greater than $1$. For example, $91$ is composite because we can write $91 = 7 \times 13$.
> 
> Prove that the number
> $$\frac{1}{2}\!\left(2^{2004}+2\right)+1$$
> is composite.
