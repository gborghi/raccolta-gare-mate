*Comparing averages after moving last-place student*

> The $61$ students approved in a competition, whose marks were all different, were distributed in two groups: the $31$ best-scoring students in group $A$ and the $30$ following in group $B$. The averages of both groups were calculated. Then it was decided to move the last-ranked student in group $A$ to group $B$. With this:
> 
> (A) The average of group $A$ improved, but that of group $B$ worsened. \quad (B) The average of group $A$ worsened, but that of group $B$ improved. \quad (C) The averages of both groups improved. \quad (D) The averages of both groups worsened. \quad (E) The averages of both groups can improve or worsen, depending on the marks of the candidates.
