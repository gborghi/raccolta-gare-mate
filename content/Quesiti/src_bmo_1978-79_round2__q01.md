*Prove inequality for four distinct positive reals with interleaving condition*

> $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$ are different positive real numbers. Prove that if at least one of the numbers $a$ and $b$ lies between the numbers $c$ and $d$, or at least one of the numbers $c$ and $d$ lies between the numbers $a$ and $b$, then
> $$(*) \quad \sqrt{(a+b)(c+d)} > \sqrt{ac} + \sqrt{bd}.$$
> Otherwise show that the four numbers can be chosen so that $(*)$ is false.
