*No continuous function asymptotic to prime-counting function*

> Let $\pi(x)$ denote the number of positive primes less than or equal to $x$, for $x \in \mathbb{R}$. Show that there is no continuous function $f : [0, +\infty) \to \mathbb{R}$ satisfying $\lim_{t \to \infty}(f(x) - \pi(x)) = 0$.

*Nessuna funzione continua asimptotica alla funzione di conteggio primario*
