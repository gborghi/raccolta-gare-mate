*Sequence satisfying sum condition; bounds and limit of derived sequence*

> The real numbers $a_0, a_1, a_2, \ldots$ satisfy the condition:
> $$1 = a_0 \le a_1 \le a_2 \le \cdots.$$
> The numbers $b_1, b_2, b_3, \ldots$ are defined by
> $$b_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(1 - \frac{a_{k-1}}{a_k}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{a_k}}.$$
> (a) Prove that $0 \le b_n < 2$ for all $n$.
> 
> (b) Given $c$ with $0 \le c < 2$, prove that there exist numbers $a_0, a_1, a_2, \ldots$ with the above properties such that $b_n > c$ for large enough $n$.
