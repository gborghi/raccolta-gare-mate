*Find maximum m for quadratic satisfying symmetry, growth, and shift conditions*

> Suppose a quadratic function $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$, with $a \ne 0$ satisfies the following conditions:
> 
> (1) When $x \in \mathbf{R}$, $f(x - 1) = f(2 - x)$ and $f(x) \ge x$.
> 
> (2) When $x \in (0, 2)$, $f(x) \le \left(\dfrac{x + 1}{2}\right)^2$.
> 
> Find the maximum value of $m$ such that there exists $t \in \mathbf{R}$ for which $f(x + t) \le x$ holds for all $x \in [1, m]$.
