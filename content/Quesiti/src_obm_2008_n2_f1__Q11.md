*Length MN from feet of perpendiculars, orthic triangle perimeter 10*

> In triangle $ABC$ the altitude $AH$ is drawn. Let $M$ and $N$ be points on sides $AB$ and $AC$, respectively, such that $HM$ is perpendicular to $AB$ and $HN$ is perpendicular to $AC$. Find $MN$, knowing that the perimeter of the orthic triangle of triangle $ABC$ is equal to $10$.
> 
> \textit{Observation:} The orthic triangle of a triangle is the triangle whose vertices are the intersections of the altitudes of the triangle with their respective sides. One can show that the altitudes of any triangle are always the bisectors of its orthic triangle (the orthocenter being the meeting point of the altitudes of the original triangle).
> (A) $5$ \quad (B) $6$ \quad (C) $7$ \quad (D) $8$ \quad (E) $9$
