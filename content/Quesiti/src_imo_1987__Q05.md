# IMO 1987 (28th IMO, Havana) — Quesito 5

*n points with irrational distances and rational triangle areas*

> Let n be an integer greater than or equal to 3. Prove that there is a set of n
> points in the plane such that the distance between any two points is irrational
> and each set of three points determines a non-degenerate triangle with rational
> area.
