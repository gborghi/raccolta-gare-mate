*A coin tossed n times; partial sums with toss values 1 or 2; express the probability that the partial sums hit n recursively.*

> A coin is tossed $n$ times, and the outcome is written in the form $(a_1,a_2,\ldots,a_n)$, where $a_i=1$ or $2$ depending on whether the result of the $i$-th toss is the head or the tail, respectively. Set $b_j=a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_j$ for $j=1,2,\ldots,n$, and let $p(n)$ be the probability that the sequence $b_1,b_2,\ldots,b_n$ contains the number $n$. Express $p(n)$ in terms of $p(n-1)$ and $p(n-2)$.
