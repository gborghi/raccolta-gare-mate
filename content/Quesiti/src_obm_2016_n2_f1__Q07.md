# Olimpíada Brasileira de Matemática 2016 — Nível 2 — Fase 1 — Quesito 7

*Which value cannot be the variable side of triangles ABC and ABD*

> By the triangle inequality applied to triangles $ABC$ and $ABD$, respectively, $x<4+3=7$ and $8+x>10$, that is $2<x<7$. In principle the possible values of $x$ are $3$, $4$, $5$ or $6$. By the converse of the triangle inequality, three positive numbers are side lengths of a triangle only if the largest is less than the sum of the other two; the four found values satisfy the conditions. Which value of $x$ is not admissible? (Note: original multiple-choice options are not reproduced in the source gabarito.)
